'''Bunnelby''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''ホルビー''' ''Horubee'') is a {{type|Normal}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation VI]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] into {{p|Diggersby}} starting at level 20.

==Biology==
Bunnelby is a small, gray, rabbit-like {{OBP|Pokémon|species}}. The tips of its large ears are dark brown and have three finger-like sections each. It has black eyes, a small pink nose, prominent round cheeks, two tiny whiskers on each cheek, and large upper incisors. Its neck is surrounded by a ruff of stiff, brown fur. There are small brown spots on its hind legs and it has brown toes. It also has a fluffy gray tail. 

Bunnelby's hand-like ears are strong enough to shovel dirt and chop tree roots. It can dig a nest up to 33 feet in just one night. Bunnelby is very sensitive when it comes to danger. {{p|Corviknight}} is a natural predator of Bunnelby. Should it hear Corviknight's flapping, it will quickly dig a hole to hide underground.

===Evolution===
Bunnelby evolves into {{p|Diggersby}}.

===Major appearances===
====[[Clemont's Bunnelby]]====
Bunnelby debuted in ''[[XY001|Kalos, Where Dreams and Adventures Begin!]]'', under the ownership of {{an|Clemont}}. In ''[[XY088|A Trip Down Memory Train!]]'', he was revealed to have {{pkmn2|caught}} him after saving him from a {{pkmn2|wild}} {{p|Diggersby}}. In ''[[JN103|The Future is Now, Thanks to Strategy!]]'', he was revealed to have evolved into a Diggersby.

====Other====
In ''[[JN042|Sword and Shield, Slumbering Weald!]]'', [[Goh]] caught a {{DL|List of Goh's Pokémon|Bunnelby}} that had stolen one of his scones in [[Galar]]. It has since then made further appearances in {{aniseries|JN}}.

===Origin===
Bunnelby seems to be based on a {{wp|rabbit}}. Its ability to use its ears as digging tools to excavate through earth and roots may be a reference to {{wp|trowel}}s or {{wp|shovel}}s.

====Name origin====
Bunnelby may be a combination of ''bunny'' and ''tunnel''. It may also involve ''{{wp|wallaby}}.

Horubee may be a combination of 掘る ''horu'' (to dig) or ''hole'', ''rabbit'', and possibly ''wallaby''.
