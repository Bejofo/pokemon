'''Morelull''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''ネマシュ''' ''Nemasyu'') is a dual-type {{2t|Grass|Fairy}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation VII]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] into {{p|Shiinotic}} starting at [[level]] 24.

==Biology==
Morelull is a small, white {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} similar to a mushroom. It is one of the [[List of Pokémon by height|shortest]] {{type|Grass}} Pokémon. It has large, black eyes with pale green half-circle markings on the outer sides. Small bumps ring the top of its head, similar to hair. Its head is much larger than its thin, stem-like body. At the base, the stem splits into three green roots that act as feet. Three large mushrooms sprout from the center of its head. Each mushroom has a thin white stem and a darker ring of color around the base of the cap. The center and largest mushroom is light pink, while the other two mushrooms are smaller and a light purplish-blue. At night, Morelull can make the spores filling its mushroom caps glow.

Morelull likes dark, damp areas such as dense jungles, but changes locations every night to avoid depriving the plants in any one area of too many nutrients. In addition to locomotion, its roots are also used to communicate with other Morelull.<ref>[http://www.pokemon-sunmoon.com/en-us/pokemon/morelull/ Pokémon Sun and Moon site | Morelull]</ref> When in danger, it scatters spores that burst into bright, glittering sparks. The pulsing light of the spores can induce drowsiness. It is rumored that once when a group of Morelull were disturbed and all threw out their spores at once, a plane passing overhead suffered a near brush with disaster.<ref>[http://www.pokemon.co.jp/ex/sun_moon/pokemon/160812_01.html Japanese Pokémon Sun and Moon site]</ref>. The caps on Morelull's head are a delicacy for Pokémon living in the forests and can grow back overnight.

In [[Generation VII]], {{m|Strength Sap}} was its [[signature move]], but other Pokémon could still inherit it via {{pkmn|breeding}}.

==Trivia==
* Morelull is the only Pokémon that can have {{a|Rain Dish}} but cannot learn {{m|Rain Dance}}.
* Morelull can be seen as a counterpart to {{p|Paras}}. Both share the {{t|Grass}} type, the same base stat total of 285, evolve at Level 24, both are mushroom-based Pokémon and thus can learn {{m|Spore}}, and both can be found in the wild in [[Pokémon Sun and Moon]], taking the place of the other depending on the [[time]], with Morelull being found at night and Paras during the day.
* Morelull is tied with {{p|Cleffa}} and {{p|Igglybuff}} for having the lowest base {{stat|Speed}} stat of all {{type|Fairy}} Pokémon.
* In {{g|Sun and Moon}}, while Morelull can learn {{m|Sunny Day}} via [[TM]], its Evolution, {{p|Shiinotic}}, cannot learn it that way.
* Morelull is the first {{t|Grass}}/{{t|Fairy}} type to debut with this type combination, as the {{p|Cottonee}} family were pure Grass in their debut.

===Origin===
Morelull may be based on bioluminescent mushrooms such as those of the genus ''{{wp|Mycena}}'', although its name suggests inspiration from the genus ''{{wp|Morchella}}''. Its {{t|Fairy}} type may be due to {{wp|fairy ring}}s, or it may possibly be a reference to {{wp|foxfire|fairy fire}}.

Given that some of the foreign names of {{p|Shiinotic|its Evolution}} include the word "lamp", and that Morelull shine lights and put people to sleep, it may also be based on a {{wp|nightlight}}. The connection between fungi and lights may be a pun on the homophonous Japanese words 発酵 ''hakkō'' (fermentation) and 発光 ''hakkō'' (luminescence) — Morelull and Shiinotic's Japanese {{pkmn|category}} is はっこうポケモン ''Hakkō Pokémon''.

====Name origin====
Morelull may be a combination of ''{{wp|Morchella|morel}}'' and ''lull''.

Nemasyu may be a combination of 寝 ''ne'' (sleep) and ''mushroom''. It could also come from 寝ます ''nemasu'' (I'm going to sleep), with the ''su'' ending particle being corrupted into ''shu'' to indicate drowsiness.
