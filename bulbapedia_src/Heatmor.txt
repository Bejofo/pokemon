'''Heatmor''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''クイタラン''' ''Kuitaran'') is a {{type|Fire}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation V]].

It is not known to [[Evolution|evolve]] into or from any other Pokémon.

==Biology==
Heatmor is a bipedal, {{wp|anteater}}-like {{OBP|Pokémon|species}}. It has a beige face while the rest of its fur is red with yellow stripes, sharing the appearance of molten lava. On its lower abdomen, back, and underside are raised, beige-colored bands that resemble pipes, leading to its tail. Heatmor's claws, on both its feet and hands, are a dull yellow and on its wrists are raised, dented bands; both the wrists and feet are beige-colored. Its tail is dented upward and has a hole in it, similar in appearance to a {{wp|muffler}}. It also seems to serve the same purpose as a muffler, being able to expel gases, although it is able to take in air as well.

{{p|Durant}} are the preferred prey of Heatmor. Heatmor expels flames from its mouth and uses it like a tongue, allowing it to burn through Durant's steel bodies before consuming their insides. It uses the hole in its tail to absorb air in order to keep its fire burning. Should the hole get blocked, Heatmor will become ill. In [[Generation VII]], {{m|Fire Lash}} was its [[signature move]].

==In animation==
[[File:Pokémon Grand Eating Contest Heatmor.png|thumb|250px|Heatmor in {{aniseries|JN}}]]
===Major appearances===
Heatmor made its animated series debut in ''[[BW077|Evolution by Fire!]]'', under the ownership of [[Shamus]]. It appeared alongside Shamus's {{p|Emboar}} in a [[Double Battle]] against [[Kylan]]'s {{p|Mienshao}} and {{p|Watchog}}. Shamus used it again, along with Emboar, in a Double Battle against {{Ash}}. They were able to defeat {{AP|Snivy}}, but lost to {{AP|Tepig}} when it evolved into {{p|Pignite}}.

In ''[[BW090|Jostling for the Junior Cup!]]'', [[Manning]]'s Heatmor competed in the [[Pokémon World Tournament Junior Cup]]. It was able to defeat [[Kenton]]'s {{p|Golett}} and advance to the next round, but it quickly lost to [[Trip]] and his {{TP|Trip|Serperior}}.

===Minor appearances===
Heatmor debuted in the opening sequence of ''[[M14|White—Victini and Zekrom]]''.

In ''[[M15|Kyurem VS. The Sword of Justice]]'', the {{OBP|Swords of Justice|M15}} legend featured a Heatmor.

In ''[[BW127|The Island of Illusions!]]'', a {{pkmn2|giant}} Heatmor was one of {{p|Zoroark}}'s {{a|illusion}}s.

In ''[[M16|Genesect and the Legend Awakened]]'', {{OBP|Mewtwo|M16}}'s flashback featured a Heatmor.

In ''[[SM034|A Crowning Moment of Truth!]]'', two {{pkmn|Trainer}}s' Heatmor attended the [[Wela Fire Festival]]. When an {{rf|Alolan}} {{TP|Kiawe|Marowak}} stole the crown, one of them tried to stop it.

In ''[[JN047|Crowning the Chow Crusher!]]'', a Trainer's Heatmor competed in the [[Pokémon Grand Eating Contest]].

Three Heatmor appeared in ''[[JN072|Everybody's Doing the Underground Shuffle!]]''.

A Heatmor appeared in ''[[HZ085|The Pokémon Paradise of Laqua!]]'', among the Pokémon living in [[Laqua]].

Trish from {{tc|The Riches}} owns a Heatmor, which she used during her trek through [[Mistralton Cave]] in ''[[PS506|A Week to Go and Old Wounds]]''.

A Trainer's Heatmor appeared in ''[[PS547|The Final Battle]]''.

A Heatmor appeared in ''[[PS585|Pinsir Changes]]''.

A Heatmor appeared in a flashback in ''[[PS589|Charizard Transforms]]''.

===Pokémon Pocket Monsters===

==Trivia==
* Heatmor being the natural predator of {{p|Durant}} is reflected in their [[type]]s.
* Heatmor and Durant were both designed by [[Ken Sugimori]].<ref name=ND205>''Nintendo Dream'' Vol. 205, May 2011 ([https://web.archive.org/web/20110402003550/http://shellspider.blog101.fc2.com/blog-entry-562.html Shellspider's blog (Japanese)], [http://lavacutcontent.com/ken-sugimori-nintendo-dream/ Translation by Dr. Lava])</ref>
* Heatmor and Durant have a lot of similarities. They share the same leveling rate, catch rate, hatch time, base experience yield, base stat total, and gender ratio.

===Origin===
Heatmor is based on an {{wp|anteater}} with elements of {{wp|ironworks}}.<ref name=ND205 /> Its wrists resemble {{wp|air filter}} intake inlets and its tail is similar to an engine's {{wp|muffler}}. Its {{type|Fire}} and tongue made out of flames might have been chosen to be effective on its rival ant Pokémon's type, {{p|Durant}}, which is {{t|Bug}} and {{type|Steel}} — both types are weak to Fire.

====Name origin====
Heatmor may be a combination of ''heat'' and ''armor'' or ''{{wp|Mortar (weapon)|mortar}}'', as well as a play on ''eat more'' or ''heat more''.

Kuitaran may be a combination of 蟻食 ''arikui'' (anteater) and ''anteater'' or ''{{wp|Tatara (furnace)|tatara}}'' (a kind of furnace), and possibly a play on 食い足らん ''kuitaran'' (has not eaten enough).
