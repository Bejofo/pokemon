'''Indeedee''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''イエッサン''' ''Yessan'') is a dual-type {{2t|Psychic|Normal}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation VIII]].

It is not known to [[Evolution|evolve]] into or from any other Pokémon.

==Biology==
Indeedee is a dark purple and white bipedal {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} that differs in appearance depending on their gender. Both forms have white faces, rounded heads and pear-shaped bodies, and white hands and feet with three digits on each of them. The males have upward-turned spiral horns, blue markings in-between their eyes, a generally more navy body, and more white around the legs. Females have downward-turned spiral horns, pink markings above their eyes, a white body, and darker legs. These markings all give them the appearance of butlers and maids. 

These Pokémon willingly serve people and other Pokémon, as they derive energy from feelings of gratitude. They sense emotions by touching their horns to determine other's needs and serve them better. Males are skilled at being valets while the females are supposed to be excellent babysitters. Indeedee is always bustling around, searching and serving those with happy feelings. The Indeedee never leaves its Trainer's side. It uses its psychic power to predict its Trainer's actions and take care of everyday needs. Indeedee dislikes conflict and will throw things at anyone who disturbs its territory using its psychic powers.

In [[Generation IX]], Indeedee is the [[Signature Ability|only known Pokémon]] that can have {{a|Psychic Surge}} as an [[Ability]].

===Forms===
Male and female Indeedee [[Gender#Indeedee|differ by moves]] they learn and their stat spreads. Male Indeedee can have {{a|Inner Focus}} as an ability, while female Indeedee can have {{a|Own Tempo}}.

====Major appearances====
Both male and female Indeedee debuted in ''[[JN027|Toughing It Out!]]'', under the ownership of a [[Galar]]ian [[Nurse Joy]]. They reappeared in ''[[JN028|Sobbing Sobble!]]''.

====Minor appearances====
In ''[[JN102|Battling in the Freezing Raid!]]'', a female Indeedee appeared under the ownership of [[Horace]]. She was used in a [[Raid Battle (GO)#In animation|Raid Battle]] against an {{p|Articuno}}, only to be defeated.

A {{pkmn|Trainer}}'s male Indeedee appeared in ''[[HZ054|The Treasure of Eternity]]''.

A female Indeedee appeared in ''[[HZ083|The Truth Revealed!]]''.

A [[Dynamax]]ed Indeedee appeared in ''[[PASS29|GRRR!! The Irrepairable Gears]]'' during the [[Darkest Day]].

Both male and female Indeedee appeared in [[PASS41]].

==Trivia==
* Indeedee shares its {{pkmn|category}} with {{p|Kirlia}} and {{p|Mesprit}}. They are all known as the Emotion Pokémon.
* Indeedee is the only Pokémon to have different Egg moves depending on gender.
* Indeedee is the only dual-type Psychic/Normal Pokémon to have Psychic as its primary type instead of its secondary type.
* Indeedee shares several similarities with {{p|Meowstic}}. Both are {{type|Psychic}} Pokémon with gender differences and have different movesets and Pokédex entries depending on their gender. Male Indeedee and Meowstic are easier to obtain in Sword, while female Indeedee and Meowstic are easier to obtain in Shield.
* Both forms of Indeedee were designed by [[Saya Tsuruta]].<ref>[https://twitter.com/saya_tsuruta/status/1206016520830107648 Tweet by つるたさや Saya Tsuruta (@saya_tsuruta).] Posted on December 15, 2019.</ref>
* In [[Generation IX]], prior to the Version 2.0.1 update of {{g|Scarlet and Violet}}, Female Indeedee was unable to learn {{m|Trick Room}} via [[TM]], with the only way of learning it being through a Tera Raid encounter.

===Origin===
Indeedee may be based on {{wp|Lares}}, the household deities in {{wp|Religion in ancient Rome|ancient Roman religion}}, said to protect the house in exchange for worship and gratitude. Lares are commonly depicted in pairs and holding {{wp|Rhyton|rhyta}}, horn-like containers that often depict {{wp|Satyrs}}, half-goat creatures from {{wp|Greek mythology}}. It may also draw inspiration from {{wp|Brownie (folklore)|brownies}}, the household spirits from {{wp|British folklore}} said to come out at night to perform domestic and farming tasks. Their gender-specific motifs may also be based on {{wp|Housekeeper (domestic worker)|housekeeper}}s, with its male form having traits of a butler and its female form having traits of a maid.

====Name origin====
Indeedee may be derived from ''indeed'' and the phrase, "Yes indeedy."

Yessan may be a combination of ''yessir'' and ～さん ''-san'' (honorific title). It may also be derived from ''"[[wp:Yes, and...|yes, and...]]"''.
