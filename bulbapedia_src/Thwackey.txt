'''Thwackey''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''バチンキー''' ''Bachinkey'') is a {{type|Grass}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation VIII]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Grookey}} starting at [[level]] 16 and evolves into {{p|Rillaboom}} starting at level 35.

==Biology==
[[File:811Thwackey Dream.png|thumb|left|200px|Thwackey's mohawk]]
Thwackey is a bipedal, humanoid monkey {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} with a light-green body, and lighter-green markings on its face and chest. Its ears, arms, and tail are dark brown, and its two large eyes are yellow in color. It has dark green bands around its wrists and the stick it holds splits in two after its Evolution,<ref name='SwShsite' /> which it uses to hold up a tuft of leaves resembling hair on its head. When it removes the sticks, the leaves resemble a mohawk, with a dark green color on the ends. It has several sharp teeth.

Thwackey can use its two sticks to create a rhythmic beat. The faster a rhythm Thwackey creates, the more respect it earns from its peers. Often, when it drums out rapid beats in battle, Thwackey will get so caught up in the rhythm that it does not realize its opponent has been knocked out. This action will also cause Thwackey to even ignore its own Trainer’s commands. To further develop its sense of rhythm, it constantly uses it in daily life. It can always feel a beat, and it walks while swaying back and forth to this beat, along with drumming with its sticks.<ref name='SwShsite'>[https://swordshield.pokemon.com/en-us/pokemon-galar-region/thwackey/ Thwackey | Official Website | Pokémon Sword and Pokémon Shield]</ref> As seen in the {{aniseries|JN}} episode ''[[JN101|A One-Stick Wonder!]]'', Thwackey can groom its friends to cheer them up; this is its way to show friendship and trust.

In [[Generation IX]], Thwackey and its evolutionary relatives are the [[Signature Ability|only known Pokémon]] that can have {{a|Grassy Surge}} as an [[Ability]].

==Trivia==

===Origin===
Thwackey appears to be based on {{wp|monkey}}s, specifically {{wp|macaque}}s, {{wp|capuchin monkey|capuchin}}s, and {{wp|pygmy marmoset}}s, as well as common depictions of {{wp|Caveman|cavemen}}. Its behavior may reference both the fact that macaques {{wp|Drum#Animal drumming|drum objects}} to show {{wp|Social dominance theory|social dominance}} and that {{wp|War dance|songs}} are used during {{wp|Endemic warfare|ritual battles}} of many {{wp|Indigenous peoples|indigenous cultures}} to embolden warriors and evoke ferocity. Its tools resemble drumsticks, {{wp|hair stick}}s, and {{wp|Club (weapon)|clubs}}. When Thwackey takes off the sticks from its head, its leafy hair forms shape of a {{wp|mohawk hairstyle}}.

====Name origin====
Thwackey may be a combination of ''thwack'' and ''monkey''.

Bachinkey may be a combination of バチン ''bachin'' (whack) and ''monkey''. It may also derive from ''capuchin monkey''.
