'''Primarina''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''アシレーヌ''' ''Ashirene'') is a dual-type {{2t|Water|Fairy}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation VII]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Brionne}} starting at [[level]] 34. It is the final form of {{p|Popplio}}.

==Biology==
[[File:Lana Primarina hair undone.png|thumb|left|250px|[[Lana's Primarina]]'s hair undone while using {{m|Sparkling Aria}}]]
Primarina is a marine {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} that resembles a cross between a sea lion and a mermaid. Most of its body is white, but it has a long, blue, fish-like tail. It has a long snout with a round, pink nose, bright blue eyes with long white eyelashes, and long light blue hair trailing off the back of its head. A {{wp|tiara}}-like structure rests on its forehead, consisting of a line of pearls with a pink sea star at each corner and a thin, transparent blue fin extending back over its hair. Its hair is contained to a {{wp|ponytail}} by two strings of pearls. This ponytail, as seen in the {{pkmn|games}} and {{pkmn|animated series}}, becomes temporarily undone when it performs certain moves. On its body are three fins similar to the one on its crown: one that drapes around its shoulders and chest, one around its waist where the fishtail begins, and one near the tip of its tail. It has long front fins with three digits. Primarina's long fishlike tail is bright blue with small pointed, pink fins around its hips. Its tail fins are white, and it is capable of using them as feet to stand up.

Primarina uses water balloons and its voice as its main weapons. Because its voice is instrumental in its battle strategy, its care should be a priority for Trainers. The melody it uses has been passed down for generations. Water balloons are released as it dances, and are controlled by its voice. In the moonlight, the balloons glitter and create a beautiful scene. However, some of the balloons are explosive and triggered by touch. This theatrical Pokémon considers every battle a stage and uses the non-explosive balloons as platforms to tease opponents.<ref>[http://www.pokemon-sunmoon.com/en-us/pokemon/primarina/ Pokémon Sun and Moon site | Primarina]</ref> 

Primarina is the [[signature move|only known Pokémon]] capable of learning the move {{m|Sparkling Aria}} by leveling up. Primarina is also the only known Pokémon capable of using the [[Z-Move]] {{m|Oceanic Operetta}}. Additionally, Primarina and its pre-evolved forms are the [[Signature Ability|only known Pokémon]] that can have {{a|Liquid Voice}} as an [[Ability]].

==Trivia==
* Primarina is the only {{type|Fairy}} {{DL|List of Pokémon by shape|Pokémon with fins}}.
* Primarina is currently the only Pokémon evolved from a [[first partner Pokémon]] to have the Fairy type.
* Primarina has the highest Special Attack and Special Defense [[stat]]s of all the final Evolutions of [[first partner Pokémon]], excluding [[Mega Evolution|Mega Evolutions]].
** Primarina's Special Attack is also the highest base stat of any non-Mega Evolved fully evolved first partner Pokémon.
* Official confidential art featuring {{p|Decidueye}}, {{p|Incineroar}}, Primarina, and [[Mallow]] was leaked on the Internet on June 4, 2016. This leak happened 145 days before these Pokémon were officially revealed on October 27, 2016.<ref name="leakedart">[http://66.media.tumblr.com/0250e03bd66ab9ca99dc62e78ecf5d7b/tumblr_o8adnyxaSF1qhkgqso1_540.jpg Official confidential art of Decidueye, Incineroar, and Primarina | Game Freak]</ref> From the leaked material it is known that Primarina's code name was "アシカ 3", or "Sea Lion 3" during development.
* Primarina is the first {{t|Water}}/{{t|Fairy}} type to debut with this type combination, as {{p|Marill}} and {{p|Azumarill}} were pure Water in their debut.

===Origin===
Primarina is based on a {{wp|Japanese sea lion|sea lion}}.<ref name=leakedart/>. It could also draw inspiration from the {{wp|Hawaiian monk seal}}, a species of seal endemic to the {{wp|Hawaiian Islands}}, as well as {{wp|mermaid}}s.

Primarina's propensity to perform may draw inspiration from sea lion performances at circuses, zoos and {{wp|marine mammal park}}s, where captive sea lions, most commonly {{wp|California sea lion}}s, are trained to perform various tricks in front of large crowds. Its ability to sing may be inspired by {{wp|Siren (mythology)|sirens}}, mythological oceanic creatures that lure sailors with enchanting songs. Its behavior and appearance may also be inspired by {{wp|ballet dancer|ballerinas}}, {{wp|opera singer}}s, and the {{wp|Pierrot}} character. It may also draw inspiration from {{wp|selkie}}s.

====Name origin====
Primarina may be a combination of ''{{wp|prima donna}}'', ''marina'', and ''ballerina''.

Ashirene may be a combination of 海驢 ''ashika'' (sea lion) and ''sirène'' (French for ''mermaid'' or ''siren'').
