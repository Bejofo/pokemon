'''Fletchinder''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''ヒノヤコマ''' ''Hinoyakoma'') is a dual-type {{2t|Fire|Flying}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation VI]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Fletchling}} starting at [[level]] 17 and evolves into {{p|Talonflame}} starting at level 35.

==Biology==
Fletchinder is an avian {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} with predominantly orange and black plumage. It has a sharp, black beak, black eyes with yellow, mask-like markings behind them, and a small, pointed crest on the back of its head. Its upper side is reddish-orange and its belly is gray. There is a flame pattern on its breast where the two colors meet. The tips and undersides of its pointed, black wings are yellow. Its legs are also black with sharp talons and two toes facing forward and one back. Two white, V-shaped markings are located near the tip of its long black tail-feathers. Its tail ends in two points and has another feather sticking out of the base on each side, resembling the {{wp|fletching}} on the end of an arrow.

Fletchinder has a flame sac in its belly. It hunts by igniting tall grass and swooping down on bug Pokémon that attempt to escape the blaze. Once captured, the prey is cooked before being consumed. As its internal fire gradually heats up, it can fly at increasingly faster speeds. It is aggressive and territorial, even against its own kind. It often fights with its own kind for the right to feeding grounds. Its territory range has a radius of over one mile, or around 2-3 km.<!--as mentioned in the non-English versions of the Violet dex entry-->

Fletchinder and its evolutionary relatives are the [[Signature Ability|only known Pokémon]] that can have {{a|Gale Wings}} as an [[Ability]].

==Trivia==
<!--Fletchinder does NOT have the longest name. Crabominable has the longest name and its trivia already says so.-->
* Fletchinder is the [[List of Pokémon with unique base stat totals|only Pokémon]] with a base stat total of 382.
* Fletchinder shares its {{pkmn|category}} with {{p|Simisear}}. They are both known as the Ember Pokémon.
** In Japanese, the two Pokémon also share their category with {{p|Charcadet}}, namely ひのこポケモン ''Hinoko Pokémon'' (Sparks Pokémon).
* Fletchinder is the first Pokémon, and the only Pokémon in [[Generation VI]], to have an English name more than 10 letters long.
** This is because games from previous generations had a ten-character limit on English Pokémon names, which can be seen in the shortening of ''gator'' to ''gatr'' in {{p|Feraligatr}}'s name.

===Origin===
Fletchinder appears to be a cross between a {{wp|Japanese robin}} and a {{wp|waxwing}}. Its {{t|Fire}} type may be a reference to the {{wp|flame robin}}.

====Name origin====
Fletchinder may be a combination of ''{{wp|fletching}}'' and ''tinder'' or ''cinder''.

Hinoyakoma may be a combination of 火の粉 ''hinoko'' (sparks), 矢 ''ya'' (arrow), and 駒鳥 ''komadori'' ({{wp|Japanese robin}}).
