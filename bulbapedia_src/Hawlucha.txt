'''Hawlucha''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''ルチャブル''' ''Luchabull'') is a dual-type {{2t|Fighting|Flying}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation VI]].

It is not known to [[Evolution|evolve]] into or from any other Pokémon.

Hawlucha will be able to [[Mega Evolution|Mega Evolve]] into '''Mega Hawlucha''' in the upcoming game {{g|Legends: Z-A}}.

==Biology==
Hawlucha is a bipedal, birdlike {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} with colorful plumage. Its head curves into a beak-shape in the front, but its mouth is located in a white patch under the beak's curve. It has a blue-green mask-like marking across its face, an orange blaze running from its forehead to the back of its neck, an orange ring around each eye, and a small, red patch at the tip of its beak. There is black space around its eyes, which are yellow with paler yellow sclerae. On the back of its head are three rectangular, tassel-like feathers. Its red, cape-like wings have blue-green undersides and are connected to its feathery tail. Near the tip of each wing is a three-clawed hand. Its front is white with a line of red along its neck and the top of its chest. It has small, yellow feet with three toes each.

This forest-dwelling Pokémon prefers to live a quiet and solitary life, which makes the noisy {{p|Primeape}} its natural enemy. Despite its small body, Hawlucha's proficiency in fighting allows it to keep up with bigger fighters, such as {{p|Machamp}} and {{p|Hariyama}}. It overwhelms its opponents with quick and nimble moves, exploiting their blind spots and controlling its position in the air with its wings. One of its favorite tactics is to strike from above, because it is difficult for opponents to defend against. Once its foe is driven to exhaustion, Hawlucha is compelled to show off with a flashy finishing move, which it has practiced in the forests it calls home. However, opponents can sometimes exploit its vanity to gain the upper hand.

Hawlucha is the [[signature move|only known Pokémon]] capable of learning the move {{m|Flying Press}} by leveling up.

===Forms===
Hawlucha can [[Mega Evolution|Mega Evolve]] into Mega Hawlucha.

Mega Hawlucha is considerably bulkier compared to Hawlucha, thanks to the energy of Mega Evolution coursing through its muscles.<ref name=ZAsite>[https://legends.pokemon.com/en-us/story-world/pokemon/mega-hawlucha Pokémon Legends: Z-A | Mega Hawlucha] — Pokémon Official Website</ref> Its face is black with a gold arrow-like pattern running down from the top, a gold coloration at its snout, and gold eyelash-like decorations around its blank white eyes, giving the impression of a luchador mask. Its head feathers have become longer, and it gains more plumage around its neck, legs (resembling shin guards), and waist (forming a "belt" around it). Its wings have also become sharper and more curved in shape. The three central plumes on its wings have an elaborate black-and-gold patterning on the back at their tips. Mega Hawlucha gains a large, golden chestplate with black stripes around the edges that covers its shoulders and forearms.

Mega Evolution increases Hawlucha's attacking and defensive capabilities, allowing it to use its muscles to tank hits from opponents' moves and to flex its strength. Mega Hawlucha's heightened defense also allows it to showboat and display its toughness and beauty, holding out its hands and spreading its wings without worrying about letting its guard down. By luring its opponent into attacking, it is able to retaliate with a flinch-inducing move before diving at them with its signature move.<ref name=ZAsite/>

===Major appearances===
====[[Ash's Hawlucha]]====
Hawlucha debuted in ''[[XY035|The Forest Champion!]]''. Prior to his capture by {{Ash}}, Hawlucha was the champion of a forest. Later, Ash challenged Hawlucha to battle with the condition that if he wins, Hawlucha will join his [[Party|team]]. Although Hawlucha didn't lose the battle, he decided to join Ash's team anyway.

====Other====
A [[Mirror World]] Hawlucha appeared in ''[[XY037|The Cave of Mirrors!]]'', under the ownership of [[Mirror Ash]]. He was sent out to battle {{TRT}}, but he was defeated due to his timid personality.

A {{DL|List of alternately colored Pokémon in animation|Hawlucha|Shiny}} Hawlucha, nicknamed "Dark Hawlucha", appeared in ''[[XY051|When Light and Dark Collide!]]'', under the ownership of {{OBP|Carl|XY051}}. It was one of Carl's actors in his Super Pokémon Battle Shows.

A Hawlucha appeared in ''[[JN034|Solitary and Menacing!]]'', under the ownership of [[Bea]]. It was used in her [[World Coronation Series]] {{pkmn|battle}} against Ash, where it went up against his {{AP|Farfetch'd}} and won. It was then recalled for {{p|Grapploct}}. It reappeared in a flashback in ''[[JN039|Octo-Gridlock at the Gym!]]''. In ''[[JN085|Battle Three With Bea!]]'' and ''[[JN086|A Battle of Mega Versus Max!]]'', it took part in Ash's third battle against Bea.

In ''[[JN122|It's... Champion Time!]]'', [[Diantha]]'s Hawlucha was briefly seen during a montage of her and {{an|Leon}}'s [[Masters Eight Tournament]] battle.

===Minor appearances===
A Hawlucha appeared in ''[[M19|Volcanion and the Mechanical Marvel]]''.

===Origin===
Hawlucha appears to be based on a combination of a {{wp|bird of prey}} (its English name suggests it is based on a {{wp|hawk}}) and a {{wp|lucha libre|luchador}}, and may also draw inspiration from Aztec {{wp|eagle warrior}}s. The clawed wings resemble those of a juvenile {{wp|hoatzin}}, and its coloration may be based on the male {{wp|resplendent quetzal}}. Hawlucha's green, white and red color scheme may be inspired by the {{wp|flag of Mexico}}, which fits its lucha libre inspiration. Its eagle-like design and the gold color on Mega Hawlucha may be inspired by the {{wp|golden eagle}} featured on the flag. Hawlucha debuting in the [[Kalos]] region may be a reference to {{wp|wrestling mask|masked wrestling}} originating in {{wp|Paris}}.

<!--Do NOT add Rey Mysterio as a possible inspiration, unless you have a source confirming it.-->
Mega Hawlucha's face resembles various designs of {{wp|wrestling mask|luchador masks}}. The thin head feathers and covered eyes may also be inspired by {{wp|falconry training and technique|falconry hoods}}. The covered eyes may also be inspired by {{wp|nictitating membrane|nictitating membranes}}. Mega Hawlucha may also take further inspiration from Aztec culture, with ornamentation resembling Aztec ceremonial garb.

====Name origin====
Hawlucha may be a combination of ''hawk'' and ''{{wp|lucha libre}}'', a form of Latin American professional wrestling.

Luchabull may be a combination of ''lucha libre'' and ''bull''.
