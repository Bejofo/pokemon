'''Buizel''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''ブイゼル''' ''Buoysel'') is a {{type|Water}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation IV]].

It [[Evolution|evolve]]s into {{p|Floatzel}} starting at [[level]] 26.

==Biology==
[[File:Flying Buizel.png|thumb|left|250px|Buizel flying using its tail in ''[[DP011|Mounting a Coordinator Assault]]'']]
Buizel is an orange {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} resembling a weasel and a sea otter. It has a yellow collar around its neck and two blue fins on its arms. Buizel has a cream-colored spot above each eye and two black parallel marks on its cheeks. The underside of its face and stomach are also cream-colored, as are the tips of its two tails. A male Buizel has two cream-colored spots on their back, while a female has only one.

Buizel's yellow collar is a flotation sac that can be inflated when it inhales. By doing so, Buizel can keep its face above water to watch for prey movement or survey its surroundings. Conversely, it deflates this sac in order to dive. Its tails can spin to act as a propeller, allowing it to swim and slice through obstacles like seaweed. In the {{aniseries|DP}} episode ''[[DP011|Mounting a Coordinator Assault!]]'', it is also shown to be able to fly using its spinning tail. Buizel usually lives alongside bodies of water.

===Major appearances===
====[[Ash's Buizel|Dawn's/Ash's Buizel]]====
{{an|Dawn}} {{pkmn2|caught}} a male Buizel in ''[[DP034|Buizel Your Way Out Of This!]]''. Eventually, in ''[[DP055|Throwing the Track Switch]]'', she [[trade]]d him for [[Ash's Aipom]] because Buizel turned out to be more interested in [[Gym]] {{pkmn|battle}}s than [[Pokémon Contest]]s.

====Other====
In ''[[M09|Pokémon Ranger and the Temple of the Sea]]'', [[the Marina Group]] owns a Buizel. However, it didn't perform with them like the other Water Pokémon, instead being treated more like a pet.

In ''[[PK17|Pikachu's Island Adventure]]'', a Buizel, six {{p|Wynaut}}, and two {{p|Pichu}} were forced off their island by {{MTR}} and his cronies, who claimed the island for themselves. As a result, Pikachu and his friends agreed to help them reclaim the island.

In ''[[BW099|A Surface to Air Tag Battle Team!]]'', [[Soren and Rocko|Soren]] caught a Buizel whose {{m|Aqua Jet}} became a vital component for a unique combination invented by Soren, called "Jet Brave".

===Minor appearances===
Buizel debuted in ''[[PK16|Pikachu's Ocean Adventure]]''.

Buizel made its animated series debut in ''[[DP011|Mounting a Coordinator Assault!]]'', under the ownership of a {{pkmn|Coordinator}} competing in the [[Jubilife City]] [[Pokémon Contest]].

A Coordinator's Buizel appeared in ''[[DP027|Settling a Not-So-Old Score!]]''.

Two Buizel appeared in ''[[M17|Diancie and the Cocoon of Destruction]]''.

In the [[Where Are You Going, Eevee?]] segment at the end of ''[[SM093|Lillier and the Staff!]]'', two Buizel were swimming in the ocean. They reappeared in a flashback in ''[[SM099|We Know Where You're Going, Eevee!]]''.

Two Buizel appeared in ''[[JN046|Getting More Than You Battled For!]]''.

In ''[[JN063|On Land, In the Sea, and to the Future!]]'', a {{pkmn|Trainer}}'s Buizel competed in the [[Marine Athletic Race]].

In ''[[JN090|Showdown at the Gates of Warp!]]'', a Trainer's Floatzel [[devolution|devolved]] into Buizel due to {{OBP|Dialga|recurring}}'s influence.

Two Buizel appeared in ''[[HZ031|Song Within the Mist]]'', under the ownership of two {{tc|Sailor}}'s.

A Trainer's Buizel appeared in ''[[HZ042|Transform! Hero of the Seas, Palafin]]''.

==Trivia==
* Buizel was one of three Pokémon revealed in March 2006 in promotions for ''[[M09|Pokémon Ranger and the Temple of the Sea]]'', along with {{p|Mantyke}} and {{p|Chatot}}.
* Every Buizel that has appeared in the {{pkmn|animated series}} has been male.

===Origin===
Buizel seems to be based on a {{wp|weasel}}, as well as a {{wp|sea otter}}. Its design also appears to incorporate various {{wp|flotation devices}}, such as {{wp|swim ring}}s and {{wp|inflatable armbands}}. Buizel's two tails can rotate like a screw similarly how boats use {{wp|propeller}}s to move by pushing water.

====Name origin====
Buizel and Buoysel may be combinations of ''{{wp|buoy}}'' and ''{{wp|weasel}}''.
