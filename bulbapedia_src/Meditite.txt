'''Meditite''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''アサナン''' ''Asanan'') is a dual-type {{2t|Fighting|Psychic}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation III]].

It [[Evolution|evolve]]s into {{p|Medicham}} starting at [[level]] 37.

==Biology==
Meditite is a humanoid, bipedal {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} that resembles a person meditating. It usually sits in a yoga position, with knees bent and feet together with its hands resting on its knees. Its lower body is blue, with white on the wrists, feet, and lower body. Its onion-shaped head is also white with large eyes and pink ovals on its cheeks. It has round, swirled ears on the sides of its head. These ears are higher up on a male than on a female.

Meditite heightens its inner energy and spiritual power through meditation, which also allows it to levitate. It trains hours a day and never skips its daily yoga training. Meditite's training never ends, since it constantly loses concentration and focus while meditating. It eats one berry per day as part of training, which tempers its spirit and makes its senses sharper. It lives and trains on the {{DL|List of Pokémon by habitat|Mountain Pokémon|mountains}}.

Meditite and its evolved form, {{p|Medicham}}, are the [[Signature Ability|only known Pokémon]] that can have {{a|Pure Power}} as an [[Ability]].

==In animation==
===Main series===
[[File:Kira Meditite.png|thumb|250px|Meditite in {{aniseries|JN}}]]
====Major appearances====
Meditite debuted in ''[[AG030|A Meditite Fight]]'', under the ownership of {{ho|Shauna}}. It was used during her two {{pkmn|battle}}s with {{Ash}}, and it ultimately lost in the second battle.

In ''[[M09|Pokémon Ranger and the Temple of the Sea]]'', [[the Marina Group]] used a Meditite for their water show. It and a {{p|Medicham}} would use {{m|Psychic}} to make balls of water that would retain their form.

In a flashback in ''[[DP066|Lost Leader Strategy!]]'', [[Maylene]] used a Meditite in her [[Gym]] {{pkmn|battle}} with [[Paul]], but it was defeated by {{TP|Paul|Honchkrow}}. In ''[[DP067|Crossing the Battle Line!]]'', it was used in a battle with {{an|Dawn}}. It battled her {{TP|Dawn|Buneary}} and won, but then lost to {{TP|Dawn|Ambipom}}. In ''[[DP068|A Triple Fighting Chance!]]'', Meditite was used to battle Ash. It managed to confuse his {{AP|Chimchar}} and {{AP|Staravia}} before losing to the latter.

In ''[[JN014|Raid Battle in The Ruins!]]'', {{OBP|Kira|JN014}}'s Meditite first saved Ash after he fell into a trap. It was then used to battle a {{pkmn2|giant}} {{TP|Goh|Golurk}} during a [[Raid Battle (GO)#In animation|Raid Battle]], but it was soon defeated.

====Minor appearances====
A Meditite appeared in the opening sequence of ''[[M08|Lucario and the Mystery of Mew]]''.

In ''[[AG162|Spontaneous Combusken!]]'', a Meditite was at the [[Chrysanthemum Island]] beach.

==Trivia==
* In {{game|HeartGold and SoulSilver|s}}, Meditite is obtainable via the [[Sinnoh Sound]] even though it was introduced in [[Generation III]]. However, it appears in the [[List of Pokémon by Sinnoh Pokédex number|Sinnoh Pokédex]].
* Like its Evolution, {{p|Medicham}}, Meditite is one of the few Pokémon whose official artwork shows the female form.
** In Generation III, before the introduction of gender differences, all Meditite and Medicham had the appearance of a female regardless of gender.
* Meditite has the lowest base {{stat|HP}} stat of all {{type|Fighting}} Pokémon.
* Meditite's normal and {{Shiny}} colorations are opposites of those of its Evolution {{p|Medicham}}. Meditite's normal coloration is mainly blue, while its Shiny coloration is mainly pink, whereas {{p|Medicham}} is pink and blue, respectively.

===Origin===
Meditite appears to have been based on a practitioner of {{wp|yoga}} and is {{wp|meditation|meditating}} in almost all of the media it appears in. Its yoga position in its artwork and sprites is the {{wp|Lotus position}}, where one crosses one's legs and places their feet on the opposing thighs. This position supposedly holds the mind completely steady for long periods of time and calms it, heightening the user's senses. Its hat somewhat resembles a {{wp|turban}} and may also be the caps worn by many depictions of {{wp|Hindu deities}}, such as {{wp|Shiva|Shiva}}, who are commonly seen meditating.

====Name origin====
Meditite may be a combination of ''meditate'' and ''petite'' or ''tyke''. It may also involve ''tight'' as in ''tight focus''.

Asanan may be derived from ''{{wp|asana}}'' (body posture in yoga) and possibly 軟 ''nan'' (soft, flexible).
