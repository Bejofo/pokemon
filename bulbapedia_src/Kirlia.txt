'''Kirlia''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''キルリア''' ''Kirlia'') is a dual-type {{2t|Psychic|Fairy}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation III]]. Prior to [[Generation VI]], it was a pure {{type|Psychic}} Pokémon.

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Ralts}} starting at [[level]] 20 and evolves into {{p|Gardevoir}} if male or female starting at level 30 or, if male, {{p|Gallade}} when exposed to a [[Dawn Stone]].

==Biology==
Kirlia is a bipedal, maiden-like humanoid {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} that appears to be wearing a tutu. Most of its body is colored white, and there are three ruffles on each hip. Beneath the ruffles, it has skinny green legs with long, pointed feet. Its arms are also thin with two digits each. It has green hair that covers most of its face and reaches its shoulders on either side, resembling two {{wp|ponytail}}s. Large, red eyes are visible on either side of the hair that covers its face. On each side of its head is a flat, crimson horn that resembles a hairpin.

Kirlia's horns are used to amplify its psychokinetic power and distort the air around it. The distortion in the air causes scenery mirages and can cause a rip in dimensions. This ability also allows Kirlia to see into the future. It enjoys dancing on sunny mornings, and grows beautiful when exposed to a {{pkmn|Trainer}} with positive emotions. Kirlia inhabits {{DL|List of Pokémon by habitat|Urban Pokémon|urban areas}}. As mentioned in {{g|Sleep}}, Kirlia is known to dance while it sleeps.<ref>[[Sleep style|Sleep Style Dex]]</ref> The reason is due to Kirlia having a premonition of a fun future in its dreams.

===Evolution===
Kirlia evolves from {{p|Ralts}} and evolves into either {{p|Gardevoir}} if male or female or, if [[Gender|male]], {{p|Gallade}}.

===Major appearances===
Kirlia debuted in ''[[M06|Jirachi: Wish Maker]]'', under the ownership of [[Butler]]. It was used for a magic act during the [[Millennium Festival]], and later to fight an {{p|Absol}}.

In ''[[AG086|Lights, Camerupt, Action!]]'', [[Elijah]] used a Kirlia in a movie where it played the {{wp|damsel in distress}} in the movie, being taken by an {{p|Exploud}} and later rescued by {{p|Plusle}} and {{p|Minun}}.

In ''[[AG109|Do I Hear a Ralts?]]'', a {{pkmn2|wild}} Kirlia and Gardevoir sent {{TRT}} flying when they cornered [[Max]] and {{AP|Snorunt}} while they were caring for a Ralts and taking it to the [[Pokémon Center]]. However, Kirlia and Gardevoir thought Max was also trying to steal Ralts. When Ralts blocked Kirlia and Gardevoir's {{m|Psychic}}, the two Pokémon calmed down and left. Later, Kirlia and Gardevoir sent [[James]] blasting off, as Ralts had already launched [[Jessie]] and {{MTR}} away for posing as Gardevoir and Kirlia, respectively.

In ''[[AG146|Fear Factor Phony]]'', a Kirlia was living at an abandoned mining colony. [[Jessie's Wobbuffet]] fell in love with the Emotion Pokémon.

In a flashback in ''[[DP020|Mutiny in the Bounty!]]'', a Kirlia was one of [[J]]'s bounties. In the present day, [[Melodi]] owned another Kirlia. It was used to help Ash and {{ashfr}} find out where J and [[J's henchmen|her minions]] will strike next, after they stole Meowth, [[Ash's Pikachu]], and Melodi's Gardevoir.

In ''[[DP155|Double-Time Battle Training!]]'', [[Zoey]] used a male Kirlia, along with her {{p|Leafeon}}, in a [[Double Battle]] against [[Dawn's Cyndaquil]] and {{TP|Dawn|Mamoswine}}. In ''[[DP177|A Grand Fight for Winning!]]'', he was revealed to have evolved into a {{p|Gallade}}.

==Trivia==
* Kirlia shares its {{pkmn|category}} with {{p|Mesprit}} and {{p|Indeedee}}. They are all known as Emotion Pokémon.
* Kirlia's number in the [[List of Pokémon by Sinnoh Pokédex number|Sinnoh Pokédex]] ({{v2|Platinum}} only) and the [[List of Pokémon by Almia Browser number|Almia Browser]] are the same: 158.
* Kirlia and its evolutionary relatives are the only Pokémon that are in both the {{egg|Amorphous}} and {{egg|Human-Like}} [[Egg Group]]s.

===Origin===
Kirlia seems to be based on a young, feminine {{wp|ballet dancer}} and the concept of {{wp|empathy}}. The white frills surrounding its body resemble a {{wp|tutu (clothing)|tutu}}, and its thin, pointed legs make it appear as if it is standing {{wp|pointe technique|en pointe}} and wearing green {{wp|tights}}. Its hair is styled into {{wp|pigtail}}s, with its horns positioned like {{wp|barette}}s or {{wp|scrunchie}}s. The red horns also resemble two halves of a {{wp|heart shape|heart}}, which may relate to its ability to sense emotions.

Kirlia and its evolutionary line also bear a strong resemblance to the {{wp|elf|meadow elves}}, who were believed to possess magical powers and supernatural beauty. The word ''elf'' in {{wp|Germanic|Germanic}} languages originally meant "white being," which may explain why they were often depicted wearing white tights. By the end of the medieval period, the word ''elf'' was frequently supplanted by the French loanword ''fairy,'' which may explain their {{t|Fairy}} typing starting from [[Generation VI]].

Kirlia in particular appears to be inspired by the Swedish ''älvor'', elves who lived in forests and were often depicted in paintings as young girls adorned with {{wp|Wreath (attire)|flower wreaths}}, dancing over meadows. This imagery parallels the horn-like ornaments on its head as well as its characteristic fondness for dancing.

====Name origin====
Kirlia may be derived from ''{{wp|Kirlian photography}}'' (a special way of taking pictures that supposedly reveals psychic energy auras around subjects).
