'''Beautifly''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''アゲハント''' ''Agehunt'') is a dual-type {{2t|Bug|Flying}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation III]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Silcoon}} starting at [[level]] 10. It is one of {{p|Wurmple}}'s final forms, the other being {{p|Dustox}}.

==Biology==
Beautifly is a {{wp|butterfly}}-like {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} easily recognizable by its two pairs of colorful wings. It has large blue eyes and black antennae with rounded tips. Its face, underside, and four stubby limbs are gray, while the rest of its body is black. The main body of its wings are black with large, orange-yellow markings, and smaller red and blue markings. The upper pair of wings are larger and have a line of yellow along the top edge, while each of the lower wings has a long, round-tipped extension on the bottom. The female's wings have smaller red spots than the male's.

Beautifly has a long and curled black proboscis that it uses to gather pollen and nectar — its favorite food — from flowers. Its diet also includes water, fruit juices, and bodily fluids from its prey, all of which it sucks up with its proboscis. Despite its appearance, Beautifly is known to be aggressive and greedy; it will jab at foes with its proboscis if it is disturbed while collecting pollen. The preferred habitats of a Beautifly are {{DL|List of Pokémon by habitat|Forest Pokémon|forests}}, where it rides the spring winds while searching for pollen. It competes with {{p|Combee}} for nectar.

===Evolution===
Beautifly evolves from {{p|Silcoon}} and is the final form of {{p|Wurmple}}.

====Major appearances====
=====[[May's Beautifly]]=====
In ''[[AG028|Seeing is Believing]]'', {{an|May}}'s Silcoon evolved into a Beautifly. From then on, it was used in many [[Pokémon Contest]]s during May's [[Hoenn]] {{pkmn|journey}}. It was eventually left at the [[Petalburg Gym]] after May competed in the [[Hoenn Grand Festival]]. Beautifly briefly rejoined May's [[Party|team]] in ''[[AG187|The Unbeatable Lightness of Seeing!]]'', and again to participate in the [[Wallace Cup]] during {{aniseries|DP}}.

=====[[Aaron's Beautifly]]=====
In ''[[DP099|A Trainer and Child Reunion!]]'', [[Aaron]]'s Wurmple was revealed to have evolved into a Beautifly after the two reunited.

=====Other=====
In ''[[AG013|All Things Bright and Beautifly!]]'', [[Janet]] used a Beautifly for the [[Rustboro City]] {{pkmn|Contest}}. Passing through the [[Contest Battle|Battle Stage]], it reached the final round, where it went up against {{an|Chaz}}'s {{p|Venomoth}} and won, earning Janet the Rustboro [[Ribbon]]. This Beautifly inspired {{an|May}} to become a {{pkmn|Coordinator}} and {{pkmn2|caught|catch}} her own {{TP|May|Wurmple}}.

In ''[[HS05|The Blue Badge of Courage]]'', [[Sakura]] used a Beautifly to help {{an|Misty}} stop [[Gym Badge thieves|the thieves]] stealing her [[Gym]] [[Badge]] supply.

In ''[[M12|Arceus and the Jewel of Life]]'', [[Kiko]] used a Beautifly in a Tag Battle against [[Ash's Pikachu]] and [[Dawn's Piplup]], where it worked alongside [[Kato]]'s {{p|Heracross}}.

In [[PK18]], Pikachu and his friends collided into multiple Beautifly during an airborne chase. Out of anger, they caused a strong wind that blew everyone in different directions.

In ''[[JN022|Goodbye, Friend!]]'', {{Ash}} and [[Goh]] witnessed multiple Beautifly evolving from Silcoon. They reappeared in a flashback in ''[[JN046|Getting More Than You Battled For!]]''.

==Trivia==
* Beautifly shares its {{pkmn|category}} with {{p|Butterfree}}. They are both known as the Butterfly Pokémon.
* Prior to [[Generation VI]], Beautifly and its [[List of Pokémon with branched Evolutions|evolutionary counterpart]] {{p|Dustox}} shared the same [[base stats]], with Beautifly's {{stat|Attack}} and {{stat|Special Attack}} swapped with Dustox's {{stat|Defense}} and {{stat|Special Defense}}. This changed in Generation VI when Beautifly received a ten-point increase to its Special Attack, but Dustox did not receive a stat increase. It shares this counterpart difference with {{p|Poliwrath}}.

===Origin===
Beautifly seems to be based on a {{wp|swallowtail butterfly}}, particularly the {{wp|Papilio machaon|common yellow swallowtail}} due to the shape and colors of its wings. The orange-yellow and black colors of its wings also bring to mind the {{wp|monarch butterfly}}. Its ability to pierce prey with its {{wp|proboscis}} is similar to a {{wp|Calyptra (moth)#Penetrating skin|vampire moth}} or a {{wp|mosquito}}.

====Name origin====
Beautifly may be a combination of ''beauty'' and ''butterfly''. It could also be a play on ''beautify'' (to improve the appearance of) or ''beautifully''.

Agehunt may be a combination of 揚羽蝶 ''agehachō'' (swallowtail butterfly) and ''hunt'' (referring to its aggressive nature).
