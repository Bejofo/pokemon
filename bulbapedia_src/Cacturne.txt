'''Cacturne''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''ノクタス''' ''Noctus'') is a dual-type {{2t|Grass|Dark}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation III]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Cacnea}} starting at [[level]] 32.

==Biology==
Cacturne is a humanoid, bipedal {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} that resembles a green scarecrow made of cacti. Its face has two eye holes through which yellow eyes can be seen, and six smaller holes representing a grinning mouth. It has numerous spikes along the length of its arms, legs, and around its neck. A pattern of green rhombi extends down the front of its body. A female has a large dark green rhombus above a smaller one, while a male has three small dark green rhombi. There is a triangular, dark green feature on its head that resembles a hat with spiky tips.

Cacturne is a nocturnal Pokémon that rarely moves during the day, which allows it to hold moisture. At night, it searches for prey or follows travelers until they become exhausted. Prior to [[Generation V]], {{m|Needle Arm}} was its [[signature move]]. It has lived in {{DL|List of Pokémon by habitat|Rough-terrain Pokémon|deserts}} for so long that its blood has transformed into sand.

==Trivia==
* No other Pokémon has the same [[Egg Group]] combination as Cacturne and its pre-evolved form.
** Cacturne and its pre-evolved form are also the only {{type|Grass}} Pokémon in the {{egg3|Human-Like}}.
* Not counting the {{p|Porygon}} line, {{p|Jynx}} and {{p|Kadabra}}, which all have legal or controversial issues attached, Cacturne is the only Pokémon from the first three generations that never appeared in ''[[Pokémon the Series]]'' or {{pkmn|movies}} based on that series since ''[[Pokémon the Series: Ruby and Sapphire]]''.

===Origin===
Cacturne appears to be based on a {{wp|cactus}}, possibly the {{wp|saguaro}}, and a {{wp|scarecrow}}. It could be associated with the nighttime because cacti are known as night ({{wp|Crassulacean acid metabolism|CAM}}) plants, since their {{wp|carbon dioxide}} fixation process occurs during the night. Its face is similar to a classic {{wp|Halloween}} {{wp|jack-o'-lantern}}, referencing the tendency for scarecrows to have pumpkins for heads.

====Name origin====
Cacturne and Noctus may be a combination of ''{{wp|cactus}}'' and ''nocturne'' (French for ''{{wp|nocturnal}}'').
