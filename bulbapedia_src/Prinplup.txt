'''Prinplup''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''ポッタイシ''' ''Pottaishi'') is a {{type|Water}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation IV]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Piplup}} starting at [[level]] 16 and evolves into {{p|Empoleon}} starting at level 36.

==Biology==
Prinplup is a blue, {{wp|penguin}}-like {{OBP|Pokémon|species}}. It is mostly dark blue with light blue facial markings, wing tips, and lower body. Its eyes are blue and it has a short yellow beak with a small point above it. Two large, yellow ridges extend from its beak over the back of its head and meet just above the nape of its neck. On its body are four large, white spots that resemble buttons. Instead of arms, it has long, sturdy, flipper-like wings that are capable of knocking over and snapping trees. It has short legs with yellow, three-toed feet.

Prinplup has a high sense of self-pride, which prevents it from forming groups of its own kind. This Pokémon can be found in icy waters, where it hunts for prey. Prinplup can swim gracefully in the cold seas. Its singing voice has been compared to a roaring tide.

===Evolution===
Prinplup evolves from {{p|Piplup}} and evolves into {{p|Empoleon}}.

===Main series===
====Major appearances====
=====[[Kenny's Prinplup]]=====
A Prinplup appeared in ''[[DP026|Getting the Pre-Contest Titters]]'', under the ownership of {{si|Kenny}}. In [[DP027|the following episode]], it battled [[Dawn's Piplup]]. In ''[[DP174|Last Call — First Round!]]'', it was revealed to have evolved into an Empoleon by the time of the [[Sinnoh Grand Festival]].

=====Other=====
Prinplup debuted in ''[[DP004|Dawn Of A New Era!]]''. Three young {{pkmn|Trainer}}s stopped by at a [[Pokémon Center]] with their [[first partner Pokémon]], but the girl of that group had a Prinplup.

A Student's Prinplup appeared in [[HZ090]].

==Trivia==
* Prinplup shares its category with its pre-evolved form and {{p|Eiscue}}. They are all known as the Penguin Pokémon.

===Origin===
Prinplup is based on a {{wp|penguin}}. The crests on its head suggest it may be based on a species of {{wp|crested penguin}}. Prinplup's body looks like it is clothed in a {{wp|tailcoat}} adorned with buttons, often likened to the black and white plumage of penguins.

====Name origin====
Prinplup may be a combination of ''prince'' and ''plup'' (sound of a pebble hitting water).

Pottaishi may be a combination of ぽちゃぽちゃ ''pochapocha'' (sound of a pebble splashing in water) and 皇太子 ''kōtaishi'' (crown prince).
