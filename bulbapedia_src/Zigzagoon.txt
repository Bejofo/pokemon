'''Zigzagoon''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''ジグザグマ''' ''Jiguzaguma'') is a {{type|Normal}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation III]].

It [[Evolution|evolve]]s into {{p|Linoone}} starting at [[level]] 20.

In [[Galar]], Zigzagoon has a dual-type {{2t|Dark|Normal}} [[regional form]]. It evolves into Galarian Linoone starting at level 20, which evolves into {{p|Obstagoon}} when leveled up starting at level 35 during the [[Time-based Evolution|night]]. Hoennian Linoone is not known to evolve into any other Pokémon.

==Biology==
Zigzagoon is a quadrupedal mammalian {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} resembling a raccoon, raccoon dog, or badger, with bristly, zigzag-patterned fur. It has stripes formed by alternating layers of cream and brown fur. It has a brown head with spiky ears and a black nose. A black mask-like pattern surrounds its brown eyes. The upper half of its mouth is jagged, and when it is open, there are two pointed teeth in its lower jaw. Its feet have three claws and pink paw pads. Its fore feet are cream-colored, but the hind feet are brown. Its brush tail is spiky and bristly.

Zigzagoon is abundantly curious, as it wanders restlessly back and forth while rubbing its nose to the ground in search of something. It always moves in a zigzag fashion, and leaves behind distinct zigzag footprints as it runs. It rubs its bristly back hair against trees to mark its territory and plays dead to fool opponents in battle. The Zigzagoon found outside of [[Galar]] are friendlier and calmer compared to their Galarian relatives. Zigzagoon are capable of {{a|Pickup|finding items in the grass or ground}}, including items that were lost. It is found commonly in {{DL|List of Pokémon by habitat|Grassland Pokémon|grassy fields}}.

===Forms===
Zigzagoon has a [[regional form]]: [[Galarian form|Galarian]] Zigzagoon.

Zigzagoon native to [[Galar]] generally look similar to its Hoennian counterpart with a few differences, most notably its black-and-white fur. Its red eyes are surrounded by a black spiked mask-like pattern somewhat resembling stars. Its fore feet are black with white claws, while its hind feet are white with black claws. It has a long pink tongue that is constantly hanging outside of its mouth, between its lower teeth. This Galarian form of Zigzagoon is believed to be the earliest, and thus the original, instance of the species.

Much like the Zigzagoon found outside of Galar, Galarian Zigzagoon are known to be constantly on the move and never settling down. It is believed that Galarian Zigzagoon's restless temperament is what originated the zigzag movements observed on both forms of the species. Their known habitat stretches the entirety of Galar, being found in fields, forests, and towns. They enjoy battling to the point where they often attempt to provoke others into fighting them. While this succeeded with other Pokémon, humans instead tend to mistake it as wanting affection, much to the Zigzagoon's frustrations.<ref>[https://swordshield.pokemon.com/en-us/pokemon-galar-region/zigzagoon/ Galarian Zigzagoon | Official Website | Pokémon Sword and Pokémon Shield]</ref>

===NPC appearances===
* [[Pokémon Pinball: Ruby & Sapphire]]: Zigzagoon sits to the left of the [[Sapphire Field]] and will change its position when the ball hits the button in front of it. When Zigzagoon is positioned to pounce, it will immediately stop the slot machine when the A button is pressed, allowing for the prize to be chosen by the player.

====Major appearances====
In ''[[AG005|In the Knicker of Time]]'', [[Nicholai]] tried to {{pkmn2|caught|catch}} a Zigzagoon. He was eventually successful after encountering several. In ''[[AG048|Zig Zag Zangoose!]]'', it was used in a battle against {{Ash}} and his Pokémon.

In ''[[HS05|The Blue Badge of Courage]]'', the [[Gym Badge thieves]] used a Zigzagoon and a {{p|Poochyena}} to battle [[Misty's Psyduck]] and [[Sakura's Espeon]], but both were defeated.

In ''[[AG046|Candid Camerupt!]]'', [[Winstrate family|Victor Winstrate]]'s Zigzagoon battled [[Brock's Mudkip]] and was able to defeat it using {{m|Thunderbolt}}.

In a flashback in ''[[XY088|A Trip Down Memory Train!]]'', a wild {{TP|Clemont|Bunnelby}} befriended a Zigzagoon.

In ''[[JN022|Goodbye, Friend!]]'', two Zigzagoon attempted to steal Ash's [[Bag|backpack]], only to be warded off by [[Goh's Raboot]]. Raboot later visited them and their leader {{p|Lombre}} to give them some apples, after which they befriended Raboot.

In ''[[JN070|Take My Thief! Please!]]'', [[Jessie]] pulled a Zigzagoon from the [[Rocket Prize Master]] to battle Ash and [[Goh]]; however, it was defeated by [[Ash's Pikachu]].

==Trivia==
* Zigzagoon is one of the two Pokémon left out of the [[Hoenn Pokérap]]; the other is {{p|Relicanth}}.
* A {{Shiny}} Zigzagoon was distributed to {{game|Ruby and Sapphire|s}} via an interactive demo to patch the [[Berry glitch]].
** Due to this being available for over three years in North America, Zigzagoon has the honor of having the longest event.
* Zigzagoon has the same [[List of Pokémon by Hoenn Pokédex number|Hoenn Pokédex]] and [[List of Pokémon by Kalos Pokédex number|Central Kalos Pokédex]] numbers.
* According to [[Ken Sugimori]] in an interview, Zigzagoon's name was conceived first before its character design was created.<ref name=ND84>''Nintendo Dream'' Volume 84, February/March 2003 ([http://lavacutcontent.com/sugimori-masuda-gen-3-interview/ English translation] written by Jacob Newcomb and commissioned by Dr. Lava)</ref>
* Galarian Zigzagoon and its {{shiny}} form were designed by [[James Turner]].<ref>[https://twitter.com/JamesTurner_42/status/1348881864086478848 Tweet about designing Galarian Zigzagoon]</ref><ref>[https://twitter.com/JamesTurner_42/status/1354718268762824705 Tweet about designing shiny Galarian Zigzagoon]</ref>
* Zigzagoon, along with its Evolution {{p|Linoone}}, are the only Generation III Pokémon to have a [[regional form]].
* Galarian Zigzagoon and Galarian Linoone are the first regional variants to become [[Shadow Pokémon (GO)|Shadow Pokémon]] in {{g|GO}} before their original forms.

===Origin===
Zigzagoon's English and Japanese names, category, and fur pattern suggest it is based on a {{wp|raccoon}}, while its body shape and brown coloration were stated to be inspired by the {{wp|Japanese badger}}<ref name=Pokemon.com>(November 12, 2019). "[https://www.pokemon.com/us/pokemon-news/interview-with-game-freaks-ohmori-and-masuda-on-pokemon-sword-and-pokemon-shield/ Interview with GAME FREAK’s Ohmori and Masuda on ''Pokémon Sword'' and ''Pokémon Shield'']." Pokemon.com</ref> with its fur making a zigzag pattern. It may also be inspired by the {{wp|Japanese raccoon dog}} (''Nyctereutes procyonoides viverrinus''), also known as ''tanuki''. The word ''tanuki'' is used for both badgers and raccoon dogs in Japan, depending on the region. This may be the inspiration behind its mixture of traits, having similarities to both species. Japanese folklore and legend claim that ''tanuki'' are known for {{m|Belly Drum|drumming on their bellies}} with their front paws similar to how Zigzagoon has been known to do. Zigzagoon's body shape and appearance are also similar to certain small dog breeds such as {{wp|Pekingese}} and {{wp|Wheaten Terrier}}s.

<!--Do not add Gene Simmons/KISS or David Bowie/Ziggy Stardust, unless you have official source stating that it was inspired by either.-->
Galarian Zigzagoon's fur coloration draws inspiration from the {{wp|European badger}}, which has a black-and-white color scheme unlike its Japanese relative.<ref name=Pokemon.com/> Taking its evolved form {{p|Obstagoon}} under consideration, Galarian Zigzagoon's color scheme, pointed tongue, and star marking around its eyes may also be inspired by {{wp|Glam rock}} bands.

Zigzagoon's evolutionary line shares a common theme of movement where Zigzagoon moves in a zigzag pattern, {{p|Linoone}} moves in a straight line, and {{p|Obstagoon}} blocks the way by obstructing it.

====Name origin====
Zigzagoon may be a combination of ''zigzag'' (referring to its fur pattern and unusual habits) and ''raccoon''.

Jiguzaguma may be a combination of ''zigzag'' and アライグマ ''araiguma'' ({{wp|raccoon}}) or 穴熊 ''anaguma'' ({{wp|badger}}). ''Guma'' (from 熊 ''kuma'', "bear") as a suffix is often used for mammals that resemble bears.
