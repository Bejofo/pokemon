'''Luxio''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''ルクシオ''' ''Luxio'') is an {{type|Electric}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation IV]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Shinx}} starting at [[level]] 15 and evolves into {{p|Luxray}} starting at level 30.

==Biology==
Luxio is a feline, quadrupedal {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} resembling a young lynx or lion. Blue and black fur cover most of its body. Its ears, face, hind paws, and front half of its body are blue, while its hindquarters are black. Additionally, Luxio has a tufty black mane surrounding its face. The mane is longer on the male Luxio, while the blue coloration on the hind paws extends to the ankles on the female. The insides of its rounded ears are yellow, as are its eyes. There are two yellow rings around each front leg and a yellow, four-point star on the tip of its tail.

When facing an opponent, Luxio extends its claws in preparation, which can unleash 1,000,000 volts of electricity. Even a slight scratch from these claws is enough to cause fainting. Besides for attacking, Luxio also uses its claws to communicate with others of its kind, by resting them on the others' forelegs and sending electrical pulses through them. Luxio live in small groups and are able to generate powerful electricity by gathering the group's tails together. Luxio are known to be kind enough to share the spoils from a hunt to its own kind. 

As mentioned in [[Pokémon Sleep]], Luxio is often observed sleeping peacefully. It is believed that Luxio feel a sense of safety due living in small groups. On occasion, Luxio releases electricty from its claws while it slumbers. Researchers uses the rhythm released by the electric pulses to study Luxio's dreams. 

===Evolution===
Luxio evolves from {{p|Shinx}} and evolves into {{p|Luxray}}.

==In animation==
[[File:Clemont Luxio.png|thumb|250px|Luxio in {{aniseries|XY}}]]
===Major appearances===
====[[Clemont's Luxio]]====
In ''[[XY048|A Campus Reunion!]]'', {{an|Clemont}} {{pkmn2|caught}} a Luxio, which, prior to the episode, evolved from a Shinx that he befriended. In ''[[XY062|The Future Is Now, Thanks to Determination!]]'', Luxio evolved into a Luxray.

====Other====
Luxio debuted in ''[[M10|The Rise of Darkrai]]'', where it was among the Pokémon residing in a garden in [[Alamos Town]]. Luxio seemed to be shy, as it hesitated before it ran to {{Ash}} and {{ashfr}}' Pokémon. It later participated in a near-skirmish between two groups of Pokémon, caused by a misunderstanding. Afterwards, it was a bystander during the battle between {{mov|Dialga}} and {{mov|Palkia}}.

Luxio made its animated series debut in ''[[DP109|Leading a Stray!]]'', where it helped a {{p|Wailmer}} that was trapped in the sewers. As a result, the Wailmer made it back into the ocean safely and evolved into a {{p|Wailord}} before rejoining its pod.

===Minor appearances===
A Luxio appeared in the opening sequence of ''[[M11|Giratina and the Sky Warrior]]''.

A Luxio appeared in ''[[DP149|Sticking With Who You Know!]]''.

In ''[[DP160|A Marathon Rivalry!]]'', a Trainer's Luxio competed in the [[Pokéathlon]] held in [[Camellia Town]].

A Trainer's Luxio appeared in ''[[M23|Secrets of the Jungle]]''.

A Luxio appeared in ''[[HZ001|The Pendant That Starts It All (Part One)]]'', under the ownership of a student of [[Indigo Academy]].

A Trainer's Luxio appeared in ''[[HZ008|The Door That Never Opens]]''.

A Luxio appeared in ''[[HZ075|The Wonders of the World!]]'', during a flashback.

A Trainer's Luxio appeared in [[HZ097]].

==In the manga==

===Pokémon Adventures===
{{main|Rayler}}
{{adv|Platinum}} was attacked by a pack of {{pkmn2|wild}} {{p|Shinx}} and Luxio in ''[[PS340|Extreme Luxio]]''. One of them, due to his broken claws, was unable to communicate with his group and was shunned by them. In an attempt to regain his group's favor, he battled with {{adv|Diamond}}'s Pokémon. {{adv|Pearl}} eventually managed to get his comrades' trust and befriended the Luxio. Later, he evolved into a Luxray and joined Pearl's team after being named Rayler.

Multiple Luxio appeared in ''[[PS349|Passing by Probopass and Maneuvering around Magnezone]]''.

Two Luxio appeared in ''[[PS389|Cautious Clefairy]]''.

In ''[[PS435|The Final Dimensional Duel V]]'', a Luxio appeared on [[Fullmoon Island]] in [[Palmer]]'s flashback.

A Trainer's Luxio appeared in [[PASV12]].

===Pokémon Diamond and Pearl Adventure!===
{{main|Hareta's Luxio}}
[[Hareta]]'s {{p|Shinx}} evolved into Luxio after defeating [[B-2]] on [[Iron Island]] in ''[[DPA10|Serious Training on Iron Island!!]]''. His {{pkmn|Trainer}} attempted to use him against [[Cyrus]]'s {{p|Gyarados}} at [[Lake Valor]], but he was instead ambushed by [[Saturn]]'s {{p|Rhyperior}}, who had been [[revive]]d after being [[Fainting|knocked out]]. Later, he evolved into {{p|Luxray}} during Hareta's {{si|Pokémon League}} {{pkmn|battle}} with [[Koya]].

==Trivia==
* Luxio is the only {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} with a base stat total of 363.
* The official Japanese Pokémon website (Pokemon.co.jp) has a picture of Luxio on its {{wp|HTTP 404}} error page.<ref>[https://www.pokemon.co.jp/404 Pokemon.co.jp sample 404 error page]</ref> This alludes to Luxio's [[List of Pokémon by National Pokédex number|National Pokédex number]], 0404.

===Origin===
Luxio and its evolutionary relatives seem to be based on the {{wp|Lynx (constellation)|constellation Lynx}}, as well as the {{wp|lynx (mythology)|lynx in mythology}}, which medieval bestiaries sometimes depicted as a lion-like creature. Resembling an adolescent male {{wp|lion|lion}}, it may also be based on the neighboring constellation(s), {{wp|Leo (constellation)|Leo}} and/or {{wp|Leo Minor}}.

====Name origin====
Luxio may be a combination of ''{{wp|lux}}'' (Latin for light) and ''leo'' (Latin for lion).
