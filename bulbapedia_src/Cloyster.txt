'''Cloyster''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''パルシェン''' ''Parshen'') is a dual-type {{2t|Water|Ice}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation I]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Shellder}} when exposed to a [[Water Stone]].

==Biology==
Cloyster is a black {{wp|bivalve}} {{wikipedia|mollusk}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}}. It is encased inside a light-gray shell, which is surrounded by a second jagged, blue-violet outer shell casing. It has one barb-like spike protruding from above its head, and multiple conical horns on its outer shell. Its {{a|Shell Armor|shell}} is harder than a diamond with spikes that are even harder; harsh tidal currents will affect the size and sharpness of these spikes. On its pearl-like face, it has two white eyes and a mouth. Aside from its face and head, its internal anatomy has never been seen. Once it has shut itself in, it is impossible to open its shell from the outside. This impressive defense allows it to withstand anything from a bomb blast to even a missile strike.

Cloyster typically only opens its shell to attack an opponent. However, it is fully capable of retaliating against any enemy with its shell tightly closed. With rapid succession, it launches its spikes at the foe, keeping them repelled. The same projectile system it uses to launch its spikes is also used for swimming. It swallows seawater and then ejects it towards the rear, pushing it through with siphon-jet propulsion. Being the result of Shellder evolving through an [[Evolution stone]], Cloyster is rarely found in the wild, though they can be found on the {{DL|List of Pokémon by habitat|Sea Pokémon|sea floor}}, nestled among the rocks and coral; however, it will actively hunt on land for {{p|Slowpoke}} tails. Cloyster and its pre-evolved form, {{p|Shellder}}, are relatives of {{p|Gastrodon}}. {{p|Kingler}} is a natural predator of Cloyster.

In [[Generation I]], {{m|Clamp}} was its [[signature move]]. Additionally, prior to [[Generation V]], Cloyster and its pre-evolved form, Shellder, were the [[Signature Ability|only known Pokémon]] that could have {{a|Skill Link}} as an [[Ability]].

===Major appearances===
====[[Lorelei's Cloyster|Prima's Cloyster]]====
[[Lorelei|Prima]] was shown to own a Cloyster in ''[[EP099|The Mandarin Island Miss Match]]''. It battled {{Ash}} and {{AP|Pikachu}} and won.

====Other====
Cloyster debuted in ''[[EP036|The Bridge Bike Gang]]'', under the ownership of [[Tyra]]. Cloyster was used to battle {{an|Misty}}, who was going to use {{TP|Misty|Starmie}}. However, {{TP|Misty|Psyduck}} popped out of its {{i|Poké Ball}} instead, which resulted in Tyra winning the match.

In ''[[EP076|Fire and Ice]]'', [[Pete Pebbleman]] used a Cloyster during the [[Indigo Plateau Conference]]. Ash battled it with {{AP|Kingler}}, who was able to win. It reappeared in a flashback in ''[[EP080|Friends to the End]]'', an episode where another {{pkmn|Trainer}}'s Cloyster appeared during the closing ceremonies of the Indigo Plateau Conference.

In ''[[EP087|The Crystal Onix]]'', {{OBP|Mateo|EP087}} used a Cloyster to try and {{pkmn2|caught|catch}} the [[Crystal Onix]]. However, its {{m|Water Gun}} had no effect, and Cloyster was knocked out.

In ''[[HS18|The Search for the Legend]]'', [[Butch]] used a Cloyster during his and [[Cassidy]]'s attempt to catch a {{pkmn2|wild}} {{p|Moltres}}.

In ''[[DP077|Staging a Heroes' Welcome!]]'', {{si|Kyle}} used a Cloyster during the [[Appeal|Performance Stage]] of the [[Wallace Cup]]. It impressed the audience with its {{m|Spike Cannon}}.

In ''[[SM012|The Sun, the Scare, the Secret Lair!]]'', [[Jessie]] tried to catch a Cloyster without battling it, but it ate her Poké Ball. It splashed {{TRT}} before swimming away, spitting the Ball out on the way.

In ''[[JN102|Battling in the Freezing Raid!]]'', [[Goh]] caught a {{DL|List of Goh's Pokémon|Cloyster}} while exploring the [[Seafoam Islands]].

==Trivia==
* Cloyster is tied with {{me|Slowbro}} for the highest base {{stat|Defense}} stat of all {{type|Water}} Pokémon.
** Slowbro's tail has an attached Shellder, which grows to engulf it during [[Mega Evolution]]. This may have been an intended parallel.
* Cloyster and its pre-evolved form share their {{pkmn|category}} name with {{p|Clamperl}}. They are all known as the Bivalve Pokémon.

===Design variations===
In some early artwork, the shiny spot on Cloyster's head is depicted as a more defined shape, possibly intended as an embedded object such as a gemstone or pearl. This detail is prominent in its {{game|Red and Blue|s}} artwork, [[Bandai Pokémon Carddass Cards|Carddass]] card art, and early depictions in the [[Pokémon Adventures]] manga. Its original {{game|Red and Green|s}} artwork gives Cloyster's head the appearance of a shiny black pearl, with the sheen perhaps later being interpreted as a distinct feature rather than light reflecting off its surface. Later appearances, particularly from [[Generation III]] onwards, typically use a more subtle highlight or omit the detail entirely.

===Origin===
Cloyster appears to be based on a {{wp|Bivalvia|bivalve}} of the genus ''{{wp|Spondylus}}'' with a black {{wp|pearl}} as its head.

====Name origin====
Cloyster may be a combination of ''{{wp|clam}}'' and ''{{wp|oyster}}'', as well as a play on ''cloister'' (to seclude or shelter oneself).

Parshen may be a combination of パール ''pearl'' and シェル ''{{wp|seashell|shell}}''.
