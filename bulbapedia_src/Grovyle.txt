'''Grovyle''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''ジュプトル''' ''Juptile'') is a {{type|Grass}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation III]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Treecko}} starting at [[level]] 16 and evolves into {{p|Sceptile}} starting at level 36.

==Biology==
Grovyle is a bipedal {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} similar in appearance to a {{wp|Theropoda|theropod}} dinosaur. It is primarily green with a red underside. There is a belt-like green stripe across its belly. Grovyle's hands have two clawed fingers and powerful legs with birdlike feet. Its well-developed muscles make it an expert climber and allow it to leap quickly from branch to branch. Grovyle has three long leaves on its wrists, a large leaf on top of its head, and two tails that have a leafy appearance. This leafy appearance allows it to be camouflaged in the {{DL|List of Pokémon by habitat|Forest Pokémon|forests}} and dense jungles where it lives. As mentioned in [[Sleep style|Sleep Style Dex]], veteran researchers have difficulty finding the slumbering Grovyle due to its excellent camouflage. Grovyle has four different sleep styles: ''Knee-Up Sleep'', ''Dream-Stance Sleep'', ''Leaf-Tucked Sleep'', and ''Atop-Belly Sleep''.

In [[Generation III]], {{m|Leaf Blade}} was its [[signature move]].

===NPC appearances===
{{Spoilers}}
* [[Pokémon Mystery Dungeon: Explorers of Time and Explorers of Darkness|Pokémon Mystery Dungeon: Explorers of Time, Darkness]] and [[Pokémon Mystery Dungeon: Explorers of Sky|Sky]]: {{mdc|Grovyle|2}} is a major character in the main storyline. He travels to the present to prevent the collapse of [[Temporal Tower]] by stealing Time Gears, actions that make him appear villainous for most of the game. The {{player}} was his partner in the future. He also stars in the Special Episode [[In the Future of Darkness]].
* [[Pokémon Super Mystery Dungeon]]: The same Grovyle from Explorers of Time, Darkness, and Sky is recruitable via the Connection Orb. He requests assistance in rescuing {{mdc|Celebi|2}}.

==In animation==
===Main series===

====Major appearances====
=====[[Ash's Grovyle]]=====
{{Ash}}'s Treecko evolved into a Grovyle in ''[[AG066|Exploud and Clear]]''. In ''[[AG161|Odd Pokémon Out]]'', Grovyle evolved into a Sceptile.

=====[[Grovyle (Explorers of Time, Darkness, and Sky)]]=====
At the start of ''[[Pokémon Mystery Dungeon: Explorers of Time & Darkness]]'', a Grovyle stole one of the [[Time Gear]]s. He later had a much larger role in ''[[Pokémon Mystery Dungeon: Explorers of Sky - Beyond Time & Darkness]]''.

=====[[Sawyer's Grovyle]]=====
In ''[[XY075|Rivals: Today and Tomorrow!]]'', [[Sawyer]]'s Treecko evolved into Grovyle during a {{pkmn|battle}} against {{Ash}} and his {{AP|Frogadier}}. It was later revealed to have evolved into a Sceptile before ''[[XY106|A Meeting of Two Journeys!]]''.

=====Other=====
In ''[[AG041|What You Seed is What You Get]]'', [[Natasha]] used a Grovyle and a {{p|Slugma}} in a [[Double Battle]] against [[Ash's Corphish]] and Treecko. Working as a team, they were able to defeat Ash's Pokémon.

====Minor appearances====
In ''[[DP124|To Thine Own Pokémon Be True!]]'', a Trainer's Grovyle competed in the [[Pokémon Ping Pong Tournament]].

After a few more weeks of training at the [[Sky Pillar]] under [[Norman]], he was able to awaken the ancient legendary Pokémon {{p|Rayquaza}}, but the huge gust of wind sent him flying miles away into a distant island where he evolved again to a Sceptile and was this time caught by [[Guile Hideout]], who smuggled him into the {{OBP|Battle Factory|Generation III}} to try to drive the nosy press away. He ended up being stolen by his legitimate owner, Emerald, and became a fixed member of his team.

Emerald had borrowed a Grovyle during his Factory challenge in ''[[PS306|Pinsir Me, I Must Be Dreaming]]''; it should not be confused with his Sceptile. This Grovyle, like the other Factory Pokémon, is at [[level]] 50.

==Trivia==
* Grovyle was [[List of references to Pokémon in science|the inspiration for the naming]] of ''Anyphaena grovyle'', a species of spider. The species name was chosen in reference to both Grovyle and the discovered spider being found in the forest.<ref name="yejie lin">[https://web.archive.org/web/20230309124205/https://www.zootax.com.cn/CN/abstract/abstract296.shtml Twenty-three new spider species (Arachnida: Araneae) from Asia]</ref>

===Origin===
{{bulbanews|On the Origin of Species: Treecko, Grovyle and Sceptile}}
Like the other members of its evolutionary line, Grovyle's design shares some similarities with the {{wp|Uroplatus|leaf-tailed gecko}}, a species of {{wp|lizard}} that blends in with plantlike appendages. It also seems to be inspired by a {{wp|dinosaur}}, bearing significant resemblance to the ''{{wp|Dromaeosaurus}}''. It is believed that the dinosaur had feathers, much like how Grovyle has leaves on its body - the leaves on its tail are especially similar to the tails of {{wp|Epidexipteryx}}, which only had short tails that supported much larger feathers. Its design could be based on a ''{{wp|Protarchaeopteryx}}''. Its green skin and red underside seem to take inspiration from {{wp|Anolis carolinensis|male green anoles}}, whose dewlaps inflate and turn red to show dominance.

====Name origin====
Grovyle may be a combination of ''grove'' (a group of trees), ''reptile'', and possibly ''vine'' or ''ivy''.

Juptile may be a combination of 樹 ''ju'' (tree) and ''reptile'' or ''{{wp|Dromaeosauridae|raptor}}''.
