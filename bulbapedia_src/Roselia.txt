'''Roselia''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''ロゼリア''' ''Roselia'') is a dual-type {{2t|Grass|Poison}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation III]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Budew}} when [[friendship Evolution|leveled up with high friendship]] during the [[Time#Day|day]] and evolves into {{p|Roserade}} when exposed to a [[Shiny Stone]].

==Biology==
Roselia is a small, light green bipedal {{OBP|Pokémon|species}}. It has three thorns on top of its head, and it has black eyes with long eyelashes. Visible from the back is a small stretch of yellow coloration around its neck much like a collar. There are leaves extending from its shoulders like epaulettes. It has a leaf skirt with a yellow stripe running down the front. A male will have a shorter leaf than a female. It holds a rose in each of its hands, a red one in its right, and a blue one in its left. However, it is said that it will occasionally have unusually colored flowers.

Roselia's aroma brings serenity, but the thorns on its head contain a vicious poison. The more healthy it is, the more pleasant its flowers' aroma. It shoots sharp thorns at anything that tries to steal its flowers and uses different poisons in each hand when it attacks. It lives in {{DL|List of Pokémon by habitat|Grassland Pokémon|grassy areas}} where it drinks nutrient-rich spring water. Roselia's thorns are used as part of an old tradition, challenging an opponent to a duel by sending these thorns.

In [[Generation III]], {{m|Grass Whistle}} was its [[signature move]], but other Pokémon could still inherit it via {{pkmn|breeding}}.

Roselia evolves from {{p|Budew}} and evolves into {{p|Roserade}}.

==In animation==

===Major appearances===
====[[Drew's Roselia]]====
Roselia debuted in [[AG033|''Now'' That's ''Flower Power!'']], under the ownership of [[Drew]]. It is his signature Pokémon, and it has been used in many [[Pokémon Contest]]s. It evolved into a Roserade at some point between ''[[AG187|The Unbeatable Lightness of Seeing!]]'' and ''[[DP076|A Full Course Tag Battle!]]'', while Drew was in [[Johto]].

====[[Nando's Roselia]]====
[[Nando]] owned a Roselia, which evolved from his Budew during a {{pkmn|battle}} against {{Ash}} and his {{AP|Pikachu}} in ''[[DP004|Dawn of a New Era!]]''. It later evolved into Roserade at some point prior to ''[[DP176|Coming Full-Festival Circle!]]''.

====Other====
In ''[[AG046|Candid Camerupt]]'', [[Winstrate family|Victoria Winstrate]] used a Roselia in a battle against {{an|May}}.

In ''[[AG052|Cheer Pressure]]'', a Roselia was part of [[Sheridan]]'s cheering squad along with a {{p|Makuhita}}, {{p|Vileplume}}, and {{p|Shroomish}}. Sheridan later assigned it to heal [[James's Cacnea]] and [[Jessie's Seviper]] with {{m|Aromatherapy}}.

In ''[[BW125|Cilan and the Case of the Purrloin Witness!]]'', [[Mr. Shaw]] used a Roselia to put [[Mrs. Ripple]]'s {{p|Watchog}} asleep while its Trainer tried to take a look at the prize "Eye of Liepard" jewel.

In ''[[PK27|Pikachu and the Pokémon Music Squad]]'', a Roselia was living in a forest.

===Pokémon Adventures===
{{adv|Wally}} caught a Roselia on {{rt|117|Hoenn}} who first appeared in ''[[PS240|Talk About Timing, Treecko]]'', which still remains on his team to this day. Being fairly small, it typically rests on Wally's shoulder and heals Pokémon with its {{m|Aromatherapy}}.

Multiple Roselia appeared in ''[[PS367|Suffering Psyduck]]''.

A wild Roselia appeared in the [[Wild Area]] in ''[[PASS04|Zap!! A Rising Beam of Light]]''.

===Pokémon Diamond and Pearl Adventure!===
A {{pkmn|Trainer}}'s Roselia appeared in ''[[DPA06|Beauty Contest: The Pokémon Super Contest!!]]'', where it was seen participating in a [[Pokémon Super Contest]].

==Trivia==
* Roselia is the only Pokémon to receive both a pre-evolved form and an evolved form in the same [[generation]] outside of its debut generation.
* Roselia and its evolved form are the only {{type|Poison}} Pokémon in the {{egg3|Fairy}}.
* In 2007, employees of Nintendo of America put {{DL|List of GTS event Pokémon|Heracross}} up on the [[Global Terminal|Global Trade Station]] for a Japanese Roselia.
* Roselia is one of fourteen Pokémon that one can collect [[foreign Pokédex entries]] for in {{game|Diamond and Pearl|s}}.

===Origin===
Roselia may be based on {{wp|rose}} bushes. Its humanoid design, connection to flowers, and one of its [[Egg Group]]s suggest that it may also be based on {{wp|fairy|fairies}}. While red and blue are known rose colors, these two specifically together could be referencing {{wp|hydrangea}}s, which produce red or blue flowers depending on the {{wp|soil pH|pH level of the soil}}.

====Name origin====
Roselia may be a combination of ''{{wp|rose}}'', ''{{wp|azalea}}'', and ''{{wp|camellia}}''.
