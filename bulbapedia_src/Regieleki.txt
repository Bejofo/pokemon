'''Regieleki''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''レジエレキ''' ''Regieleki'') is an {{type|Electric}} [[Legendary Pokémon]] introduced during [[Generation VIII]], in [[The Crown Tundra]].

It is not known to [[Evolution|evolve]] into or from any other Pokémon.

It is a member of the [[legendary giants]] along with {{p|Regirock}}, {{p|Regice}}, {{p|Registeel}}, and {{p|Regidrago}}.

==Biology==
[[File:RegielekiVSilverTempest57.jpg|thumb|left|150px|Regieleki opening its arms]]
Regieleki is an electric golem-like {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} made mostly of electric energy generated by a single organ. It has a round yellow body with three spikes on top of its head, and a pattern of seven pink dots on its face that resembles the letter "X". Regieleki's arms are shaped like lightning and usually appear bunched together, while its legs are tiny and resemble both lightning bolts and an electrical plug. Its body can glow bright yellow when attacking. Regieleki has five blue rings on its body, with two on the base of its arms acting as its shoulders, and the other three located at the bottom connecting to its legs. 

According to legend, Regieleki was created by the [[Legendary Pokémon]] {{p|Regigigas}} using pure electric energy. It used to be worshipped by [[human]]s, however, at some point, ancient people started fearing its power and sealed it away in the [[Split-Decision Ruins]]. The blue rings are insulated and cannot conduct electricity, they've been put on it by ancient people who were tormented by it, in order to restrain its powers.<ref name="SwShsite">[https://swordshield.pokemon.com/en-us/expansionpass/pokemon/regieleki/ Newly discovered Legendary Pokémon Regieleki | Official Website | Pokémon Sword and Pokémon Shield]</ref>. It is said that if the rings on its body were to be removed, Regieleki would be able to unleash its latent powers. Regieleki absorbs electrons in order to stay alive and is said to have the greatest power of any {{type|Electric}} Pokémon,<ref name="SwShsite" /> being powerful enough to generate all of [[Galar]]'s electricity. It can trap its foes inside {{m|Thunder Cage|a cage of sparking electricity}} that inflicts damage over time.

Regieleki is the [[Signature move|only known Pokémon]] capable of learning the move {{m|Thunder Cage}}. It is also the [[Signature Ability|only known Pokémon]] that can have {{a|Transistor}} as an [[Ability]].

==Trivia==
*Regieleki has the highest base {{stat|Speed}} stat of all {{OBP|Pokémon|species}}.
* Along with {{p|Regidrago}}, Regieleki is the only [[Legendary Pokémon]] introduced in [[Generation VIII]] that is not prevented from being {{Shiny}} in {{g|Sword and Shield}}.
* Regieleki is the smallest and lightest of the [[legendary giants]], at 3'11" (1.2 m) and 319.7 lbs. (145.0 kg).
*Regieleki is the only member of the legendary giants that cannot learn {{m|Hammer Arm}} by leveling up. Instead, it learns {{m|Thunderbolt}}.
*Regieleki's base {{stat|HP}} and Speed stats are the reverse of Regidrago's.

===Origin===
Regieleki appears to be based on the concept of an electric charge being restrained by electrical insulation. Its overall shape resembles an {{wp|incandescent light bulb}}, its restraints resemble electrical coils, its arms resemble {{wp|electric arc}}s, and its legs resemble a {{wp|AC power plugs and sockets|power plug}}. As an electric generator found in ancient ruins, its inspiration may also be drawn from an {{wp|out-of-place artifact}} (OOPART), such as the {{wp|Baghdad Battery}}.

Along with the other [[legendary giants]], Regieleki may also be based on the golems of Hebrew legend. The legends cast them as servants of higher powers and are said to have writing on their heads. When the writing is removed, the creature would be weakened, or even killed in some legends. Regieleki's face, which consists of seven dots, may be based on the {{wp|Braille alphabet}} which was used in the puzzle for encountering the legendary giants for the first time in the [[Sealed Chamber]].

The five legendary giants may also collectively be themed after historical epochs, with Regieleki representing the {{wp|Modern Age}}.

====Name origin====
Regieleki may be a combination of ''regis'' (Latin for royal) and エレキ ''ereki'' (Japanese for electric).
