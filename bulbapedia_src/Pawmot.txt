'''Pawmot''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''パーモット''' ''Parmot'') is a dual-type {{2t|Electric|Fighting}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation IX]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Pawmo}} when leveled up while outside of its [[Poké Ball]] after the player walks 1000 [[step counting|steps]] using the [[Let's Go!]] feature.<ref>https://twitter.com/Sibuna_Switch/status/1678027317891694593</ref><ref>https://twitter.com/Sibuna_Switch/status/1680044846403272704</ref> It is the final form of {{p|Pawmi}}.

==Biology==
[[File:Masters EX Nemona Debut Artwork.png|thumb|left|150px|Pawmot's forepaws discharging electricity]]
Pawmot is a stout, bipedal, rodent-like Pokémon that has a body that is almost entirely orange, with tan colorations on its forepaws, snout, and long, fluffy tail, and green on the insides of its ears. Its face has beady brown eyes, a tiny nose, and yellow electric sacs on its cheeks. It has shaggy tufts of fur on its forehead and near its ears, as well as on its neck which forms a collar. Pawmot has five fingers on its considerably large forelimbs, which stretch down to its tiny feet. The colorations on its forelimbs create a zigzag pattern. It has yellow pads on its forepaws which turn teal-blue when it uses electric attacks.

Although Pawmot's reaction speed is normally low, it is able to strike opponents with fast movements when battling. The fluffy fur on its body acts like a battery, allowing Pawmot to store as much electricity as in an electric car. As mentioned in [[Pokémon Sleep]], Pawmot has a peaceful personality. On occasion, Pawmot will heal injured Pokémon by discharging electricity from its forepaws. If Pawmot raises its paws while it sleeps, it is possibly dreaming of tending to other Pokémon.

Pawmot is the [[signature move|only known Pokémon]] capable of learning the move {{m|Double Shock}}.

===Concept and development===
Pawmot and its pre-Evolutions were designed by [[Atsuko Nishida]],<ref>[https://www.instagram.com/coloatsu/p/DDCg3wKPJDR/ Instagram post by Atsuko Nishida (@coloatsu) on the Paldean Pokémon designed by her.] Posted on December 1, 2024.</ref> who was responsible for designing {{p|Pikachu}} and {{p|Raichu}}.

===Origin===
Pawmot seems to be based on a {{wp|marmot}}, such as the {{wp|alpine marmot}}, which was reintroduced in 1948 to the {{wp|Pyrenees}} mountain range at the border of Spain and France, after its disappearance in the region following the end of the {{wp|Pleistocene}} epoch. Pawmot's ability to discharge electricity from its paws may be inspired by {{wp|Defibrillation|defibrillator}}s. Both aspects may attribute to its ability to learn {{m|Revival Blessing}}.

====Name origin====
Pawmot may be a combination of ''{{p|Pawmi}}'' or ''{{p|Pawmo}}'' and ''marmot''.

Parmot may be a combination of パモ ''Pamo'' (Pawmi) or パモット ''Pamot'' (Pawmo) and ''marmot''.
