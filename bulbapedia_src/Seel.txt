'''Seel''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''パウワウ''' ''Pawou'') is a {{type|Water}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation I]].

It [[Evolution|evolve]]s into {{p|Dewgong}} starting at [[level]] 34.

==Biology==
Seel is a {{wp|pinniped}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} with a thick hide covered in bluish-white fur. It has a pale tan canine maw with a large red tongue and a pair of tusks on its lower jaw. It has circular eyes, prominent black eyebrows (it’s one of the few Pokémon that has eyebrows) and a black nose that is capable of closing when it swims. There is a small horn on its forehead, which it uses in combination with its tusks to bore through ice floes. It has two flippers on its chest, and two more on the end of its tail. When the tail is raised, it resembles a waterspout.

Seel lives on icebergs in {{DL|List of Pokémon by habitat|Sea Pokémon|frigid ocean waters.}} Its warm fur and thick hide allow it to swim in waters as cold as -40° Fahrenheit (-40° Celsius). It thrives in these cold environments, becoming more active as the temperature drops and tires easily in warm waters. Preferring to sleep during the day, it hunts for prey under sheets of ice in the late day and evening hours. While it is a graceful swimmer, it is awkward and struggles to move on dry land.

===Evolution===
Seel evolves into {{p|Dewgong}}.

===Major appearances===
====[[Seel (Cerulean Gym)]]====
In ''[[EP007|The Water Flowers of Cerulean City]]'', multiple Seel are kept by {{an|Misty}} and [[Sensational Sisters|her sisters]] at the [[Cerulean Gym]]. One evolved into a Dewgong in ''[[EP061|The Misty Mermaid]]''.

====Other====
In ''[[EP103|Misty Meets Her Match]]'', a Seel was caught in a whirlpool alongside its {{pkmn|Trainer}}, named [[Mahri]], but were rescued by Misty and her {{TP|Misty|Staryu}}.

==In the manga==
[[File:Pryce Seel Adventures.png|thumb|200px|Seel in [[Pokémon Adventures]]]]
===Pokémon Adventures===
Seel is the first Pokémon [[Lorelei]] owned, which debuted in ''[[PS067|Can You Diglett?]]''. As a child, Lorelei saw her Seel become {{status|poison}}ed due to the effects of nearby industrialization. [[Agatha]], who was passing by, healed Seel using her {{p|Gastly}}. This encounter with the negative effects that humanity had on Pokémon would be the motivation Lorelei had to join the [[Elite Four]] in a plot to destroy all of humanity years later.

[[Pryce]] owns two Seel that assist him in sculpting ice. They first appeared in ''[[PS143|Irked Igglybuff and Curmudgeonly Cleffa]]''.

A Seel appeared in ''[[PS179|The Last Battle XIII]]'' as one of the Pokémon sent to participate in the fight in [[Ilex Forest]].

A Seel liberated by [[Team Plasma]] appeared in ''[[PS547|The Final Battle]]'' as one of the Pokémon stuck in [[N's Castle]].

===Pokémon Gold & Silver: The Golden Boys===
A {{pkmn|Trainer}}'s Seel appeared in ''[[GB23|Let's Fight For The Future!!]]''.

===Pokémon Pocket Monsters===

==Trivia==
* Seel shares its {{pkmn|category}} with {{p|Dewgong}} and {{p|Popplio}}. They are all known as the Sea Lion Pokémon.
* Seel is the only non-{{type|Ice}} Pokémon that can have {{a|Ice Body}} as its [[Ability]]. Consequently, Seel is the only Pokémon with Ice Body to benefit from its {{weather|hail}} immunity, since all other Pokémon with the Ability are naturally immune to hail damage due to their typing.

===Origin===
Seel may be based on an {{wp|earless seal}}, particularly a {{wp|harp seal}} pup due to its white fur and icy habitat. It also shares characteristics with other {{wp|aquatic mammal}}s that live in the {{wp|Arctic Ocean}}. Its long, white body is similar to a {{wp|beluga whale}}, its horn may be inspired by a {{wp|narwhal}}, and its tusks may be inspired by a {{wp|walrus}}. The shape of its tail resembles a stylized {{wp|waterspout}} or {{wp|geyser}}.

====Name origin====
Seel is derived from ''{{wp|Pinniped|seal}}''.

Pawou may be derived from an onomatopoeia of a seal's bark.
