'''Granbull''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''グランブル''' ''Granbulu'') is a {{type|Fairy}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation II]]. Prior to [[Generation VI]], it was a {{type|Normal}} Pokémon.

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Snubbull}} starting at [[level]] 23.

==Biology==
Granbull is a stout, purplish-pink, bipedal, {{wp|bulldog}}-like {{OBP|Pokémon|species}}. It has a prominent lower jaw with two long, fang-like growths. Its upper jaw has two small fangs. The jaws are so heavy that the Pokémon has trouble keeping its head up. On the sides of its head are flat, curved ears, and its small nose is black. There is a black collar around its neck with two small white markings on it. Around its wrists are two black band markings, and it has a short tail.

A Granbull's main weapons are its well-developed lower jaw and heavy fangs. The bite from its jaws is said to be very powerful. However, due to how heavy its jaws are, Granbull keeps its head tilted down constantly for balance. Despite its fierce appearance, it is actually very timid and sensitive, even more so than {{p|Snubbull}}. Granbull rarely bites as it prefers not to get involved in disputes. Unless Granbull is startled, it will not bite indiscriminately. If attacked, it will flail about to fend off its attacker. Granbull is popular with young people due to how it acts and looks. It is considered too incompetent to be a guard dog due to its nature. Granbull wanders around {{DL|List of Pokémon by habitat|Urban Pokémon|urban areas}}.

===Main series===
====Major appearances====
=====[[Madame Muchmoney's Granbull]]=====
Granbull made her animated series debut in ''[[EP175|The Trouble with Snubbull]]'', where she evolved from the {{p|Snubbull}} that ran away from her home and followed {{TRT}} (and, by extension, {{Ash}} and {{ashfr}}) around the [[Johto]] [[region]] for a short while. She was then returned to her owner, [[Madame Muchmoney]].

=====Other=====
In ''[[DP065|Sleight of Sand!]]'', [[Cassidy]]'s Granbull battled [[Jessie's Seviper]] and was then defeated by [[Dawn's Piplup]] and a wild {{OBP|Hippopotas|recurring}}.

In ''[[DP146|Dressed for Jess Success!]]'', [[Mario]] used a Granbull during the {{to|Lilypad}} {{pkmn|Contest}}. It went up against {{an|Dawn}} and {{TP|Dawn|Mamoswine}}, and it was soon defeated.

In ''[[SM070|The Young Flame Strikes Back!]]'', the [[Officer Jenny]] from [[Akala Island]] owns a Granbull. It prevented [[Viren]] and [[Viren's henchmen|his henchmen]] from escaping after they arrived to arrest them for attempting to destroy the [[Paniola Ranch]].

====Minor appearances====
Granbull debuted in ''[[M03|Spell of the Unown: Entei]]'', under the ownership of {{OBP|Lisa|M03}}. She used it during her battle against {{Ash}}, only for it to be defeated by {{AP|Totodile}}.

In ''[[EP194|Ring Masters]]'', two Granbull competed in [[Rikishii Town]]'s [[Sumo Conference]].

A Granbull appeared in ''[[PK10|Pikachu's PikaBoo]]''.

A Granbull appeared in ''[[EP228|Extreme Pokémon!]]'', under the ownership of a participant in the [[Extreme Pokémon Race]].

A {{pkmn|Coordinator}}'s Granbull appeared in ''[[AG160|Harley Rides Again]]''.

In ''[[DP051|Glory Blaze!]]'', a {{pkmn|Trainer}}'s Granbull competed in the [[Hearthome City Tag Battle Competition]].

In ''[[DP189|The Semi-Final Frontier!]]'', a Trainer's Granbull went up against {{si|Tobias}} during the [[Lily of the Valley Conference]] finals. It battled his {{p|Darkrai}} off-screen, but as with the other five Pokémon owned by its Trainer, it was easily defeated by the {{pkmn|category|Pitch-Black Pokémon}}.

Two Trainers' Granbull appeared in ''[[M21|The Power of Us]]''.

In ''[[SM105|Showdown on Poni Island!]]'', {{an|Lillie}} photographed a {{pkmn2|wild}}, sleeping Granbull during her time on [[Poni Island]].

A Granbull appeared in ''[[JN110|Narrowing the Chaser Chase!]]'', under the ownership of a [[Project Mew]] Challenger.

==Trivia==
* Granbull and its pre-evolved form [[List of categories by abundance|share]] their {{pkmn|category}} with {{p|Clefairy}} and {{p|Clefable}}. They are all known as the Fairy Pokémon.
** The "Fairy Pokémon" category predates the introduction of the {{type|Fairy}} in [[Generation VI]]. From this generation onwards, the type and category of these four Pokémon share the same English name. However, this is not true in Japanese, where the type is フェアリータイプ ''Fairy-type'' and the category is ようせいポケモン ''Yōsei Pokémon''.
* As the [[Ability]] {{a|Quick Feet}} did not exist until [[Generation IV]], a Snubbull with either {{a|Intimidate}} or {{a|Run Away}} will always evolve into a Granbull with Intimidate in [[Generation III]] games.<!--If a Granbull with Intimidate that has second Ability flag is transferred to the Generation VI games, its Ability will become Quick Feet?--> This means that in Generation III, Granbull is the only Pokémon with an Ability that evolves from a Pokémon with one of two possible Abilities that shares to Granbull's Ability.

===Origin===
Granbull may be based on the {{wp|bulldog}}, a dog breed that can appear fierce but is actually gentle. Bulldogs are often depicted as being used as {{wp|guard dog}}s, which may explain Granbull's thick collar and wristband/shackle-like markings. Due to its large size, it is possible that Granbull may be based on large bulldog breeds, like the {{wp|Serrano Bulldog}}, {{wp|Ca de Bou}}, and {{wp|Alano Español}}.

Its {{pkmn|category}}, [[Fairy (Egg Group)|Egg Group]], and {{t|Fairy|type}} may reference the {{wp|Cù-sìth}}, a creature in {{wp|Celtic mythology}} whose name means ''dog fairy''. Alternatively, while its intimidating appearance could be reminiscent of various grotesque mythical creatures such as {{wp|goblin}}s and {{wp|orc}}s, it might be inspired specifically by {{wp|kobold}}s, sprites that are commonly represented as dog-like in Japanese media.

====Name origin====
Granbull and Granbulu may be a combination of ''grand'', ''grumble'', and ''bulldog'' or ''bully''.
