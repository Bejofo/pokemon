'''Carkol''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''トロッゴン''' ''Toroggon'') is a dual-type {{2t|Rock|Fire}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation VIII]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Rolycoly}} starting at [[level]] 18 and evolves into {{p|Coalossal}} starting at level 34.

==Biology==
Carkol is a {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} which resembles a minecart. It has a head with two triangular eyes and a mouth that has a warm, orange glow. Behind its head is a small heap of coal which can glow orange-red, atop a body of black stone. It has four wheel-like legs which it moves on.

Carkol forms coal and coal tar inside of its body. Although it frequently drops its coal, the heap on Carkol's back never falls apart completely even when it travels at high speeds, due to the tar binding it together. People in the [[Galar]] [[region]] once used Carkol's coal to fuel their lives. Carkol can move faster than 18 mph (30 km/h) and can breathe flames hotter than 1,800 degrees Fahrenheit (1,000 degrees Celsius). When Carkol experiences strong emotions, the temperature inside its body increases.

Carkol and its evolutionary relatives are the [[Signature Ability|only known Pokémon]] that can have {{a|Steam Engine}} as an [[Ability]].

According to [[Professor Friede's Pokémon Seminar]], the supply of coal that Carkol produces is infinite.

Carkol evolves from {{p|Rolycoly}} and evolves into {{p|Coalossal}}.

==Trivia==
* Carkol and its evolutionary relatives share their {{pkmn|category}} with {{p|Torkoal}}. They are all known as the Coal Pokémon.
* Carkol and its evolutionary relatives have several unique attributes:
** They are the only {{DL|List of Pokémon by color|black}} {{type|Rock}} Pokémon.
** Carkol and {{p|Coalossal}} are the only {{type|Fire}} Pokémon in the {{egg3|Mineral}}.
** Carkol and Coalossal are the only dual-type Fire/Rock Pokémon to have Rock as their primary type instead of their secondary type.

===Origin===
Carkol appears to be based on a mine cart filled with coal. Its {{Shiny}} variant's gray coloration may reference how coal turns white with ash as it burns.

====Name origin====
Carkol may be a combination of ''car'', ''cart'', ''carbon'', and ''coal'' or ''charcoal''.

Toroggon may be a combination of トロッコ ''torokko'' (minecart) and ''wagon''.
