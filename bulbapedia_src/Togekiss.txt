'''Togekiss''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''トゲキッス''' ''Togekiss'') is a dual-type {{2t|Fairy|Flying}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation IV]]. Prior to [[Generation VI]], it was a dual-type {{2t|Normal|Flying}} Pokémon.

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Togetic}} when exposed to a [[Shiny Stone]]. It is the final form of {{p|Togepi}}.

==Biology==
Togekiss is a white, avian {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} with an ovoid body. While its feet are small and situated closely together, its wings are broad and triangular. Red and blue triangular markings over its underside, and it has a short tail consisting of three feathers. On the back of its head are three spikes: a red-tipped one on the right, a blue-tipped one on the left, and a pure white one in the middle. Togekiss has a relatively flat face and small, black eyes.

Togekiss have long been known worldwide as bringers of blessings, being depicted on good-luck charms since ancient times. It is said that Togekiss brings good luck simply by virtue of its existence. However, they only appear in peaceful places and have never been witnessed anywhere near conflict or turmoil, making their recent sightings exceptionally rare. It shares many kind gifts and sweet blessings when it visits peaceful places or sees people who avoid strife and respect the rights of others. However, Togekiss is a capable fighter, and can use {{m|Aura Sphere|a powerful energy wave}} in battle. As mentioned in the [[Sleep style|Sleep Style Dex]], seeing several Togekiss sleeping contently is a sign that peace reigns in the area.

===Evolution===
Togekiss evolves from {{p|Togetic}} and is the final form of {{p|Togepi}}.

==In animation==

===Major appearances===
====[[Dawn's Togekiss]]====
A female Togekiss made its animated series debut in ''[[DP171|Dawn of a Royal Day!]]'', originally under the ownership of [[Princess Salvia]]. Salvia wanted to enter a [[Pokémon Contest]] but could not due to her royal obligations. Therefore, she swapped places with {{an|Dawn}} for a day and entered the {{to|Arrowroot}} Contest with Togekiss. After winning, she gave Togekiss to Dawn as a {{pkmn2|gift}} as she felt the Jubilee Pokémon would be better off with a traveling {{pkmn|Coordinator}}.

====Other====
In ''[[JN123|Bewitch, Battle, and Bewilder!]]'', {{an|Cynthia}} used her Togekiss in her [[Masters Eight Tournament]] match against {{Ash}}, where it battled against {{AP|Pikachu}} before being recalled. In ''[[JN124|Valor: A Strategic Part of Battling!]]'', it was used to battle against {{AP|Lucario}} and was [[Dynamax]]ed, only to wound up getting defeated in [[JN125|the next episode]].

===Minor appearances===
Togekiss debuted in the opening sequence of ''[[M11|Giratina and the Sky Warrior]]''.

A Togekiss appeared in ''[[HZ089|Where the Adventure Leads]]'' as one of the Pokémon fleeing from [[Laqua]].

==Trivia==
* Togekiss and its pre-evolved form are the only Pokémon that are in the {{egg|Flying}} and {{egg|Fairy}} [[Egg Group]]s. They are also the only {{type|Fairy}} Pokémon in the Flying Egg Group.
* Togekiss is the only Pokémon that has a [[List of Pokémon by gender ratio|gender ratio]] of 7:1 that have had a female [[Dawn's Togekiss|shown in the animated series]] but not a male.
* In [[Pokkén Tournament]], Togekiss is the only Support Pokémon with a charge time of "Normal".
* Togekiss, alongside its pre-evolved form {{p|Togetic}}, is one of only two Pokémon in {{g|Legends: Arceus}} whose "Number defeated" {{OBP|research task|Legends: Arceus}} maxes out at 1. This is most likely because the only time a Togekiss can be battled in the base game (without [[Path of Solitude]] or [[massive mass outbreaks]]) is during the final battle with [[Volo]].

===Origin===
Togekiss appears to be based on {{wp|fairy|fairies}} and {{wp|bird}}s. Its white color and association with peace suggest inspiration from {{wp|Doves as symbols|doves}}, while its triangular belly markings are similar to a {{wp|snowy owl}}. Its reputation for sharing blessings is reminiscent of {{wp|angel}}s. Like its pre-Evolutions, its round body resembles an {{wp|bird egg|egg}}. Its rigid, triangular wings are also reminiscent of a {{wp|flying wing}}. Togekiss may also be based on the Japanese interpretation of the Hōō, also known as the {{wp|fenghuang}} or Chinese phoenix. This is due to it's heavy associations with peace and unwillingness to appear in any areas with strife or conflict, as well as its avian nature.

====Name origin====
Togekiss may be a combination of 刺 ''toge'' (spike) and ''kiss'', referencing its kind nature.
