'''Sealeo''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''トドグラー''' ''Todoggler'') is a dual-type {{2t|Ice|Water}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation III]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Spheal}} starting at [[level]] 32 and evolves into {{p|Walrein}} starting at level 44.

==Biology==
Sealeo is a bright-blue, pinniped {{OBP|Pokémon|species}}. Its throat and chest are beige with speckling around the edges. There are two short tusks protruding from its upper jaw, and it has dark brown, oval eyes. It has a powerful pair of front flippers, which are able to crush ice. Additionally, it has a short, flipper-like tail. On the sides of its blunt muzzle are thick, white whiskers. Sealeo's white whiskers are described as being super sensitive.

When a Sealeo encounters new objects, they are placed on its nose and rolled around to test its smell and texture. The reason is to determine if it likes the object or not. As mentioned in the [[Sleep style|Sleep Style Dex]], Sealeo can sense the environment even when it sleeps. It has also been known to roll {{p|Spheal}} or [[Poké Ball]]s on its nose. The reason Sealeo does this to Spheal is to check its scent and ensure it is still healthy. This Pokémon lives in the {{DL|List of Pokémon by habitat|Sea Pokémon|sea}} and on drift ice. Sealeo dives underwater to hunt for prey five times a day. It searches for the prey by its scent. 

Prior to [[Generation VI]], {{m|Ice Ball}} was its [[signature move]], but other Pokémon could still inherit it via {{pkmn|breeding}}.

Sealeo evolves from {{p|Spheal}} and evolves into {{p|Walrein}}.

===NPC appearances===
* {{g|Pinball: Ruby & Sapphire}}: Sealeo stars in the Bonus Field {{p|Whiscash}} or {{p|Pelipper}} takes the player to. They will bounce the Poké Ball or a {{p|Spheal}} into the basket to earn the points of the round. Hitting a Sealeo while it is bouncing a Spheal will cause it to drop the Pokémon and miss out on the points for it.

==In animation==
===Main series===

====Major appearances====
Sealeo debuted alongside its pre-Evolution at the [[Oceanic Museum]] in [[Slateport City]] in ''[[AG036|The Spheal of Approval]]''. It was owned by [[Captain Stern]], who sent it out to battle [[Team Magma]] and their three {{p|Mightyena}}.

In ''[[AG110|The Great Eight Fate!]]'', {{ci|Sootopolis}} [[Gym Leader]] [[Juan]] used a Sealeo in his [[Gym]] {{pkmn|battle}} against {{Ash}}. Sealeo battled against {{AP|Snorunt}} and {{AP|Pikachu}} alongside {{p|Seaking}} and managed to defeat {{p|Snorunt}} but were defeated by {{p|Pikachu}} and {{AP|Corphish}}.

In ''[[DP089|Up Close and Personable!]]'', [[Angie]] stumbled across a Sealeo at the lake near the [[Pokémon Summer Academy]] with Ash, and she decided to sketch it.

====Minor appearances====
In ''[[M07|Destiny Deoxys]]'', multiple Sealeo were startled by the crash-landing of an asteroid containing two {{OBP|Deoxys|M07}} and fled, traumatizing [[Tory Lund]] in the process.

A {{pkmn|Coordinator}}'s Sealeo appeared in ''[[AG122|Deceit and Assist]]''.

In ''[[DP026|Getting the Pre-Contest Titters!]]'' and ''[[DP027|Settling a Not-So-Old Score!]]'', a Coordinator's Sealeo competed in the {{to|Floaroma}} {{pkmn|Contest}}.

A Sealeo appeared in ''[[M11|Giratina and the Sky Warrior]]''.

In [[PK19]], a Sealeo was living on the icy island.

A Sealeo appeared in a flashback in ''[[SM125|A Timeless Encounter!]]'', where it was seen in a picture.

==Trivia==
===Origin===
Sealeo appears to be based on an {{wp|earless seal}} with elements of a {{wp|walrus}}. Sealeo's skill of balancing and rolling Spheal on its nose may be a reference to the {{wp|Pinniped#Intelligence|common trick}} performed by {{wp|Sea lion|sea lions}} with balls.

====Name origin====
Sealeo may be a combination of ''sea'' or ''seal'' and ''leo'' (Latin for "lion"), in reference to ''sea lion''.

Todoggler may be a combination of 胡獱 ''todo'' ({{wp|Steller sea lion}}) and ''juggler''.
