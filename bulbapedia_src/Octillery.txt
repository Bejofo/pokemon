'''Octillery''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''オクタン''' ''Okutank'') is a {{type|Water}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation II]].

It [[Evolution|evolve]]s from {{p|Remoraid}} starting at [[level]] 25.

==Biology==
Octillery is a red, {{wp|cephalopod}}-like {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} with large, round eyes and a turret-like mouth. There are yellow bumps on its rock-hard head and yellow suction cups on each of its eight tentacles. These suction cups and yellow bumps are smaller on a female Octillery. It uses its tentacles for manipulating objects and the suction cups give it strong gripping power. Because of this, it is very hard for it to be blown away.

Octillery is a relaxed Pokémon who loves fuss from its Trainer. Even though it is more docile when domesticated, it can be very stubborn at times in the wild. It instinctively sneaks into rocky holes or pots, and if it gets sleepy, it steals the nest of a fellow Octillery. When threatened, it will spurt ink from its mouth to cover itself before escaping, allowing it to protect itself and its young from danger. Octillery's ink obscures vision and contains substances that dull predators' sense of smell. Octillery also uses this ink on its prey, before its suction cups hold the prey tightly. Its ink can also be used as a cooking ingredient. Besides ink, Octillery sometimes uses its rock-hard head on its foes. Prior to [[Generation VIII]], {{m|Octazooka}} was its [[signature move]], but other Pokémon could still inherit it via {{pkmn|breeding}}.

Octillery is normally found in {{DL|List of Pokémon by habitat|Sea Pokémon|oceans}} and caves. For a long time, people refused to believe Octillery was an [[Evolution]] of {{p|Remoraid}}, because of the extreme physical differences between the two. It is thought to be a distant relative to {{p|Omastar}}. In [[New Pokémon Snap]], an Octillery can be seen about to be preyed on by a {{p|Seviper}}.

===NPC appearances===
* [[Pokémon Mystery Dungeon: Red Rescue Team and Blue Rescue Team]]: Octillery is the leader of Team Constrictor, a [[Rank (Mystery Dungeon)|Silver Rank]] rescue team. It joins a new team with {{p|Blastoise}} and {{p|Golem}} designed to travel through [[Magma Cavern]], but is defeated.

===Major appearances===
Octillery debuted in ''[[EP215|Octillery The Outcast]]'', under the ownership of [[Marcellus]]. It evolved from one of his Remoraid, a task that Marcellus had been spending the majority of the episode trying to do.

In ''[[AG160|Harley Rides Again]]'', [[Harley]]'s Octillery managed to defeat [[May's Munchlax]] in a [[Contest Battle]] during the {{to|Wisteria}} {{pkmn|Contest}}. In ''[[AG181|Thinning the Hoard!]]'', it was used during the main [[Appeal|Appeals Round]] of the [[Kanto Grand Festival]].

In ''[[BW129|The Pirates of Decolore!]]'', an Octillery abandoned by its Trainer was a powerful member of {{p|Croconaw}}'s pirate gang, and it would often battle any Pokémon it came across. It eventually joined [[Officer Jenny]]'s new squad.

===Minor appearances===
In ''[[EP272|Johto Photo Finish]]'', {{Gary}}'s demonstration featured an Octillery evolving from a Remoraid.

In ''[[AG021|Which Wurmple's Which?]]'', {{Ash}} and {{ashfr}} [[Fishing|fished]] out an Octillery, and it landed on [[Max]]'s head.

In ''[[AG191|Once More With Reeling!]]'', a {{pkmn|Coordinator}}'s Octillery competed in the {{to|Terracotta}} {{pkmn|Contest}}.

An Octillery appeared in ''[[DP149|Sticking With Who You Know!]]''.

An Octillery appeared in ''[[XY024|An Undersea Place to Call Home!]]''.

In ''[[SM117|Drawn with the Wind!]]'', an Octillery was running a {{wp|takoyaki}} vendor in [[Malie City]].

In ''[[SM120|The One That Didn't Get Away!]]'', {{TP|Kiawe|Turtonator}} fished out an Octillery.

In ''[[SM129|Battle Royal 151!]]'', a {{pkmn|Trainer}}'s Octillery competed in the [[Battle Royal]] preliminary round of the [[Manalo Conference]] but ended up losing.

Two Octillery appeared in ''[[JN008|The Sinnoh Iceberg Race!]]'', with one under the ownership of a Trainer and the other being wild.

In ''[[JN036|Making Battles in the Sand!]]'', Ash battled a Trainer's Octillery with his {{AP|Riolu}} in a [[World Coronation Series]] match, only to be defeated.

In ''[[JN063|On Land, In the Sea, and to the Future!]]'', a Trainer's Octillery competed in the [[Marine Athletic Race]].

An Octillery appeared in ''[[HZ031|Song Within the Mist]]'' and ''[[HZ032|Lapras's Feelings for its Friends]]'', as part of {{TP|Lucius|Lapras}}'s crew.

===Pokémon Adventures===
Octillery debuted in ''[[PS101|Teddiursa's Picnic]]'', under the ownership of [[Wilton]], where it was in a {{i|Poké Ball}} that was stuck inside the mouth of a {{pkmn2|wild}} {{p|Granbull}}.

An Octillery appeared in ''[[PS128|Indubitably Ditto]]'', under the control of [[Team Rocket Elite Trio|Ken]]. In ''[[PS127|Miltank Melee]]'', it was used in an attempt to capture {{DL|Legendary beasts (Adventures)|Suicune}}. It was put to sleep temporarily by {{adv|Crystal}}'s {{p|Parasect|Parasee}}'s {{m|Spore}} in ''[[PS128|Indubitably Ditto]]'', but later woke up.

An Octillery appeared in ''[[PS179|The Last Battle XIII]]'' as one of the Pokémon sent to participate in the fight in [[Ilex Forest]].

In ''[[PS387|Outwitting Octillery]]'', [[Saturn]] used an Octillery to fend off {{adv|Pearl}} during a mission at [[Lake Verity]].

A {{pkmn|Trainer}}'s Octillery appeared in ''[[PS446|Weavile Wobbles But It Won't Fall Down]]'' as one of the Pokémon seen at the {{safari|Johto}}.

===Pokémon Gold & Silver: The Golden Boys===

===Origin===
Octillery seems to be based on an {{wp|octopus}}. It also has some basis on a {{wp|tank}} or a wheeled {{wp|cannon}}. Beside the firearm theme, {{p|Remoraid}} and Octillery share the trait of being based on aquatic animals with suction cups.

====Name origin====
Octillery may be a combination of ''octopus'' and ''{{wp|artillery}}''.

Okutank may be a combination of ''octopus'' and ''tank''.
