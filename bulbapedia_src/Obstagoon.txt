'''Obstagoon''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''タチフサグマ''' ''Tachifusaguma'') is a dual-type {{2t|Dark|Normal}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation VIII]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{rf|Galarian}} {{p|Linoone}} when [[level]]ed up starting at level 35 during the [[Time-based Evolution|night]]. It is the final form of Galarian {{p|Zigzagoon}}.

==Biology==
Obstagoon is a black-and-white, bipedal {{OBP|Pokémon|species}}. Its head sits forward of its body on a long, thick neck; it has a pointed snout like a {{wp|badger}}, with its long tongue that frequently hangs out of its mouth. It has a thicker coat of gray fur around its forearms and lower legs, as well as around its back, and most of its chest like a vest.

It is said that Obstagoon's Evolution is the result of the Galarian {{p|Linoone}}'s survival instinct being honed from competition and harsh conditions of the [[Galar]] region. While combative to the extreme, it doesn't attack often, as it prefers taunting an opponent into attacking first. When that happens, it uses its [[signature move]] {{m|Obstruct}}, in which it threateningly crosses its arms and lets out a staggering yell, so loud and obnoxious that it would cause any opponent to flinch. Obstagoon is the only known Pokémon capable of learning the move Obstruct. By using the move, it skillfully throws opponents off guard before countering with its sharp claws.<ref>[https://swordshield.pokemon.com/en-us/pokemon-galar-region/obstagoon/ Obstagoon | Official Website | Pokémon Sword and Pokémon Shield]</ref> Obstagoon are also known for their duels against {{p|Pangoro}}.

===Evolution===
Obstagoon evolves from {{rf|Galarian}} {{p|Linoone}} and is the final form of Galarian {{p|Zigzagoon}}.

===Origin===	
<!--Do not add Gene Simmons/KISS or David Bowie/Ziggy Stardust, unless you have official source stating that it was inspired by either.-->
Obstagoon appears to be based on a {{wp|European badger}}. Its humanoid traits, color scheme, and pointed tongue may also be inspired by {{wp|glam rock}} bands, further supported by a particular Obstagoon's presence as a member of a band called the Maximizers. It's name, humanoid traits, posture, and typing could also be inspired by hired thugs or "goons" who often have criminal backgrounds and serve as bodyguards. It also shares traits with the {{wp|List of hybrid creatures in folklore#Modern fiction|gnoll}}, a hyena-like humanoid creature.

Obstagoon's evolutionary line shares a common theme of movement where {{p|Zigzagoon}} moves in a zigzag pattern, {{p|Linoone}} moves in a straight line, and Obstagoon blocks the way by obstructing it.

====Name origin====
Obstagoon may be a combination of ''obstacle'' or ''obstruct'', ''raccoon'', and ''goon''.

Tachifusaguma may be a combination of 立ち塞ぐ ''tachifusagu'' (to obstruct) and 穴熊 ''anaguma'' ({{wp|badger}}). ''Guma'' (from 熊 ''kuma'', "bear") as a suffix is often used for mammals that resemble bears.
