'''Swanna''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''スワンナ''' ''Swanna'') is a dual-type {{2t|Water|Flying}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation V]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Ducklett}} starting at [[level]] 35.

==Biology==
Swanna is a {{wp|swan}}-like {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} with primarily white plumage. Its beak is long and yellow topped with a black cere, and it has several white feathery features adorning its head. The more rounded of the features are to the sides of its head, and the more pointed feature is atop. Swanna possesses a long, curving neck. With a fluid neck and a strong bill, Swanna is capable of very accurate jabbing and thrusting attacks. Two light-blue feathery features, similar to those on the sides of Swanna's head, are present on Swanna's underside, and Swanna's large wings are positioned so that they curve away from its body. Swanna's legs are small, and its webbed feet are dark.

Swanna is known to fly for thousands of miles without rest, and it flies out when the sun rises. At dusk, flocks of Swanna will dance, with the leader in the middle of the routine. Swanna is quite physically powerful for its graceful appearance as its beak can deliver forceful blows. Many artists have claimed to have taken inspiration from a Swanna performing a beautiful dance during the light of dawn.

====Major appearances====
=====[[Skyla's Swanna]]=====
In ''[[BW067|Cilan Takes Flight!]]'', [[Skyla]] first used a Swanna in her {{pkmn|battle}} against {{an|Cilan}}, where it was able to defeat {{TP|Cilan|Crustle}} and {{TP|Cilan|Stunfisk}}. In ''[[BW068|An Amazing Aerial Battle!]]'', it was used in a [[Gym]] battle against {{Ash}}. It defeated {{AP|Pikachu}} but fell to Ash's newly evolved {{AP|Unfezant}}.

=====Other=====
In ''[[BW021|The Lost World of Gothitelle!]]'', the [[Officer Jenny]] stationed near [[Skyarrow Bridge]] unsuccessfully used a Swanna to {{m|Defog|clear a fog}} that had formed around the bridge.

In ''[[BW081|Crisis at Ferroseed Research!]]'', another Officer Jenny used multiple Swanna to control the moss growing around the [[Ferroseed Research Institute]] with {{type|Ice}} moves.

In ''[[BW107|Cameron's Secret Weapon!]]'', {{un|Cameron}} used a Swanna in his [[Vertress Conference]] battle against {{Ash}}. It was defeated by {{AP|Pikachu}}.

Multiple Swanna appeared in ''[[XY069|Defending the Homeland!]]''. Two of them reappeared in ''[[XY070|Beyond the Rainbow!]]'', ''[[XY139|Facing the Needs of the Many!]]'', and ''[[XY140|Till We Compete Again!]]''.

In the Hoopa's Surprise Ring Adventures short ''{{DL|Hoopa's Surprise Ring Adventures|Flying in the Air}}'', {{an|Hoopa}} summoned a Swanna to create wind for Ash and Pikachu, but its {{m|Gust}}, combined with other summoned {{type|Flying}} Pokémon, was too much for them and sent them blasting off.

In [[HZ095]], [[Dash]]'s {{p|Ducklett}} was shown to have evolved into a Swanna.

==Trivia==
* Swanna was first seen in a video for the [[Pokémon Musical]]s, again when its art leaked on {{wp|Yahoo!}}'s Pokémon website, and finally in the September issue of CoroCoro.
* Swanna is the only Pokémon with a base stat total of 473.

===Origin===
Swanna is based on a {{wp|mute swan}}, and its evolutionary line is based on the fairy-tale {{wp|The Ugly Duckling}}. Additionally, its design seems to draw inspiration from the outfit worn by the main character of the ''{{wp|Swan Lake}}'' {{wp|ballet}}, Odette.

====Name origin====
Swanna is derived from ''swan'' and may be a combination of ''swan'' and ''ballerina''.
