'''Copperajah''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''ダイオウドウ''' ''Daioudou'') is a {{type|Steel}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation VIII]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Cufant}} starting at [[level]] 34.

Copperajah can [[Gigantamax]] into '''Gigantamax Copperajah''' if it has the {{DL|Gigantamax|Gigantamax Factor}}.

==Biology==
Copperajah is a large, cuboid, elephantine {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} with a dark green body and a light teal underbelly. [[Pokédex entry|Pokédex entries]] for its pre-Evolution {{p|Cufant}} suggest that this coloration probably results from the tarnishing of copper in its skin over time. Untarnished copper accents cover most of Copperajah's face, and pattern its ears, feet, trunk, and back. It has short, blunt tusks and a long, broad, muscular trunk that ends in three claw-like protrusions. It has three raised, white ridges on the dorsal end of its trunk, and several rocky protrusions on the dome of its skull.

Despite being found in the [[Galar]] and [[Paldea]] regions, Copperajah and its pre-evolved form {{p|Cufant}} are not native to either of them, having been imported from another [[region]] to work with humans. It also works with other Pokémon, such as {{p|Gigalith}}, in construction sites and mines. Copperajah's tarnished skin is resistant to water. It has enough physical strength to pull an airplane, and its trunk is strong enough to reduce boulders to a fine powder. It lives in social herds and has a boastful disposition; the more vibrant its skin is, the prouder it will be.

===Forms===
Copperajah has a [[Gigantamax]] form.

When it [[Gigantamax]]es, Copperajah's trunk enlarges, its body becomes block-like, and it adopts a bipedal posture, standing on its large hind feet. Its rectangular tusks angle upward and the raised bumps on its trunk extend into overlapping, serrated ridges. Clouds of Dynamax energy float above its head. While Gigantamaxed, Copperajah's trunk is powerful enough to level mountains and obliterate large structures. 

Gigantamax Copperajah is the [[Signature move|only known Pokémon]] capable of using the [[G-Max Move]] {{m|G-Max Steelsurge}}.

===Main series===
====Major appearances====
Copperajah debuted in ''[[JN044|Sword and Shield: "From Here to Eternatus!"]]'', under the ownership of [[Rose]]. It was used to battle [[Leon]] and {{Ash}} before teaming up with Rose's {{p|Ferrothorn}} to battle {{AP|Pikachu}} and {{AP|Riolu}}. It was defeated in [[JN045|the following episode]].

A Copperajah, [[nickname]]d Rajah, appeared in ''[[HZ055|Showdown! The Paldea Elite Four]]'', under the ownership of [[Poppy]]. It was used alongside Poppy's {{p|Tinkaton|Tinkie}} in a battle against [[Dot]] and {{an|Iono}}.

==Trivia==
* When Copperajah has the {{a|Heavy Metal}} [[Ability]], its [[weight]] becomes 2,866.0&nbsp;lbs (1,300.0&nbsp;kg), exceeding the 2,204.4&nbsp;lbs (999.9&nbsp;kg) "limit" for a Pokémon's weight.
* Although Copperajah is unobtainable in [[Pokémon Legends: Arceus]], it is briefly mentioned in the Pokédex entry for {{p|Raichu}}.
* Copperajah's English {{pkmn|category}}, "Copperderm", may be a combination of ''copper'' and ''-derm'' (suffix derived from an Ancient Greek word meaning "skin") or ''{{wp|Pachydermata|Pachyderm}}''. The name can be taken to mean "copper skin".

===Origin===
Given Asia's history with Britain and the use of {{wp|captive elephants}} as {{wp|working animal}}s, Copperajah may be based on an {{wp|Indian elephant}} with the digging bucket of a {{wp|backhoe}} for a trunk. It may also be inspired by {{wp|Cultural depictions of elephants|elephant statues}} often made of {{wp|copper}} or {{wp|Bronze sculpture|bronze}}, which are common in {{wp|Indian art}} and {{wp|Thai art}}. 

====Name origin====
Copperajah may be a combination of ''copper'' and ''{{wp|Maharaja|Maharajah}}'' (Indian title of great king). It may also incorporate ''gajah'' (Indonesian and Malay for elephant), which in turn is derived from गज ''gaja'' (Sanskrit for elephant).

Daioudou may be a combination of 大王 ''daiō'' (great king) and 王道 ''ōdō'' (kingship), 黄銅 ''ōdō'' (brass), or 銅 ''dō'' (copper).
