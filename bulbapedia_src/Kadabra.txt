'''Kadabra''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''ユンゲラー''' ''Yungerer'') is a {{type|Psychic}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation I]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Abra}} starting at [[level]] 16 and evolves into {{p|Alakazam}} when [[Trade Evolution|traded]] or when exposed to a [[Linking Cord]].

(Refer to [[#Evolution data|Game data&rarr;Evolution data]] for more details.)

==Biology==
Kadabra is a primarily yellow, slightly {{wp|humanoid}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} with {{wp|mammal}}ian features. It has two long, pointed ears on top of its head, a red star on its forehead, narrow, deep-set eyes, and wide cheeks leading down to a thin snout. Extending from each side of its snout are two mustache-like tufts of fur; a male will have a longer mustache than a female. Kadabra's torso is segmented with bulky shoulders, a thick, brown chest, and a small abdomen marked by three red, wavy lines. Attached to the Pokémon's abdomen is a large, thick tail encircled with a brown band near its base. Its arms are thin with brown elbows and end in three-fingered hands with white claws. Its legs have prominent knees and large, three-toed feet, also ending in white claws. Two of its toes face forward and one backward. Kadabra is always seen carrying a silver spoon, which amplifies its telekinetic powers. A gold spoon does not work as an amplifier and Kadabra is only half as strong without a spoon.

Kadabra emits {{wp|alpha wave}}s strong enough to induce headaches, and can even cause clocks to run backward, machines to malfunction, and delicate devices to cease functioning altogether. The waves increase further in strength the more danger Kadabra faces. By closing its eyes, it can double the number of alpha particles it emits. When it has a headache, the alpha waves become unusual. All of Kadabra's brain cells work in unison while it uses its powers. It sleeps while levitating, using its springy tail like a pillow. It can store up psychic energy inside its star in preparation for its Evolution. There is a theory that Kadabra was born from a transformed human child who could not control his powers. Kadabra lives in {{DL|List of Pokémon by habitat|Urban Pokémon|urban areas}}.

Kadabra and its evolved form, {{p|Alakazam}}, are the [[signature move|only known Pokémon]] capable of learning the move {{m|Kinesis}}.

===Evolution===
Kadabra evolves from {{p|Abra}} and evolves into {{p|Alakazam}}.

====Major appearances====
=====[[Sabrina's Kadabra]]=====
Kadabra debuted in ''[[EP022|Abra and the Psychic Showdown]]'', where [[Sabrina]]'s Abra evolved into one during the [[Gym]] {{pkmn|battle}} that she held against {{Ash}}. It reappeared in ''[[EP024|Haunter versus Kadabra]]'' during Ash's [[rematch]] with Sabrina.

====Minor appearances====
In ''[[EP099|The Mandarin Island Miss Match]]'', a {{pkmn|Trainer}}'s Kadabra was defeated in a {{pkmn|battle}} by [[Lorelei|Prima]]'s {{TP|Lorelei|Cloyster}}.

A Kadabra appeared in a fantasy in ''[[EP116|The Rivalry Revival]]''.

In ''[[AG146|Fear Factor Phony]]'', a Kadabra lived at an abandoned mining colony. This marks the last physical appearance of Kadabra in the animated series to date.

A Kadabra made a brief cameo in the intro sequence of ''[[M15|Kyurem VS. The Sword of Justice]]'', along with every other Pokémon from the first five [[generation]]s, excluding {{p|Genesect}}.

==Trivia==
* In the {{DL|Pokémon Red and Green beta|Localization-specific information|Pokémon Red and Blue beta}}, Kadabra was originally known as "Pocus".
* [[Pocket Monsters Encyclopedia]] says that Kadabra's [[Pokédex]] entry from {{game|Red and Green|s}} is a reference to an in-universe novel "Transformation" that won the second Pokémon Literary Award. The plot of this in-universe novel seems to resemble the {{wp|Franz Kafka}} novel ''{{wp|The Metamorphosis}}'' whose title has had the alternate translation of ''The Transformation''.
* In {{game|Crystal}}, a {{DL|List of glitches in Generation II|Heavy Ball junk data glitch|bug}} causes the capture formula for the [[Heavy Ball]] to incorrectly retrieve this Pokémon's weight, giving it a +40 catch rate modifier. This also affects {{p|Tauros}} and {{p|Sunflora}}.
* If a Kadabra holds an [[Everstone]] from [[Generation III]] onwards while traded, the Everstone will not take effect and the Kadabra will still evolve into {{p|Alakazam}}. While it shares this trait with {{p|Machoke}}, {{p|Graveler}} and {{p|Haunter}} in Generation III due to a glitch, Kadabra keeps this trait in [[Generation IV]] onwards while the other three do not.

===Design variations===
Kadabra's original front [[#Sprites|sprite]] in {{game|Red and Green|s}} lacks the star on its head and the pattern on its abdomen. The star is also missing from its sprite in {{game|Red and Blue|s}}. Additionally, Kadabra's Red and Green sprite appears to depict it with four fingers on its hand instead of three, with its middle finger folded into its palm. In its corresponding artwork for the game, this finger is missing, but the knuckle is still visible. All other depictions of Kadabra use the three-fingered hand design. This may relate to a similar phenomenon affecting other Pokémon that originally had [[Pokémon controversies#Four-fingered hands|four fingers on each hand]].

===Controversy===
{{main|Pokémon controversies#Uri Geller|Pokémon controversies → Uri Geller}}
For 20 years, Kadabra was not printed as a {{Trading Card Game}} card since the English {{TCG|Skyridge}} expansion in 2003. This was likely the result of Nintendo and Pokémon Card Laboratories' desire to avoid further litigation from illusionist {{wp|Uri Geller}}, who claimed that Kadabra is a parody of him and attempted to sue Nintendo over the notion. However, on November 28, 2020, Geller [https://twitter.com/TheUriGeller/status/1332794451727691777 released an apology on Twitter], announcing that he would allow Nintendo to print cards featuring Kadabra again. The first {{TCG ID|151|Kadabra|64}} card released since Uri Geller's dismissal of his lawsuit is included in the Japanese {{TCG|Pokémon Card 151}} subset, released on June 16, 2023; and the English {{TCG|151}} expansion, released on September 22, 2023.

Due to the now-lifted limitation, very few {{TCG|Abra}} were printed, with the sole example, the {{TCG ID|Mysterious Treasures|Abra|69}} in {{TCG|Mysterious Treasures}}, having the ability to evolve directly into {{TCG|Alakazam}} via the '''Ultra Evolution''' {{TCG|attack}}. Alakazam cards also saw fewer than normal printings, but snuck into sets using a number of mechanics that allow for it to be printed as a {{TCG|Basic Pokémon}} that does not evolve from Kadabra. Examples include [[Alakazam ☆ (EX Crystal Guardians 99)|Alakazam]] {{Star}}, [[Alakazam-EX (Fates Collide 25)|Alakazam]]{{EX}}, and {{TCG ID|Silver Tempest|Radiant Alakazam|59}}.

For possibly the same reason, Kadabra had not made any animated appearances since ''[[AG146|Fear Factor Phony]]'' aired in the United States in 2006, except for a small cameo in the "World of Pokémon" opening of ''[[M15|Kyurem vs. The Sword of Justice]]''. Kadabra finally made its media reappearance in the [[Pokémon Evolutions]] episode ''[[PE07|The Show]]'', its first animated appearance in over 16 years, excluding its cameo in the aforementioned movie. Notably, when {{Ash}} and {{ashfr}} met [[Mira]] in ''[[Pokémon the Series]]'', she was seen using an Abra instead of a Kadabra as she does in the {{pkmn|games}}, despite the fact that the other [[Stat Trainers]] in the series own the same Pokémon as they do in the games.

Kadabra also did not appear in {{g|Ranger: Shadows of Almia}}, whereas Abra and Alakazam did.

===Origin===
Kadabra appears to be based on {{wp|Magician (fantasy)|magicians}} and {{wp|Spoon bending|spoon benders}}. It also shares similarities with {{wp|fox}}es, animals mythologically tied to magic. Its mustache-like tufts of fur may be an exaggeration of a fox's whiskers. The symbols on Kadabra's forehead and abdomen appear to be references to {{wp|Zener cards}}, a small deck of symbols used in experiments to test for {{wp|extrasensory perception}} and {{wp|clairvoyance}} in the early 1930s. As it created its spoon with its psychic powers, it may be based on the practise of {{wp|alchemy}}, an early science that sought to synthesise metals with magic. The "Transformation" novel about Kadabra may be based on {{wp|Franz Kafka}}'s ''{{wp|The Metamorphosis}}'' or the ''{{Wp|Ultra Q}}'' episode "Kanegon's Cocoon", where a young boy transformed into a strange yellow monster.

====Name origin====
Kadabra may be derived from ''{{wp|abracadabra}}'', an incantation used in {{wp|magic (illusion)|stage magic}}.

Yungerer may be named after ''{{wp|Uri Geller}}'', an illusionist famed for his spoon-bending technique. This contributed to Geller {{wp|Uri Geller#Copyright claims|suing}} [[Nintendo]] for using his likeness in a character.
