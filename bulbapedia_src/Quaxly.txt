'''Quaxly''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''クワッス''' ''Kuwassu'') is a {{type|Water}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation IX]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] into {{p|Quaxwell}} starting at [[level]] 16, which evolves into {{p|Quaquaval}} starting at level 36.

Along with {{p|Sprigatito}} and {{p|Fuecoco}}, Quaxly is one of the three [[Paldea first partner Pokémon|first partner Pokémon of Paldea]] available at the beginning of [[Pokémon Scarlet and Violet]]. They were also the first Generation IX Pokémon to be revealed to the public on February 27, 2022, through a [[Pokémon Presents]] video presentation.

==Biology==
[[File:Quaxly Messy Coif anime.png|250px|thumb|left|Quaxly with a messy, unkempt coif]]
Quaxly is a white duckling-like Pokémon. It has a yellow beak and blue eyes. It has teal webbed feet and a large teal coif-like crest that covers its head, with a feathery tuft in the front and a white curvy line shaped like a wave. Quaxly's coif has a smooth feel thanks to the rich, moist cream that holds it. The cream it secretes can repel grime. The coif can become unkempt should it get dry.<ref name=SVsite>[https://scarletviolet.pokemon.com/en-us/pokemon/quaxly/ Quaxly — Pokémon Scarlet and Pokémon Violet | Official Website]</ref>

Quaxly is an earnest and tidy Pokémon, and tends to overthink things. Quaxly's feathers secrete a glossy gel which protects its body against water and grime. It hates getting itself dirty, especially on its head. Quaxly's strong legs allow it to swim without issues, even in strong currents. It also uses its legs to repeatedly kick its foes with swift speed.<ref name=SVsite /> Although found in [[Paldea]], Quaxly is not native to the region, instead having migrated from faraway places a long time ago. As mentioned in the [[Sleep style|Sleep Style Dex]], the coif on its head grows dry and messy should Quaxly sleep for a very long time. However, this tend to happen on extremely rare occasions.

==Trivia==
* Quaxly is the only one of the [[first partner Pokémon]] that is foreign to the [[region]] that it was introduced in, as evidenced by its [[Pokédex]] entry in Pokémon Scarlet.
* In [[Generation IX]], prior to the Version 2.0.1 update of {{g|Scarlet and Violet}}, Quaxly and its Evolution were unable to learn {{m|Substitute}} and {{m|Sleep Talk}} via [[TM]]. However, this has since been fixed.

===Origin===
Quaxly appears to be based on a duckling of the {{wp|Crested (duck breed)|Crested domestic duck breed}} or the {{wp|crested duck|South American crested duck}}. The crest itself resembles a {{wp|beret}}, a {{wp|turban}}, or a {{wp|pompadour hairstyle}}. The crest might also be a visual pun on the hairstyle called the {{wp|ducktail}}. The teal colorations on its feet and crest may be inspired by the {{wp|blue-footed booby}}, or another visual pun on the {{wp|Eurasian teal|teal}} (a type of duck). Its behavior may also be based on {{wp|hornpipe}} dancing.

Quaxly's Pokédex entry in Scarlet could be a reference to the exotic origin of aforementioned species in regard to Iberia. The crested breed of ducks was introduced from the {{wp|Dutch East Indies}} and popularized in North America. Crested ducks and blue-footed boobies are native to South America. Given the carnival theme in its Evolutions, this may also refer to the {{wp|Rio Carnival}}.

====Name origin====
Quaxly may be a combination of ''quack'', ''wax'' (referring to {{wp|hair wax}}), and ''duckling''.

Kuwassu may be derived from クワック ''kuwakku'' (quack) and an {{wp|anagram}} of ワックス ''wakkusu'' (wax).
