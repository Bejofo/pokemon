'''Leavanny''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''ハハコモリ''' ''Hahakomori'') is a dual-type {{2t|Bug|Grass}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation V]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Swadloon}} when [[friendship Evolution|leveled up with high friendship]]. It is the final form of {{p|Sewaddle}}.

==Biology==
[[File:Tula trapped silk thread.png|thumb|200px|left|Sticky silk produced by Leavanny in [[Pokémon Adventures]]]]
Leavanny is a thin, yellow, bipedal insectoid {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} that resembles a leaf cutter ant. It has a broad, split-leaf around its large, round head and it has long antennae that end in semicircles. Its eyes are red with no apparent sclerae. Its tiny {{wp|thorax}} is dark green, and its long abdomen is yellow and covered in a green leaf. Its hands are long and leaf-like with a "bite" taken out of each. Its upper arms are also leaf-like but with a vein of yellow at the top. Its hips are wide and Its slender legs are yellow with dark green, thick segments around its thighs, making them appear armored. 

Leavanny's arm blades are powerful enough to slice through a tree trunk with a single swing. It generally lives in forests. Leavanny is very parental and uses its cutters and the sticky silk it produces to create clothing made of leaves for small Pokémon. Leavanny is known to make warm wrappings for young {{p|Sewaddle}}. It also warms its eggs with fermenting fallen leaves. While usually gentle, it will attack anyone or anything that harms young Pokémon.

===Evolution===
Leavanny evolves from {{p|Swadloon}} and is the final form of {{p|Sewaddle}}.

==In animation==
===Main series===
[[File:Rocket Prize Master Leavanny.png|thumb|250px|Leavanny in {{aniseries|JN}}]]
====Major appearances====
=====[[Ash's Leavanny]]=====
In ''[[BW074|Battling the Leaf Thieves!]]'', {{Ash}}'s Swadloon evolved into a Leavanny during a practice {{pkmn|battle}} against [[Iris's Emolga]].

=====Other=====
Leavanny debuted in ''[[BW018|Sewaddle and Burgh in Pinwheel Forest!]]'', under the ownership of [[Burgh]]. It reappeared in ''[[BW023|Battling For The Love of Bug-Types!]]'', where it participated in Burgh's [[Gym]] battle against Ash. It defeated Ash's Swadloon but was eventually defeated by {{AP|Pikachu}}. Leavanny reappeared in flashbacks in ''[[BW074|Battling the Leaf Thieves!]]'' and ''[[BW103|Certain Up, Unova League!]]''.

In ''[[BW038|Movie Time! Zorua in "The Legend of the Pokémon Knight"!]]'', [[Luke]]'s Leavanny would help him create costumes for the film, and it was once assisted by Ash's Swadloon. In ''[[BW082|An Epic Defense Force!]]'', it played a part of Luke's latest movie.

In ''[[JN094|Suffering the Flings and Arrows!]]'', [[James]] pulled a Leavanny from the [[Rocket Prize Master]].

====Minor appearances====
A {{pkmn|Trainer}}'s Leavanny appeared in ''[[BW067|Cilan Takes Flight!]]''.

In ''[[M15|Kyurem VS. The Sword of Justice]]'', the {{OBP|Swords of Justice|M15}} legend featured a Leavanny.

A Leavanny appeared in a flashback in ''[[BW112|Team Plasma's Pokémon Power Plot!]]''.

===Origin===
Leavanny has the characteristics of several insects. Primarily, it appears to be based on a bipedal {{wp|Phylliidae}}, but like its pre-evolved forms, it may also be based on the {{wp|Epargyreus clarus|silver-spotted skipper}} and {{wp|weaver ant}}s or {{wp|leafcutter ant}}s. It also shares some characteristics with a {{wp|European mantis|praying mantis}}. Its general body structure is also similar to that of a ''{{wp|Choeradodis}}'' {{wp|mantis}}, which also have laterally expanded thoraxes and abdomens.

The way it weaves clothes for other Pokémon from leaves with its sticky silk may be based on a {{wp|tailor}} or a {{wp|fashion designer}}. Its leafy "dress", "collar", and "paned sleeve" arms may be based on {{wp|1600–1650 in Western European fashion|fashion in the early 1600s}}. Because of the way it acts towards smaller Pokémon and its baby Sewaddle, it could also be based on a human {{wp|mother}}.

====Name origin====
Leavanny is a combination of ''leaves'' and ''nanny''. It may also derive from ''{{wp|Levana}}'', the ancient Roman goddess of newborn babies.

Hahakomori may be a combination of 葉 ''ha'' (leaf), 母 ''haha'' (mother), and 子守 ''komori'' (babysitting).
