'''Zubat''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''ズバット''' ''Zubat'') is a dual-type {{2t|Poison|Flying}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation I]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] into {{p|Golbat}} starting at [[level]] 22, which evolves into {{p|Crobat}} when [[friendship Evolution|leveled up with high friendship]].

(Specifics may differ in past games. Refer to [[#Evolution data|Game data&rarr;Evolution data]] for these details.)

==Biology==
Zubat is a blue, {{wp|bat|chiropteran}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}}. While it lacks eyes, it has pointed ears with purple insides and a mouth with two sharp teeth on each jaw. A male will have larger fangs than a female. It has purple wing membranes supported by two elongated fingers and two long, thin tails.

Zubat lives in abundance in dark {{DL|List of Pokémon by habitat|Cave Pokémon|caves}}, although it has also been known to dwell in forests and under the eaves of old buildings. Due to its habitat, Zubat has evolved to have neither eyes nor nostrils. It instead navigates through dark environments and tight caves with {{wp|animal echolocation|echolocation}}, emitting ultrasonic cries to detect targets and obstacles. The frequency of these cries can vary slightly between Zubat colonies. As demonstrated in the ''[[Pokémon the Series]]'' episode ''[[EP006|Clefairy and the Moon Stone]]'', it will leave its abode at night with a mass of other Zubat to seek prey. Zubat is nocturnal and sleeps hanging upside down during the daytime, avoiding sunlight at all costs. Daylight causes Zubat to become unhealthy; prolonged exposure can even burn its skin. However, captured and {{tp|Brock|trained Zubat|Crobat}} have been recorded as much more tenacious in the daytime, even when directly exposed to sunbeams. While sleeping, or in colder conditions, Zubat gathers for warmth with others of its kind.

==Trivia==
* No other Pokémon has the same [[List of Pokémon with unique type combinations|type combination]] as Zubat and its evolved forms.
** Zubat and Golbat are the only Pokémon introduced in [[Generation I]] to currently have a unique type combination.
** Zubat and its evolved forms are the only {{type|Poison}} Pokémon in the {{egg3|Flying}}.
* Zubat and its evolved forms share their {{pkmn|category}} with Woobat. They are all known as the Bat Pokémon.
* Zubat's evolutionary line has several similarities with {{p|Woobat}}'s.
** All of the members of both lines are called the Bat Pokémon (excluding {{p|Swoobat}}), live in caves or other dark places, and have no visible eyes at their respective unevolved states.
** Members of both lines reach their respective fully evolved forms by [[friendship]] and both have similar Shiny forms (with green main bodies).
** Woobat and Zubat have somewhat similar English names, both having rhyming prefixes and ''bat'' as a suffix.
* Zubat can also be seen as a parallel to {{p|Noibat}}, as they are both {{type|Flying}} Pokémon based on bats and can have {{a|Infiltrator}} as a possible [[Ability]]. They also share the same base stats, with their Attack and Special Attack switched around.
* Zubat has the lowest base {{stat|Special Attack}} stat of all Flying-type Pokémon, tying with {{p|Taillow}}, {{p|Starly}}, {{p|Combee}}, and {{p|Pikipek}}.
* Despite its lack of eyes, Zubat is still capable of learning {{m|Mean Look}}.
* Due to its lack of eyes, Zubat is the only Pokémon whose Alpha form in {{pkmn|Legends: Arceus}} does not bear glowing red eyes.
** It is also one of the few [[Shadow Pokémon (GO)|Shadow Pokémon]] in {{g|GO}} that doesn't have red eyes, the other being Shadow {{p|Steelix}}.
* In order to highlight shortcomings in the review process for scientific papers, in 2020, entomologist Matan Shelomi submitted a paper titled "[[Cyllage City]] COVID-19 Outbreak Linked to Zubat Consumption" to a biomedical journal. Even though that paper rather obviously presents fabricated information, and was allegedly co-authored by fictional scientists like "Nasu Joy" of the Veterinary Virology department of Cyllage City Pokecenter, it was eventually published, before being removed shortly thereafter.<ref name="Bug Squad">[https://ucanr.edu/blog/bug-squad/article/matan-shelomi-era-fakenewspredatory-journals Matan Shelomi: In the Era of #FakeNews]</ref><ref name="">[https://www.researchgate.net/publication/343066384_Cyllage_City_COVID-19_Outbreak_Linked_to_Zubat_Consumption "Cyllage City COVID-19 Outbreak Linked to Zubat Consumption" on ResearchGate]</ref><ref name="Scientist">[https://www.the-scientist.com/opinion-using-pokmon-to-detect-scientific-misinformation-68098 The Scientist: Opinion: Using Pokémon to Detect Scientific Misinformation]</ref>

===Origin===
Zubat seems to be based on a {{wp|bat}}, particularly {{wp|vampire bat}}s, which bite other animals to {{wp|Hematophagy|feed on their blood}}. Its {{t|Poison}} type may be inspired by either the {{wp|anticoagulant}} properties of vampire bat saliva or the fact that bats are infamous for spreading deadly diseases such as {{wp|rabies}}. Its lack of eyes could be a reference to {{wp|troglomorphism}} and may additionally be influenced by the misconception that bats are blind due to their use of {{wp|Animal echolocation|echolocation}} to navigate. The claim that Zubat will suffer burns when exposed to sunlight may be inspired by mythological {{wp|vampire}}s.

====Name origin====
Zubat may be a combination of ''bat'' and ずばっと ''zubatto'' (piercingly).
