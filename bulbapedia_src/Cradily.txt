'''Cradily''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''ユレイドル''' ''Yuradle'') is a dual-type {{2t|Rock|Grass}} [[Fossil]] {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation III]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Lileep}} starting at [[level]] 40.

==Biology==
Cradily is a green, plant-like {{wp|echinoderm}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} with various yellow markings on its head, neck, and torso. There are circular, yellow eyespots on its head. However, its true eyes are concealed inside the dark, crescent opening on its head. Surrounding its head at the base are eight pink tentacles, which somewhat resemble flower petals. Its long, flexible neck has yellow bands in the center, and it can be extended to assist in capturing prey. Its body is short and squat with four stubby growths and a yellow, oval, target-like pattern on the chest.

Similar to a {{wp|crinoid}}, Cradily's heavy body acts as an anchor, preventing it from getting washed away by rough ocean currents. In ancient times, it {{DL|List of Pokémon by habitat|Sea Pokémon|built its nest in the shallows of warm seas}}. When the tide went out, it came up on land to search for prey. However, Cradily cannot move very fast due to its short legs. Instead, Cradily can extend its neck and tentacles by three times its usual length.  It then uses its tentacles to capture them and melts them with strong digestive acidic fluid secreted by the tentacles.

==In animation==
[[File:Steven Cradily.png|thumb|250px|Cradily in {{aniseries|JN}}]]
===Major appearances===
Cradily debuted in ''[[AG102|Where's Armaldo?]]'' as a resident of [[Wales Island]]. It was first spotted by [[Max]] when he was out collecting firewood, but the others didn't believe him. Cradily was later found by [[Annie Proctor]] and taken back to the lab.

In ''[[DP108|Dealing With Defensive Types!]]'', a Cradily sent {{TRT}} blasting off from the [[Canalave Gym]] with {{m|Energy Ball}}.

In ''[[JN118|Battling as Hard as Stone!]]'', {{an|Steven Stone|Steven}} used his Cradily in his [[Masters Eight Tournament]] match against {{Ash}}. It battled and defeated {{AP|Gengar}} before losing to {{AP|Pikachu}}.

===Minor appearances===
In ''[[M08|Lucario and the Mystery of Mew]]'', a Cradily was a part of the feuding armies that were eventually placated by [[Sir Aaron]] in the past. Another was living in the [[Tree of Beginning]].

A Cradily appeared in a fantasy in ''[[DP016|A Gruff Act to Follow!]]''.

===Origin===
{{Bulbanews|On the Origin of Species: Lileep and Cradily}}
Cradily may be based on {{wp|crinoid}}s (also known as sea lilies), extant marine animals. Crinoid fossils are particularly abundant, possibly inspiring Cradily's status as a [[fossil|fossil Pokémon]]. Its tentacles may also be inspired by those of the {{wp|sea anemone}}. It also bears resemblance to the {{wp|predatory tunicate}}. Cradily's {{wp|Eyespot (mimicry)|fake eyes}} are a type of animal mimicry. Cradily's English and Japanese names, along with the placement of its tentacles, indicate that it is possibly inspired by a {{wp|baby mobile}}.

====Name origin====
Cradily may be a combination of ''cradle'' and ''{{wp|lily}}''. It may also involve ''crinoid'', ''crag'', and ''crazily''.

Yuradle may be a combination of 海百合 ''umiyuri'' (sea lily), 揺れ ''yure'' (shaking), and ''cradle''.
