'''Bruxish''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''ハギギシリ''' ''Hagigishiri'') is a dual-type {{2t|Water|Psychic}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation VII]].

It is not known to [[Evolution|evolve]] into or from any other Pokémon.

==Biology==
[[File:Bruxish Adventures.png|thumb|left|150px|Bruxish's flower-like protuberance ]]
Bruxish is a fishlike {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} with a thick skin covered in colorful scales and fins. The front of its body is light blue with large, dark purple lips and a mouth full of sharp teeth. The upper half of its body is mostly light purple, while the lower half is mostly yellow. The purple portion of its body is dotted with light blue spots. The yellow underside extends up around its eyes, which are light purple with light blue pupils. Its eyelids are dark purple with long, light blue eyelashes. Its fins are all dark purple, light blue, or a combination of the two colors: the pectoral fins are mostly dark purple with a short line blue line along the bottom, the dorsal fin is light blue, the ventral fin is dark purple, and the tail fin is dark purple with a light blue, heart-shaped marking in the middle. On top of its head is a protuberance with a dark purple bulb at the tip. The bulb is able to open like a flower, which reveals a light blue orb in the center.

This Pokémon buries itself on the seafloor, leaving only the growth on its head exposed. By emitting a low level of psychic power, it serves as a radar to detect prey. Additionally, the protuberance can emit a strong psychic power. Opponents bathed in this power are stricken with terrible headaches and fall unconscious.<ref name=SMsite/> Prey, which includes {{p|Shellder}} and {{p|Mareanie}}, are ground into mush and eaten. {{p|Toxapex}} is a rival of Bruxish in warm ocean currents.

When emitting psychic power, Bruxish grinds its teeth, which stimulates its brain and creates psychic energy. This sound is enough to send nearby Pokémon fleeing immediately. In the [[Alola]] region, lifeguards allow this Pokémon to remain in their area to prevent other, dangerous {{t|Water}} Pokémon from gathering.<ref name=SMsite>[http://www.pokemon-sunmoon.com/en-us/pokemon/bruxish/ Pokémon Sun and Moon site | Bruxish]</ref> Any sunlight reflecting on Bruxish's ripples will cause the water to shine brightly.

Bruxish is the [[Signature Ability|only known Pokémon]] that can have {{a|Dazzling}} as an [[Ability]]. In [[Generation VII]], {{m|Psychic Fangs}} was its [[signature move]], but other Pokémon could still inherit it via {{pkmn|breeding}}.

===Major appearances===
A Bruxish appeared in ''[[SM031|The Island Whisperer!]]'', where it used its {{m|Psychic}} powers to trap a {{p|Wailmer}} near a rock formation in the sea. It then left the scene after [[Olivia]] and her {{TP|Olivia|Lycanroc}} scared it away with {{m|Rock Slide}}.

In ''[[JN076|All Out, All of the Time!]]'', a {{DL|List of Goh's Pokémon|Bruxish}} was caught by [[Goh]] during the [[Pokémon Catch Adventure Race]].

===Minor appearances===
Bruxish debuted in ''[[SM001|Alola to New Adventure!]]'', where {{an|Lana}} hooked one up while [[fishing]].

A Bruxish appeared in ''[[SM120|The One That Didn't Get Away!]]''. {{an|Mallow}} caught it whilst fishing, leading {{TP|Mallow|Tsareena}} to attack it before it left.

In ''[[JN076|All Out, All of the Time!]]'', the [[All-Out Brothers]] attempted to catch a Bruxish during the Pokémon Catch Adventure Race, but it got away. They later caught one off-screen.

===Origin===
Bruxish may be based on the {{wp|reef triggerfish}} — also known as the ''humuhumunukunukuāpuaʻa'' — the state fish of Hawaii. Its patterning is based on that of the {{wp|clown triggerfish}}.

Its colorful pattern resembles some styles of stereotypical {{wp|psychedelic art}}. Its {{a|Dazzling|signature Ability}} suggests it is also related to {{wp|dazzle camouflage}}.

====Name origin====
Bruxish may be a combination of ''{{wp|bruxism}}'' and ''fish''.

Hagigishiri may be a combination of 歯 ''ha'' (tooth), カワハギ ''kawahagi'' ({{wp|filefish}}), ぎっしり ''gisshiri'' (densely packed), and 歯軋り ''hagishiri'' (bruxism).
