'''Hypno''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''スリーパー''' ''Sleeper'') is a {{type|Psychic}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation I]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Drowzee}} starting at [[level]] 26.

==Biology==
Hypno is a bipedal, slightly humanoid {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} that has yellow skin. It has triangular ears with brown interiors, sleepy-looking eyes, and a large nose. On its neck, it has a ruff of white fur, which is longer on the female. Hypno has five fingers on each hand and holds a pendulum in its left. The pendulum resembles a flat, silver ring and is used for hypnotism. Hypno is never seen without it. It has three toes on each foot, which also have a pink pad on the undersides.

Hypno is able to put people to sleep by swinging its pendulum. It is known to reside away from humans and on {{DL|List of Pokémon by habitat|Grassland Pokémon|less-traveled roads}}. While it awaits its prey, it polishes its pendulum. If anyone comes by, Hypno will hypnotize them and eat their dreams. It carries away people having good dreams and is even known to have stolen a child at one point. However, in the [[Alola]] [[region]], it preys primarily on {{p|Komala}}, and cases of Hypno targeting humans are notably rare. Some Hypno are even known to assist doctors in hospitals when their patients cannot sleep at night. In the {{al|Trainers' School}} in [[Pokémon Ultra Sun and Ultra Moon]], a Hypno is shown to be capable of creating illusions, namely by disguising itself as a human and causing illusory children to appear. As shown in ''[[SM130|Battling Besties!]]'', Hypno is left feeling completely despondent if its pendulum gets damaged in any way.

==In animation==
[[File:Faba Hypno.png|thumb|250px|Hypno in {{aniseries|SM}}]]
===Major appearances===
Hypno debuted alongside its pre-evolved counterpart in ''[[EP027|Hypno's Naptime]]''. The members of [[HopHopHop Town]]'s [[Pokémon Fan Club|Pokémon Lover's Club]] were using the two of them to help them with their sleeping problems. However, the "Sleep Waves" from Hypno were also causing children to act like Pokémon and draining the energy of Pokémon in the local [[Pokémon Center]]. Drowzee eventually managed to fix this problem.

In ''[[EP271|Playing with Fire!]]'', {{jo|Harrison}} used a Hypno during his [[Silver Conference]] {{pkmn|battle}} against {{Ash}}. It was able to defeat {{AP|Totodile}} before losing to {{AP|Snorlax}}.

In ''[[AG146|Fear Factor Phony]]'', a Hypno lived in an abandoned mining colony with other Psychic-types.

In ''[[SM049|Mission: Total Recall!]]'', {{an|Faba}} first used a Hypno in an attempt to wipe {{an|Lillie}}'s memories. {{an|Gladion}} stopped it by defeating it with his {{TP|Gladion|Silvally}}. It made further appearances in {{aniseries|SM}}.

===Minor appearances===
In ''[[M02|The Power of One]]'', a Hypno sensed the disturbance caused by [[Lawrence III]].

In ''[[EP156|The Psychic Sidekicks!]]'', a Hypno was living in [[Len Town]].

In ''[[EP169|Beauty and the Breeder]]'', a {{tc|Pokémon Breeder}}'s Hypno participated in the [[Pokémon Beauty Contest]].

In ''[[EP176|Ariados, Amigos]]'', [[Mickey]] used a Hypno during his battle with {{OBP|Ralph|EP176}} at the [[Pokémon Jujitsu Academy]]. It lost to {{p|Houndour}}.

In ''[[AG162|Spontaneous Combusken!]]'', a {{pkmn|Coordinator}}'s Hypno competed in the [[Chrysanthemum Island]] [[Pokémon Contest]].

In ''[[JN018|Destination: Coronation!]]'', a Trainer's Hypno battled [[Visquez]]'s {{p|Raichu}}, only to be defeated by his {{m|Volt Tackle}}.

A Trainer's Hypno appeared in ''[[JN136|This Could be the Start of Something Big!]]''.

==Merchandise==
Hypno was one of the first four Pokémon designs revealed when the [[Pokémon 151]] brand was first announced. The shirt design features an image of Hypno swinging its pendulum from the left side of the shirt.

==Trivia==
* Hypno's Red, Blue, LeafGreen, and Scarlet Pokédex entries make reference to it using "PSI moves" (or "psi moves" in Scarlet), possibly indicating an early name for the {{t|Psychic}} type in English.
* Hypno's numbers in the [[List of Pokémon by Alola Pokédex number in Pokémon Ultra Sun and Ultra Moon|Alola Pokédex]] in [[Pokémon Ultra Sun and Ultra Moon]] and the [[List of Pokémon by Alola Pokédex number|Paldea Pokédex]] are identical: 067 for the former, and 67 for the latter.
* Hypno are most likely commonly left handed due to always holding their pendulums in their left hand.
* Despite being heavily associated with the move {{m|Dream Eater}}, Hypno and its pre-Evolution, {{p|Drowzee}}, cannot learn the move by leveling up outside of [[Pokémon: Let's Go, Pikachu! and Let's Go, Eevee!]].
** Notably, as the move is not a [[TM]] in [[Pokémon Scarlet and Violet]], Drowzee and Hypno cannot legally learn Dream Eater in these games, despite both them and the move itself being present.

===Origin===
Hypno may be based on a {{wp|tapir}}, {{wp|Hypnosis|hypnotist}}, and {{wp|Baku (mythology)|baku}}, or dream eater spirit, of Japanese mythology. It also looks similar to a {{wp|proboscis monkey}}. Aspects of Hypno's behavior also resemble various {{wp|Fairy|fairies}}, {{wp|goblin}}s, {{wp|Bogeyman|bogeymen}}, and other supernatural entities in {{wp|European folklore}}.

====Name origin====
Hypno may be derived from ''{{wp|hypnosis}}''.

Sleeper is literally ''sleeper''.
