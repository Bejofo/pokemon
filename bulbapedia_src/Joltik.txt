'''Joltik''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''バチュル''' ''Bachuru'') is a dual-type {{2t|Bug|Electric}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation V]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] into {{p|Galvantula}} starting at [[level]] 36.

==Biology==
Joltik is a tiny arachnid {{OBP|Pokémon|species}}. It has four legs tipped with blue, conical feet. Its ovoid body is completely covered in yellow fur, with notable tufts sticking out on each side of its face, just over each eye, and its lower back. Joltik has four blue eyes. The two larger eyes are set relatively far apart on its face, containing black pupils. The two smaller {{wp|Simple eye in invertebrates|simple eye}}s are situated between the first pair, slightly higher up on its face. These eyes lack eyelids and pupils, and stay open when Joltik blinks or sleeps. Joltik's mandibles point downward and reside at the bottom of its face, where they closely resemble tufts of hair. It is capable of producing silk from these mandibles, enabling it to hang from trees.

At 4 inches (0.1 meters) tall, Joltik is one of the [[List of Pokémon by height|shortest Pokémon]] in existence. In fact, it is too small to generate its own electricity, so it attaches itself to larger Pokémon and feeds off their static electricity. More than often it prefers sticking on {{p|Yamper}}'s hindquarters. It will also make its way into houses and feed off electricity in electrical sockets. All of the energy it absorbs it stores in an electric pouch on its body. As seen in the {{aniseries|BW}} episode ''[[BW028|Oshawott's Lost Scalchop!]]'', Joltik is not especially aggressive, but will defend itself if bothered. In the episode ''[[BW062|Crisis at Chargestone Cave!]]'', it's shown that if Joltik is unable to find a source of energy, it will be unable to move and be put in serious danger. Joltik mainly lives in caves and cities with electricity.

==In animation==
[[File:Joltik anime.png|thumb|250px|Joltik in {{aniseries|BW}}]]
===Major appearances===
Joltik debuted in ''[[BW028|Oshawott's Lost Scalchop!]]'', where it was accidentally mistaken as [[Ash's Oshawott]]'s missing [[Scalchop and seamitar|scalchop]]. When Oshawott put it on his stomach, the angry Joltik started to shock Oshawott until it was taken off.

In ''[[BW039|Reunion Battles In Nimbasa!]]'', {{OBP|Sylvester|BW039}} used a Joltik to battle [[Georgia]] during the first round of the [[Club Battle]] tournament. They lost when {{TP|Georgia|Beartic}} fell on top of Joltik despite it using a creative strategy in which it attached itself to Beartic's hide and used {{m|Thunderbolt}} on it.

In ''[[BW062|Crisis at Chargestone Cave!]]'', multiple Joltik and Galvantula stole electricity from Pokémon and objects to feed on because the rocks in the [[Chargestone Cave]] that they normally fed on were being stolen by {{TRT}}. They reappeared in a flashback in ''[[BW065|Battling the Bully!]]''.

===Minor appearances===
Multiple Joltik appeared in ''[[BW081|Crisis at Ferroseed Research!]]''.

A Joltik appeared in ''[[BW088|The Mystery of the Missing Cubchoo!]]'', where it shocked Oshawott and {{TP|Dawn|Piplup}}.

A Trainer's Joltik appeared in ''[[JN048|A Close Call... Practically!]]''.

In ''[[JN050|A Pinch of This, a Pinch of That!]]'', a Joltik was seen in the [[Wild Area]].

In ''[[JN139|Must Be Our Heroes and the Witch!]]'', a Joltik was seen in [[Glimwood Tangle]].

A Joltik appeared in ''[[HZ054|The Treasure of Eternity]]'', under the ownership of [[Yanga]].

===Origin===
Joltik's name, appearance, and its [[Pokédex entry|Pokédex entries]] stating that it latches onto larger Pokémon to drain their {{wp|static electricity}}, all suggest that it may be based on a {{wp|tick}}. It may also be based on a {{wp|jumping spider}} or, like its evolved form, a {{wp|tarantula}}.

====Name origin====
Joltik may be a combination of ''jolt'' and ''tick'' (a parasitic arachnid).

Bachuru may be a combination of バチバチ ''bachibachi'' (onomatopoeia for electric crackling) and タランチュラ ''taranchura'' (tarantula). It may also involve ''bug'' and 虫 ''chū'' (bug).
