'''Brionne''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''オシャマリ''' ''Osyamari'') is a {{type|Water}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation VII]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Popplio}} starting at [[level]] 17 and evolves into {{p|Primarina}} starting at level 34.

==Biology==
[[File:Brionne SM concept art.jpg|thumb|250px|left|Concept art of Brionne for {{g|Sun and Moon}}]]
Brionne is a light blue, {{wp|pinniped}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} with three ruffle-like growths around its body. The first ruffle is just under its front flippers, while the last is shortly before the tail. The ruffles alternate between white and pale blue. Its long ears are made up of three segments that lighten and increase in size towards the tip; the first segment is ovoid, while the latter two are spherical. It has a long, conical snout with a white tip, a spherical pink nose, and large dark eyes with pinkish pupils. Its front flippers are much longer than its tail flippers, although both have white tips. There are three digits on the front flippers.

Brionne is a skillful dancer. While it normally learns to dance from members of its colony, Brionne can also learn to dance from humans. It works diligently to memorize each dance through imitation and gets excited when it sees a dance it does not know. It is capable of dancing in perfect time with others, even if it's never danced with them before. Large groups of Brionne can be seen dancing together on moonlit nights when the sea is calm,<ref name=SMsite /> sometimes being led by the singing of a {{p|Primarina}}. Brionne has a caring nature; if its {{pkmn|Trainer}} is feeling down, it will perform a dance to cheer them up.

As Brionne dances, it creates water balloons in a variety of colors. Its battle strategy consists of dancing while creating water balloons. The dance confuses the opponent, and during this weakness, Brionne slaps the balloons toward the target where they explode. Only those who have earned its trust can see this Pokémon when it is sad. It will otherwise always act cheerful and never allow its sorrow to show.<ref name=SMsite>[http://www.pokemon-sunmoon.com/en-us/pokemon/brionne/ Pokémon Sun and Moon site | Brionne] ([https://web.archive.org/web/0/http://www.pokemon-sunmoon.com/en-us/pokemon/brionne/ Archive])</ref>

Brionne and its evolutionary relatives are the [[Signature Ability|only known Pokémon]] that can have {{a|Liquid Voice}} as an [[Ability]].

===Major appearances===
====[[Lana's Brionne]]====
{{an|Lana}}'s Brionne first appeared in ''[[SM106|Evolving Research!]]''. It evolved from her Popplio after a {{pkmn|training}} session inspired by [[Ida]] and her {{p|Primarina}}'s {{m|Oceanic Operetta}}. It evolved into a Primarina in ''[[SM120|The One That Didn't Get Away!]]''.

====Other====
Brionne debuted in ''[[SM040|Balloons, Brionne, and Belligerence!]]'', under the ownership of Ida. It was first seen being used as part of a street performance alongside its {{pkmn|Trainer}}. It evolved into a Primarina sometime before ''[[SM106|Evolving Research!]]''.

===Minor appearances===
A Trainer's Brionne appeared in ''[[SM133|Battling on the Wing!]]'', where it was watching the {{pkmn|battle}}s during the [[Manalo Conference]].

A Brionne appeared in a pair of fantasies in ''[[JN081|Mad About Blue!]]'', one of which featured it evolving into Primarina.

A Student's Brionne appeared in [[HZ090]].

==Trivia==
* Brionne and its evolutionary relatives are the only [[first partner Pokémon]] to [[List of Pokémon by shape|have a piscine body]].

===Origin===
Brionne appears to be based on a {{wp|sea lion}} and performers such as {{wp|Japanese idol}}s, {{wp|ballet dancer|ballerinas}} and {{wp|circus clown|circus clowns}}, additionally referencing trained sea lions which performed in events such as circuses. It may also take inspiration from the {{wp|Hawaiian monk seal}}.

====Name origin====
Brionne may come from ''brio'' (vivacity of style or performance), ''sea lion'', ''brine'', ''sissonne'' (a ballet step), and ''{{wp|undine|ondine}}'' (a type of female water nymph or mermaid).

Osyamari may be a combination of オシャレ ''oshare'' (fashionable) or おしゃま ''oshama'' (precocious young girl), 鞠 ''mari'' (ball), and ''marine''.
