'''Happiny''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''ピンプク''' ''Pinpuku'') is a {{type|Normal}} {{pkmn2|baby}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation IV]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] into {{p|Chansey}} when [[level]]ed up while holding an [[Oval Stone]] during the [[Time-based Evolution|day]], which evolves into {{p|Blissey}} when [[friendship Evolution|leveled up with high friendship]].

(Specifics may differ in past games. Refer to [[#Evolution data|Game data&rarr;Evolution data]] for these details.)

==Biology==
[[File:Happiny no stone.png|thumb|left|200px|Happiny without a stone in her pouch]]
Happiny is a small {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} that is shaped like an egg. It has a soft, smooth, light pink body. On top of its head is a curly outgrowth that resembles a {{wp|ponytail}}, with a dark pink band around the base. The appearance of this curly growth of hair often annoys Happiny. It has three round bumps on its forehead and two pink circles on its cheeks. There is a pouch around the lower half of its body. The pouch is red with a white stripe around the top.

Happiny's pouch is rubbery and flexible, enabling the Happiny to put things into it with ease. As it cannot produce {{pkmn|Egg}}s yet, it makes up for it by carrying something round and white in its pouch to imitate {{p|Chansey}} and {{p|Blissey}}, typically an Egg-shaped stone. Happiny highly treasures this stone. It may give this stone to people whom it likes, however, it can throw a tantrum and cry should the stone be taken from it or not returned to it. The object it decides to carry may be too large, stopping this Pokémon from moving. It has a habit of wandering around people's feet and causing them to stumble. Happiny enjoys playing with children, often playing a game of House with them. Happiny is shown to be extremely strong in the {{pkmn|animated series}}, as {{TP|Brock|one|Happiny}} has been seen lifting heavy things above its head. Happiny is a female-only species with no male counterpart. As mentioned in [[Sleep style|Sleep Style Dex]], Happiny has been observed sleeping while its egg-shaped stone is outside of its pouch. It is believed that Happiny is dreaming about {{wp|House (game)|playing house}} with its stone. Happiny has three different sleep styles it sleeps in: ''Smiling Sleep'', ''Rock-Minding Sleep'', and ''Playing-House Sleep''.


Happiny and its evolved forms are the [[signature move|only known Pokémon]] capable of learning the move {{m|Soft-Boiled}} by leveling up.

===Evolution===
Happiny evolves into {{p|Chansey}}, which evolves into {{p|Blissey}}.

===Evolution data===
Happiny [[Evolution|evolves]] into {{p|Chansey}} when [[level]]ed up while holding an [[Oval Stone]] during the [[Time-based Evolution|day]], which evolves into {{p|Blissey}} when leveled up with high [[friendship]]. Chansey cannot evolve into Blissey in {{game|FireRed and LeafGreen|s}} before the player obtains the [[National Pokédex]], as the Evolution will be interrupted. Chansey also cannot evolve into Blissey in {{LGPE}}, as Blissey is not present in those games. In {{g|Legends: Arceus}}, Happiny evolves into Chansey when exposed to an Oval Stone during the day. In {{g|Scarlet and Violet}}, the [[Luck Incense]] is not present and is no longer required to obtain Happiny through {{pkmn|breeding}}; Happiny will always be produced when breeding Chansey or Blissey, regardless of [[held item]]s.

===Major appearances===
====[[Brock's Happiny]]====
Happiny debuted in ''[[DP038|One Big Happiny Family!]]'', where she hatched from {{an|Brock}}'s {{pkmn|Egg}}. Unusually, in contrast to Happiny's extremely low {{stat|Attack}} stat in the {{pkmn|games}}, she has amazing strength and can run with, pummel, and carry many heavy items and Pokémon for her size. In ''[[DP190|The Brockster Is In!]]'', she evolved into a Chansey.

==Trivia==
* Happiny is the only Pokémon that evolves twice by leveling up under certain conditions, once by holding an item, and again with high [[friendship]].
* Happiny is tied with {{p|Chansey}} for the lowest base {{stat|Attack}} and {{stat|Defense}} stats of all Pokémon.
* Happiny has the lowest base {{stat|Special Attack}} stat of all {{type|Normal}} Pokémon.
* Happiny was designed by [[Emi Yoshida]].<ref>[https://www.amgakuin.co.jp/contents/p=1625 "Pokémon Diamond and Pearl" development staff talks about "The Road to Diamond and Pearl", Vol.1.] Amusement Media Academy. (Japanese)</ref>
* All moves Happiny can learn by leveling up in [[Pokémon Legends: Arceus]] are moves it could not legally learn before by any means. The only other Pokémon with this distinction is {{p|Burmy}}, which only learns Struggle Bug by level-up in Legends: Arceus.

===Origin===
Happiny shares some characteristics with infants, such as its soft skin, ponytail and {{wp|diaper}}-like pouch. It also resembles an egg cup. The way it pretends to be an adult version of itself and acts like a {{wp|nurse}} is reminiscent of little girls playing with {{wp|doll}}s.

====Name origin====
Happiny may be a combination of ''happiness'' and ''tiny'' or ''teeny''. It may also involve ''happenstance''.

Pinpuku may be a combination of ピンク ''pinku'' (pink) and 福 ''fuku'' (good fortune).
