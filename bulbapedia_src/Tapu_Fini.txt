'''Tapu Fini''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''カプ・レヒレ''' ''Kapu-Rehire'') is a dual-type {{2t|Water|Fairy}} [[Legendary Pokémon]] introduced in [[Generation VII]].

It is not known to [[Evolution|evolve]] into or from any other Pokémon.

It is the [[Guardian deities|guardian deity]] of [[Poni Island]], with {{p|Tapu Koko}}, {{p|Tapu Lele}}, and {{p|Tapu Bulu}} being the deities of the other islands.

==Biology==
[[File:Tapu Fini SM concept art.jpg|thumb|250px|left|Tapu Fini's concept art from {{g|Sun and Moon}}]]
Tapu Fini is a vaguely swordfish-like {{pkmn2|Legendary}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}}. Tapu Fini has a black body shaped similarly to a {{wp|mermaid}} or fish. It has light blue eyes, white eyelids, and a light blue, fin-like marking around the outer corner of each eye. It has a white T-shaped marking on its forehead and a small, white V-shape on its chest. Around its shoulders are segmented purple rings and its long thin arms have webbed hands with three fingers. Each finger is tipped with a purple claw. On the front of its lower body is a light blue, fin-like projection with three spikes. It also has light blue fins on top of its head that resemble long, flowing hair.

Tapu Fini's main body resides inside a violet shell that resembles a {{wp|swordfish}} head when closed. The top half of the shell has a fin with light blue webbing on top and a thin, pointed spine on the front. On each side of the top shell are two C-shaped markings facing each other to form a rough circle. One marking is white and one is light blue, but both are outlined in black. These markings form the "eye" of the swordfish. Along the edges of both shell halves are a series of white triangles with black outlines.

As the guardian of Poni Island, Tapu Fini can freely manipulate water and it gains energy from ocean currents. Tapu Fini is both respected and feared. To avoid harm to itself in battle, it shrouds its surroundings in a mysterious fog that sends its opponents into a self-destructive trance. The guardian also unleashes this fog as a test for those who seek the special water it can create, which is said to cleanse anything unclean that it touches. Many humans have attempted this trial, including those with ill intent; disgusted by this history, Tapu Fini does not care much for humans. Despite this, it is willing to help those who prove themselves worthy.<ref>[http://www.pokemon-sunmoon.com/en-us/pokemon/tapu-fini/ Pokémon Sun and Moon site | Tapu Fini]</ref> As seen in ''[[SM107|Run, Heroes, Run!]]'', Tapu Fini has shown to be able to make {{wp|water clock}}s out of water and bridge the world of the living with the world of the dead using mist. As stated in ''[[SM108|Memories in the Mist!]]'', long periods of exposure to this mist can trap people within it forever, specifically targeting those who cannot endure the pain of seeing a deceased loved one again. Tapu Fini is known to defend its home from the [[Ultra Beast]]s. As seen in the ''[[SM052|Revealing the Stuff of Legend!]]'', Tapu Fini and the other tapu can transform a [[Z-Ring]] into a [[Z-Power Ring]].

Tapu Fini and the other [[guardian deities]] are the [[signature move|only known Pokémon]] capable of learning the move {{m|Nature's Madness}}. They are also the only known Pokémon capable of using the [[Z-Move]] {{m|Guardian of Alola}}.

==Trivia==
* Tapu Fini shares its {{pkmn|category}} with {{p|Tapu Koko}}, {{p|Tapu Lele}}, and {{p|Tapu Bulu}}. They are all known as the Land Spirit Pokémon.
* In ''[[SM108|Memories in the Mist!]]'', Tapu Fini's mist is depicted as connecting the world of the living with the spirits of the dead. In ''[[BW026|Scare at the Litwick Mansion!]]'', a connection between the world of the living and the [[Ghost World]] is also associated with mist.
* Tapu Fini is the only member of the Guardian deities whose primary ability doesn't generate a [[Terrain]] associated with its primary type. Instead, it generates a [[Misty Terrain (move)|Misty Terrain]], which is associated with Fairy type, its secondary type.

===Origin===
Tapu Fini's appearance when it closes its shell resembles a {{wp|swordfish}} or a {{wp|marlin}}. Its overall design is also similar to a {{wp|tiki}} statue. When its shell is open, it resembles a {{wp|mermaid}} or a {{wp|Siren (mythology)|siren}}. The hinged shell itself is similar to many {{wp|bivalve}}s. It may also be inspired by {{wp|Kanaloa}}, one of the four major Hawaiian gods and god of the ocean. Kanaloa's association with the underworld may inspire the [[Ruins of Hope]], Tapu Fini's shrine, said to be where "life ends its journey" and is called the Ruins of the Other Side in Japanese.

====Name origin====
Tapu Fini may be a combination of ''tapu'' (Hawaiian for sacred or holy) and ''fin''. It may also derive from ''finish'' or ''final'', referencing the fact that the Ruins of Hope are where "life ends its journey", and also, possibly, that [[Poni Island]] is the final island in the [[island challenge]].

Kapu-Rehire may be a combination of ''kapu'' (Hawaiian for sacred or holy) and 鰭 ''hire'' (fin).
