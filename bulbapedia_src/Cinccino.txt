'''Cinccino''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''チラチーノ''' ''Chillaccino'') is a {{type|Normal}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation V]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Minccino}} when exposed to a [[Shiny Stone]].

==Biology==
[[File:Cinccino cleaning.png|thumb|left|250px|Cinccino cleaning itself]]
Cinccino is a furry, gray, chinchilla-like {{OBP|Pokémon|species}}. It has large ears located to the sides of its head, with red insides partly covered by tufts of fur. Its eyes are large and brown, with a small, rounded nose. Its limbs are somewhat rounded and small, and its tail is long, fluffy, and incredibly white in color. Cinccino also has two large long fluffs on its head that wrap around its back. Cinccino uses its long fur as a scarf. <!--Pokédex 3D confirms that the two fur strands on its head and the scarf are separate.-->

Cinccino's fur is coated in a special oil that repels enemy attacks. Cinccino also uses this oil to coat its nest, protecting it from dust. The oil can also be used as a skin treatment, and is sold for a high price. Cinccino's fur feels amazing to touch, and it repels dust and prevents static electricity buildup.

Prior to [[Generation VIII]], {{m|Tail Slap}} was its [[signature move]], but other Pokémon could still inherit it via {{pkmn|breeding}}.

===Major appearances===
In ''[[BW098|Beauties Battling for Pride and Prestige!]]'', [[Moira]]'s female Cinccino, [[nickname]]d Cinccinoette, battled [[Ash's Snivy]] and lost.

===Minor appearances===
Cinccino debuted in a flashback in [[M14|''White—Victini and Zekrom'' and ''Black—Victini and Reshiram'']].

In ''[[M15|Kyurem VS. The Sword of Justice]]'', the {{OBP|Swords of Justice|M15}} legend featured a Cinccino.

Cinccino made its animated series debut in ''[[BW090|Jostling for the Junior Cup!]]'', under the ownership of [[Horatio]]. It participated in the [[Pokémon World Tournament Junior Cup]]. Cinccino was first seen defeating [[Simeon]]'s {{p|Zebstrika}}, but it later went up against [[Cilan's Stunfisk]] and lost to a {{m|Sludge Bomb}}.

In ''[[BW126|Crowning the Scalchop King!]]'', [[Ash's Oshawott]] immediately fell in love with a Cinccino after getting over another {{p|Oshawott}} [[nickname]]d Osharina.

In ''[[M16|Genesect and the Legend Awakened]]'', {{OBP|Mewtwo|M16}}'s flashback featured a Cinccino.

A Trainer's Cinccino appeared in ''[[M23|Secrets of the Jungle]]''.

A Cinccino appeared in ''[[HZ054|The Treasure of Eternity]]'' and ''[[HZ078|Entei's Fierce Battle Cry!]]'', under the ownership of [[Cervantis]].

==Trivia==
* Due to an error, Cinccino is unable to learn {{m|Thief}} via [[TM]] in {{game|Black and White|s}}, while its pre-evolved form Minccino does. This issue was later fixed in {{2v2|Black|White|2}}.
* Cinccino and its pre-evolved form were designed by [[Atsuko Nishida]], and almost no changes were made to her original designs. They were intended to be parallels of [[Kanto]]'s {{p|Clefairy}} and {{p|Clefable}}; Pokémon that everyone would think was cute. Nishida was hired specifically for her reputation in [[Game Freak]] for her skill in designing cute characters.<ref name="Atsuko">''Nintendo Dream'' Vol. 201, January 2011 ([http://lavacutcontent.com/ken-sugimori-nintendo-dream-3/ Translation by Dr. Lava])</ref>
* Cinccino is the only fully evolved Pokémon to appear first when the player starts a [[new game#Pokémon example|new game]].

===Origin===
Cinccino seems to be based on a {{wp|chinchilla}}. Its white "hair" is very similar in appearance to {{wp|1600–1650 in Western European fashion#Style gallery 1600–1620|high-class women's hairstyles in the early 1600s}}. The white fur draped around its neck resembles {{wp|shawl}}s, {{wp|scarf|scarves}}, and {{wp|feather boa|boas}}, possibly a reference to how chinchillas are controversially hunted for their fur.

====Name origin====
Cinccino and Chillaccino may be a combination of ''{{wp|chinchilla}}'' and ''{{wp|chino cloth}}''. The suffix ''-ino'' or ''-cino'' is also a diminutive to denote something small.
