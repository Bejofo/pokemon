'''Wyrdeer''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''アヤシシ''' ''Ayashishi'') is a dual-type {{2t|Normal|Psychic}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced during [[Generation VIII]], in {{g|Legends: Arceus}}.

In {{g|Legends: Arceus}}, it [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Stantler}} after using {{m|Psyshield Bash}} in the [[Move mastery|agile style]] at least 20 times.

==Biology==
Wyrdeer is a snow deer-like {{OBP|Pokémon|species}}. Its body is mostly gray with white spots, as well as white fur on its lower legs, underside, round tail, eyebrows, and mane, the latter resembling a beard. Wyrdeer has large, golden antlers, the shape of which resembles radio antennae. Each of these antlers has one black orb near the base. Wyrdeer also has a brown nose and black cloven hooves, as well as two tufts of fur on the back of each lower leg.

Wyrdeer is well-beloved by the people in the [[Hisui]] region due to its highly prized fur, which provides excellent insulation from the cold and is used to make clothing. By using the orbs on its antlers, Wyrdeer can unleash psychic powers strong enough to distort space, and also create invisible barriers for protection. It also uses the antlers to lead {{p|Stantler}} herds and search for safe paths to travel.<ref>[https://legends.arceus.pokemon.com/en-us/pokemon/wyrdeer/ Wyrdeer | Pokémon Legends: Arceus Official Website]</ref> Wyrdeer's sturdy legs allow it to gallop and dash across the land, and jump over any obstacle in its way. Wyrdeer is implied to be extinct in the present day, Stantler are said to be no longer capable of evolving into Wyrdeer and they're not known to be found anywhere.

A special Wyrdeer is also worshiped due to being descended from one of the ten ancient Pokémon who received a blessing from {{p|Arceus}} and serves as a {{hi|Ride Pokémon}}. The people in [[Hisui]] use the [[Celestica Flute]] to call for his aid. He resides in the [[Obsidian Fieldlands]], watched over by the [[warden]] [[Mai]].

Wyrdeer and its pre-evolved form, Stantler, are the [[signature move|only known Pokémon]] capable of learning the move {{m|Psyshield Bash}}.

==Trivia==
* Wyrdeer, along with {{p|Ursaluna}} and {{p|Kleavor}}, are the first [[List of cross-generational evolutionary lines|cross-generational Evolutions]] that [[Evolution|evolve]] from the original form rather than the regional form to be introduced since {{p|Sylveon}} in [[Generation VI]].
** Coincidentally, their pre-Evolutions cannot evolve into them outside of their [[Pokémon Legends: Arceus|debut game]]. In Wyrdeer's case, this is due to {{p|Stantler}}'s [[Methods of Evolution|Evolution method]] being removed.
** In addition, because both Ursaluna (through a different form) and Kleavor are obtainable in [[The Hidden Treasure of Area Zero]], this makes Wyrdeer the only one of these Pokémon that is only available in {{g|Scarlet and Violet}} through transfer from [[Pokémon HOME]], along with Ursaluna's original form, though they can be obtained with its origin mark and possibly, the player's Trainer ID by transferring their pre-Evolution to {{g|Legends: Arceus}}, evolving them there and returning it back.
* Wyrdeer shares many similarities with {{p|Farigiraf}}.
** They are both {{2t|Normal|Psychic}} Pokémon.
** They both have {{a|Sap Sipper}} as a [[Hidden Ability]].
** They both are [[List of cross-generational evolutionary lines|Evolutions]] of [[Generation II]] Pokémon, those being Stantler for Wyrdeer and {{p|Girafarig}} for Farigiraf respectively.
** They are both based on {{wp|Artiodactyl|artiodactyls}}, with Wyrdeer being a {{wp|deer}} and Farigiraf being a {{wp|giraffe}}.
** They both debuted in games released in 2022, with Wyrdeer first appearing in {{g|Legends: Arceus}} and Farigiraf first appearing in Pokémon Scarlet and Violet.

===Origin===
Wyrdeer seems to be based on different {{wp|deer}} species, mainly the {{wp|Yezo sika deer}} and the {{wp|reindeer}}. The former is a subspecies of the {{wp|sika deer}} native to {{wp|Hokkaido}}, the region of Japan which Hisui is based on. Much like how Wyrdeer is treasured by Hisuian people because their fur is a useful clothing material, the Yezo sika deer is considered very important to the {{wp|Ainu people}} as their hide is essential for clothing. 

Wyrdeer's white beard-like mane and eyebrows resemble elderly Ainu men, and also the stereotypical representations of a {{wp|Wise old man|wise old men}}, {{wp|wizards}}, and {{wp|Santa Claus}}. Its antlers are similar to Stantler's and as such may be based on {{wp|shishi-odoshi}}, more specifically the {{wp|Bird_scarer#Balloons|''{{tt|medama fūsen|eyeball balloon}}''}}, also known as eyespot balloons or scare-eye balloons. The way Wyrdeer's antlers branch perpendicularly also resembles {{wp|Yagi–Uda antenna|Yagi antennae}}.

====Name origin====
Wyrdeer may be a combination of ''{{wp|wyrd}}'' ({{wp|Anglo-Saxon}} concept roughly equivalent to fate or destiny), ''weird'', and ''deer''.

Ayashishi may be a combination of 怪しい ''ayashii'' (strange/weird) and 鹿 ''kanoshishi'' or ''shishi'' (archaism for deer). This uncommon reading was likely chosen so the name stays similar to Stantler's Japanese name オドシシ ''Odoshishi'', which seems to reference 鹿威し ''{{wp|shishi-odoshi}}'' (a device used to scare deer and other animals away).
