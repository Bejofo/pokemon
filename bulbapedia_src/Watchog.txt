'''Watchog''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''ミルホッグ''' ''Miruhog'') is a {{type|Normal}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation V]].

It [[Evolution|evolve]]s from {{p|Patrat}} starting at [[level]] 20.

==Biology==
[[File:Lenora Watchog Illuminate.png|thumb|250px|left|Watchog {{a|Illuminate|illuminating}}]]
Watchog is an upright, meerkat-like {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} with large cheeks and a single, pointed incisor sticking out of its mouth. It is mainly reddish-brown with beige cheeks and neck. There is yellow striping on its chest and tail. Its hands, feet, and tail tip are white. Its red eyes have small black pupils and a yellow stripe. Black markings accentuate the eyes at their outer ends. Its tail is almost exclusively depicted standing up straight, although it can also be kept in a relaxed position.<ref>ポケモン ベストウイッシュ イッシュずかん ISBN 978-4-09-281215-4</ref>

There are luminescent compounds in Watchog's body that allow its stripes and eyes to glow. It uses this ability to threaten predators. Its cheeks can hold a large amount of seeds, which it collects and spits at its opponents. Watchog possess night vision and can view its surroundings in the darkness.

====Major appearances====
A Watchog debuted in ''[[BW014|A Night in the Nacrene City Museum!]]'', under the ownership of [[Lenora]]. This also marked the species' debut. It first used its {{a|Illuminate}} [[Ability]] to reveal {{p|Yamask}}'s presence. It then battled {{Ash}} in a [[Gym]] {{pkmn|battle}} in ''[[BW015|The Battle According to Lenora!]]'', where it defeated his {{AP|Oshawott}} with {{m|Thunderbolt}}. It appeared again in ''[[BW016|Rematch at the Nacrene Gym!]]'', where it was defeated by Oshawott's {{m|Aqua Jet}}.

In ''[[BW025|Emolga and the New Volt Switch!]]'', three Watchog lived in an apple tree, and attacked {{TP|Iris|Emolga}}, {{TP|Iris|Axew}}, and Oshawott when they tried to retrieve some apples for Emolga. However, they enraged a {{p|Simisear}} in the process, and were scared away by its {{m|Flamethrower}}.

In ''[[BW040|Cilan Versus Trip, Ash Versus Georgia!]]'', [[Jimmy Ray]]'s male Watchog lost to [[Iris's Emolga]] during the first round of the [[Club Battle]] after he was hit by {{m|Attract}} and then {{m|Hidden Power}}.

In ''[[BW077|Evolution by Fire!]]'', [[Kylan]]'s Watchog battled [[Shamus]] in a [[Double Battle]] along with a {{p|Mienshao}}, but both were defeated.

In ''[[BW103|Curtain Up, Unova League!]]'', [[Mikael]]'s Watchog battled [[Bianca's Emboar]] during the qualifying round of the [[Vertress Conference]]. It lost in [[BW104|the next episode]].

In ''[[BW115|The Pokémon Harbor Patrol!]]'', a Watchog was a member of a rescue team managed by a harbor patrolman named [[Halsey]]. While originally used only for crowd control and as a lookout, Watchog, with the help of {{an|N}}, proved itself to be a capable and valuable asset to its team.

In ''[[BW125|Cilan and the Case of the Purrloin Witness!]]'', [[Mrs. Ripple]] used a Watchog as a guard to watch over her jewels.

==Trivia==
* In {{game|Black and White|s}}, Watchog was the only Pokémon to have the same {{EV}} yield as its {{p|Patrat|pre-Evolution}}, and the only evolved Pokémon to only give a single EV, giving one EV in Attack.
** Because its EV yield was 2 Attack in {{B2W2}}, Watchog is the only Pokémon to have its EV yield changed within a [[generation]].
* Watchog is the only evolved Pokémon with a [[catch rate]] of 255.
* Despite its [[Pokédex entry|Pokédex entries]] from Black, Y, and Alpha Sapphire stating Watchog spits the seeds of berries stored in their cheek pouches, Watchog couldn't learn {{m|Bullet Seed}} until {{g|Ultra Sun and Ultra Moon}}.
* As of {{g|Scarlet and Violet}}, both of Watchog's abilites have the same effect. However, Watchog is unavailable in these games.

===Origin===
Watchog's name suggests that it is based on a {{wp|groundhog}}, although, like its pre-Evolution, it also resembles a {{wp|chipmunk}}, {{wp|meerkat}} or {{wp|prairie dog}}. Its design is possibly based on a road worker, with the body resembling a {{wp|personal protective equipment|protective suit}} and the yellow stripes forming a {{wp|High-visibility clothing|safety vest}}. Its penchant for moves that interrupt the standard flow of battle could allude to {{wp|traffic guard}}s and their ability to override {{wp|Traffic#Rules_of_the_road|the rules of the road}}.

====Name origin====
Watchog may be a combination of ''watch'' and ''groundhog'', as well as a play on {{wp|guard dog|watchdog}}, or "watch out".

Miruhog may be a combination of 見る ''miru'' (to observe) and ''groundhog''.
