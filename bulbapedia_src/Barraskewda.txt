'''Barraskewda''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''カマスジョー''' ''Kamasujaw'') is a {{type|Water}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation VIII]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Arrokuda}} starting at [[level]] 26.

==Biology==
Barraskewda is a fishlike {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} with a long body and tapered head. Its head is colored brown and has small eyes with beady black pupils and no visible irises. Its mouth has a noticeable under-bite, and two teeth on each side of its lower jaw can be seen when Barraskewda has its mouth closed. At each corner of its mouth are two dark spiral markings. Barraskewda's head has four protrusions extending backward from its sides, with a spike extending from the upper and lower part of each side. Its main body is tan in color, and across each side of its midsection it has a white line running down most of its length, with other white markings branching off, resembling fish-bones. A similar branching brown marking can be found running down the length of Barraskewda's dorsal side. It has two pairs of ventral fins and one dorsal fin located near the back of its body, along with four tail-fins that can rotate like a propeller, allowing Barraskewda to swim at high speeds. All of Barraskewda's fins are colored red. Its tail fins are attached to a white hub that connects to the brown rear section of its body, and the spines in its other fins are brown as well. 

Barraskewda's jaws are said to be as sharp as a spear and strong like steel. It propels itself up to speeds of 100 knots (185 kmph; 115 mph) by spinning its tail, and uses that speed to ram into its prey, spearing it. It is able to leap out of the water and catch {{p|Wingull}} flying near the surface with its jaws. It is also known to compete with {{p|Finizen}} for food. The flesh of Barraskewda is surprisingly tasty.

Barraskewda and its pre-Evolution, {{p|Arrokuda}}, are the [[Signature Ability|only known Pokémon]] that can have {{a|Propeller Tail}} as an [[Ability]].

==Trivia==
* Barraskewda is tied with {{p|Iron Bundle}} for the highest base {{stat|Speed}} stat of all {{type|Water}} Pokémon.

===Origin===
Barraskewda seems to be based on a {{wp|barracuda}} and on fish of the ''{{wp|Esox}}'' genus, such as the {{wp|northern pike}}. It also resembles other {{wp|predatory fish}} such as the {{wp|gar|garpike}}, the {{wp|houndfish}}, and some {{wp|sturgeon}} species.

Barraskewda also appears be based on {{wp|spear}}s, drawing some inspiration from {{wp|spearfishing}}; or a pun involving {{wp|Pike (weapon)|pikes}}. In addition, it shares traits with {{wp|torpedo}}es, such as its spinning propeller-like tail. The barracuda and torpedo elements together could be a reference to the {{wp|Fairey Barracuda}}, a British {{wp|bomber|bomber aircraft}}, and its torpedoes; or the German cone-headed torpedo known as {{wp|Superkavitierender Unterwasserlaufkörper|Barracuda}}.

Both Arrokuda and Barraskewda's colors and overall design resemble an [https://canterburymuseums.co.uk/case-contents/the-anglo-saxon-kingdom-of-kent/#:~:text=Explorers%20%26%20Collectors%20Collection-,Fish%20Brooch,5th%20–%208th%20Century%20AD,-Copper%20Alloy%20and Anglo-Saxon brooch] fashioned and shaped like a fish. 

====Name origin====
Barraskewda may be a combination of ''barracuda'' and ''skewer''.

Kamasujaw may be a combination of 梭子魚 ''kamasu'' (barracuda) and ''jaw''.
