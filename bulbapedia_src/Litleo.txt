'''Litleo''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''シシコ''' ''Shishiko'') is a dual-type {{2t|Fire|Normal}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation VI]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] into {{p|Pyroar}} starting at [[level]] 35.

==Biology==
Litleo is a quadrupedal, leonine {{OBP|Pokémon|species}}. It is mostly dark brown with pale brown paws, ears, tail tip, and face. It has a short, rounded muzzle with a reddish-orange nose, round ears with darker interiors,  and circular, black eyes with white pupils. There is a reddish-orange tuft of mane between its ears. Each of its paws has three toes and dark brown paw pads. The tail ends in a pointed, teardrop shape.

Litleo is a hot-blooded Pokémon that is curious by nature and is quick to start fights. In battle, its short mane radiates heat. The stronger its opponent, the more heat it produces. Litleo live in prides, where they are raised and taught to hunt by female {{p|Pyroar}}. When a wild Litleo is capable of hunting on its own, it is kicked out of the pride and begins to live alone. It lives in grassy plains and along cliff sides.

Prior to [[Generation VII]], Litleo and its evolved form, Pyroar, were the [[signature move|only known Pokémon]] capable of learning the move {{m|Noble Roar}}.

===Evolution===
Litleo evolves into {{p|Pyroar}}.

==In animation==

===Main series===
====Major appearances====
Litleo debuted in ''[[PK26|Pikachu, What's This Key?]]''. It was a resident of a world full of ice and snow, which was accessed by {{AP|Pikachu}} and his friends, courtesy of {{p|Klefki}}'s keys.

Four Litleo appeared in ''[[XY097|A Fiery Rite of Passage!]]''. One of them was forced to leave the pride by {{p|Pyroar}}, but with {{Ash}}'s help, it was able to become independent and leave its pride.

====Minor appearances====
Litleo made its animated series debut in ''[[XY080|Performing with Fiery Charm!]]'', under the ownership of a {{pkmn|Performer}} competing in the {{to|Dendemille}} {{pkmn|Showcase}}. It reappeared in ''[[XY091|A Performance Pop Quiz!]]'', where it participated in the {{ci|Anistar}} Showcase; and in ''[[XY109|Master Class Choices!]]'', where it participated in the {{ci|Fleurrh}} Showcase. It reappeared once more in ''[[XY112|Master Class is in Session!]]'', where it was used in the Master Class Showcase in [[Gloire City]].

Multiple Litleo appeared in ''[[M19|Volcanion and the Mechanical Marvel]]'', including one during a flashback. Another was seen as a resident of the [[Nebel Plateau]].

Two Litleo appeared during a flashback in ''[[XYS05|The Legend of X, Y, and Z!]]''.

Two {{pkmn|Trainer}}s' Litleo appeared in ''[[SM034|A Crowning Moment of Truth!]]''. They were among the Pokémon attending the [[Wela Fire Festival]].

A Litleo appeared in ''[[JN098|Out of Their Elements!]]'', under the ownership of {{OBP|Harmony|JN098}}.

==Trivia==
* Litleo and {{p|Pyroar}} have several unique distinctions:
** They are the only Pokémon with {{a|Moxie}} to favor {{stat|Special Attack}} over physical {{stat|Attack}}, despite the former being its highest stat.
** No other {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} has the same [[type]] [[List of Pokémon with unique type combinations|combination]] as them.
** No other Pokémon has the same [[List of Pokémon by gender ratio|gender ratio]] — [[:Category:Pokémon with a gender ratio of one male to seven females|one male to seven females]] — as them.
* Litleo and Pyroar are the first Pokémon to be classed with the {{type|Normal}} as their secondary type (not counting [[regional form]]s of Pokémon from prior [[generation]]s).
* Litleo is the [[List of Pokémon with unique base stat totals|only Pokémon]] with a base stat total of 369.
* The [[Litleonids|Litleonid meteor shower]] in [[Pokémon Omega Ruby and Alpha Sapphire]] is named after this Pokémon, a reference to the {{wp|Leonids|Leonid meteor shower}}.

===Origin===
Litleo is based on a {{wp|lion}} cub. Its red hair looks similar to a {{wp|mohawk hairstyle}}.

====Name origin====
Litleo may be a combination of ''little'', ''lit'', and ''[[wiktionary:leo|leo]]'' (Latin for lion).

Shishiko may be a combination of 獅子 ''shishi'' (lion) and 子 ''ko'' (child).
