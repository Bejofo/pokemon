'''Kingdra''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''キングドラ''' ''Kingdra'') is a dual-type {{2t|Water|Dragon}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation II]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Seadra}} when [[Trade Evolution|traded]] while holding a [[Dragon Scale]]. It is the final form of {{p|Horsea}}.

(Specifics may differ in past games. Refer to [[#Evolution data|Game data&rarr;Evolution data]] for these details.)

==Biology==
Kingdra is a large, {{wp|fish|piscine}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} that is similar to a {{wp|seahorse}} with a tightly curled tail. It is covered in blue scales except for its yellow, compact belly scales. There are thin spines with a single branch atop its head and thin, white fins under its cheeks. Its thin snout is powerful, allowing squirts of water jets with devastating power to be fired. Two-pronged fins extend slightly past its cheeks. On its back is a white fin supported by thin, blue spines similar to the ones on its head.

The yawn of a sleeping Kingdra is powerful enough to create undercurrents capable of destroying small ships. Should it decide to emerge from the depths of the ocean, Kingdra's strength can create large whirlpools and waterspouts on the surface. Because of its immense power, Kingdra often hibernates deep in uninhabited areas of the {{DL|List of Pokémon by habitat|Sea Pokémon|sea}} to build up its energy, usually at the bottom of the seas. When a storm arrives, Kingdra is said to awaken and wander about in search of prey. Kingdra's awakening can cause tornadoes. It is known to have fierce battles with {{p|Dragonite}} whenever they meet. Kingdra's gleaming scales are gifted to royals.

===Evolution===
Kingdra evolves from {{p|Seadra}} and is the final form of {{p|Horsea}}.

==In animation==
===Main series===

====Major appearances====
Kingdra made its debut in ''[[M03|Spell of the Unown: Entei]]'', where it was one of the illusory Pokémon [[Molly Hale]] created in her dream world through the {{OBP|Unown|M03}}. It was Molly's first Pokémon during her {{pkmn|battle}} against {{an|Misty}}, where it quickly defeated {{TP|Misty|Goldeen}}.

Kingdra made its animated series debut in ''[[EP216|Dueling Heroes]]'', under the ownership of {{OBP|Christopher|EP216}}, who used it to compete in the [[Whirl Cup]]. It lost to {{AP|Totodile}} in the first match after he plugged up Kingdra's snout while it used {{m|Hydro Pump}}, causing excess water to build up inside of it, thus sending it flying across the stadium.

In ''[[EP252|Fangs for Nothin']]'', [[Clair]] used a Kingdra in her [[Gym]] {{pkmn|battle}} against {{Ash}}. It faced off against {{AP|Pikachu}} and {{AP|Noctowl}}, defeating the latter. In ''[[EP254|Better Eight Than Never]]'', Kingdra was later used in the [[rematch]], going up against {{AP|Snorlax}} and eventually being defeated.

In ''[[JN088|Looking Out for Number Two!]]'', [[Goh]] caught a {{DL|List of Goh's Pokémon|Kingdra}} as part of his second [[Project Mew]] [[Field Research|Trial Mission]].

====Minor appearances====
A Kingdra appeared in ''[[PK09|Snorlax Snowman]]''.

In ''[[The Legend of Thunder!]]'', a Kingdra was at a [[Pokémon Center]].

In ''[[M09|Pokémon Ranger and the Temple of the Sea]]'', a {{OBP|Manaphy|M09}} {{pkmn|Egg}} bounced off a Kingdra's head while adrift in the ocean.

Multiple Kingdra appeared in ''[[XY001|Kalos, Where Dreams and Adventures Begin!]]''.

A Kingdra appeared in ''[[XY024|An Undersea Place to Call Home!]]''.

==Trivia==
* Kingdra's name is often mistranslated to "Kingora" in the [[Chuang Yi]] translation of [[Pokémon Adventures]].
* Kingdra is the only {{type|Dragon}} Pokémon introduced in [[Generation II]].
* Kingdra and its pre-evolved forms share their {{pkmn|category}} with {{p|Dratini}}, its evolved forms, {{p|Salamence}}, and Kalosian {{p|Goodra}}. They are all known as the Dragon Pokémon.

===Origin===
Kingdra is inspired on a {{wp|seahorse}}, possibly the {{wp|crowned seahorse}}, with some seadragon (genera ''{{wp|Phyllopteryx}}'' and ''{{wp|leafy seadragon|Phycodurus}}'') elements. Its {{t|Dragon}} type and {{pkmn|category}} may be a reference to the Japanese word for seahorse, 竜の落とし子 ''tatsu-no-otoshigo'' (literally "illegitimate child of a dragon"), which is derived from the myth that a seahorse that lives for a century will transform into a {{wp|dragon}}, as well as the aforementioned seadragons.

The fact that Kingdra dwells on the seafloor while creating {{wp|whirlpool}}s may be a reference to {{wp|Ryūjin}}, a Japanese sea deity; or {{wp|Charybdis}}, a sea monster in Greek mythology. Its ability to shoot {{wp|cephalopod ink|ink}} is reminiscent of a {{wp|squid}}, and the shape of its snout and {{a|Sniper}} Ability are reminiscent of a {{wp|water gun}}.

====Name origin====
Kingdra may be a combination of ''king'' and ''dragon''. It may also involve ''{{wp|Lernaean Hydra|Hydra}}'' (a mythological water creature).
