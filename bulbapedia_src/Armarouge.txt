'''Armarouge''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''グレンアルマ''' ''Gurenarma'') is a dual-type {{2t|Fire|Psychic}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation IX]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Charcadet}} when exposed to a set of [[Auspicious Armor]]. It is one of Charcadet's final forms, the other being {{p|Ceruledge}}.

==Biology==
[[File:Armarouge cannon.png|thumb|left|250px|Armarouge using its pauldrons as a cannon]]
Armarouge is a bipedal, humanoid Pokémon. Its body is primarily black, with red colorations on its cylindrical legs and hands, the latter resembling oven mitts. Its head is also red, with yellow sharp "ears" protruding from either side. The top of its head is yellow and has a fire burning on top, resembling the plume on a knight's helmet. Its face is covered with a black mask with three holes at the center, and its eyes have red irises and wisps emanating from its yellow pupils. Armarouge's torso and shoulders are covered in an [[Auspicious Armor|old set of yellow and red armor]], along with levitating large yellow {{wp|pauldron}}s over its shoulders. 

Armarouge's armor is said to have belonged to a distinguished warrior, and is the source of its psychic power. The armor allows Armarouge to control its inner fire energy and use attacks. Armarouge is loyal and believes in fighting fair, willing to fight head-on no matter how strong the foe is. It first focuses on using its sturdy armor for defense before using high firepower in order to take down opponents. When attacking, Armarouge moves its pauldrons to its hands and puts them together to form a cannon, which it uses to shoot {{m|Armor Cannon|blazing projectiles}}.<ref name=SVsite>[https://scarletviolet.pokemon.com/en-us/pokemon/armarouge/ Armarouge — Pokémon Scarlet and Pokémon Violet Official Website]</ref>

Armarouge is the [[Signature move|only known Pokémon]] capable of learning the move {{m|Armor Cannon}}.

===Evolution===
Armarouge evolves from {{p|Charcadet}} and is one of its final forms, the other being {{p|Ceruledge}}.

===Origin===
Armarouge may be based on a possessed suit of armor. Its design could also be based on medieval heroic tales of European knights, as well as the idea of {{wp|chivalry}}. One specific point of origin may be the legend of the Spanish knight {{wp|Wilfred the Hairy|Guifré el Pilós}}, known for his golden armor and shield, on which was said the emperor {{wp|Charles the Bald}} painted with his fingers four red lines using the blood of the former, in honor of his bravery. This might explain the red colorations on Armarouge's armor and extremities.

Armarouge's ability to put its pauldrons together on its arms to form a cannon is reminiscent of depictions of arm cannons in fictional media. The shape the pauldrons take when in this form also resembles a {{wp|gas cylinder}}, a metal vessel that contains pressurized gases for uses such as combustion.

The [[Auspicious Armor]] required to evolve {{p|Charcadet}} into Armarouge may be inspired by ceremonial cannons cast from captured weapons and scrap metals, or cannons kept as {{wp|war trophies}} in early-modern warfare.

====Name origin====
Armarouge may be a combination of ''arma'' (Spanish, Portuguese, and Italian for weapon), ''armadura'' (Spanish and Portuguese for armor) or ''armatura'' (Italian for armor), and ''rouge'' (French for red).

Gurenarma may be a combination of 紅蓮 ''guren'' (crimson) and ''arma'', ''armadura'', or ''armatura''.
