'''Gourgeist''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''パンプジン''' ''Pumpjin'') is a dual-type {{2t|Ghost|Grass}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation VI]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Pumpkaboo}} when [[Trade Evolution|traded]].

Gourgeist has four different [[#Form data|forms]]: '''Small Size''', '''Average Size''', '''Large Size''', and '''Super Size'''.

==Biology==
Gourgeist is a ghostly gourd-like {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} that varies dramatically in size. Its body is a dark brown pumpkin with a glowing, yellow-orange face carved into the front and a pair of nubby legs. The face consists of two triangular eyes and a grinning mouth; on top of the mouth is a small, triangular cutout for a nose. Poking out of the top of the pumpkin is its slender brown-orange head, which has bright yellow eyes and a small mouth but no visible nose. Gourgeist has long, light pink hair with arm-like tendrils framing its face and bangs that usually cover one eye. On top of its head is a crescent-shaped tuft that is brown on top; this tuft resembles a pumpkin's stem. In the {{pkmn|animated series}}, Gourgeist has shown the ability to retract its upper body into the pumpkin, giving it the appearance of a traditional {{wp|jack-o'-lantern}}.

On the night of a new moon, Gourgeist wanders town streets while singing an eerie song. Those who hear its song will be cursed. It is also known to sing joyfully while observing prey it has entangled in its arms.

Gourgeist and its pre-evolved form {{p|Pumpkaboo}} are the [[Signature move|only known Pokémon]] capable of learning the move {{m|Trick-or-Treat}}.

===Forms===
Gourgeist has four separate forms: Small Size, Average Size, Large Size, and Super Size. 

Gourgeist's four different sizes determines their target for who they take into the afterlife. Small Gourgeist prefers targeting adults by pretending to be children. The large Gourgeist prefers taking children by pretending to be adults. The Supersize Gourgeist aren't picky and will target anyone.

===Form data===
Gourgeist have four different forms, each named after their respective size: Small Size, Average Size, Large Size, and Super Size. 

Its size is maintained through [[Evolution]] from {{p|Pumpkaboo}} and cannot be changed. Gourgeist typically cannot be found in the {{pkmn2|wild}}, and its different forms can usually only be obtained by evolving Pumpkaboo, whose larger sizes are rarer to encounter. However, in {{g|Sword and Shield}}, Average Size Gourgeist can be found in the wild, and Gourgeist can also be encountered in [[Dynamax Adventure]]s.<!--what sizes appear in Dynamax Adventures?-->

Super Size Gourgeist have different, lower-pitched [[Cry|cries]] than the other forms, and were also the only Pokémon to have the XXL [[Size and weight variation#Pokémon GO|size designation]] in {{g|GO}} prior to the addition of XXL and XXS sizes for all species. The different sizes of Gourgeist do not use different menu sprites, except in {{g|HOME}}.

Previously, only Average Size and Super Size Gourgeist could have their [[Hidden Ability]] {{a|Insomnia}}. Average Size can be obtained with its Hidden Ability from the [[Friend Safari]], and Super Size could be obtained with its Hidden Ability from {{pkmn2|event}}s. Since Version 1.2.0 of {{g|Sword and Shield}}, all sizes can be obtained with their Hidden Abilities via [[Ability Patch]]es.

Each form has different [[base stats]]. With each increase in size, its base {{stat|HP}} stat increases by 10 points and its base {{stat|Attack}} stat increases by 5 points while its base {{stat|Speed}} stat decreases by 15 points. Consequently, its base stat total does not vary with size.

==In animation==

===Major appearances===
====[[Jessie's Gourgeist]]====
[[Jessie]]'s Super Size Pumpkaboo evolved into a Gourgeist in ''[[XY082|A Festival Trade! A Festival Farewell?]]''. When Jessie headed to [[Alola]] in ''[[SM003|Loading the Dex!]]'', it was revealed that Gourgeist had been left behind at {{an|Team Rocket HQ}}. Gourgeist returned to Jessie's party in ''[[JN145|Rocket Revengers!]]''.

====Other====
Three Gourgeist appeared in ''[[XY082|A Festival Trade! A Festival Farewell?]]'', under the ownership of [[Count Pumpka's steward]]. They were commonly used by their {{pkmn|Trainer}} to carry out [[Count Pumpka]]'s bidding. All three were eventually defeated by [[Ash's Pikachu]], [[Clemont's Bunnelby]], and [[Serena's Braixen]].

A Gourgeist belonging to [[Diantha]] appeared in ''[[JN116|Pride of a Champion!]]''. She used it in her [[Masters Eight Tournament]] battle against {{an|Lance}}, where it defeated {{TP|Lance|Gyarados}}, before losing to {{p|Hydreigon}}.

===Minor appearances===
Five Gourgeist appeared in ''[[SM094|A Haunted House for Everyone!]]'' as illusions created by a {{p|Mismagius}}.

A Trainer's Gourgeist appeared in [[HZ112]].

==Trivia==
===Origin===
Gourgeist appears to be based on {{wp|Calabash|bottle gourd}}s and {{wp|pumpkin}}s, specifically the ones carved into {{wp|jack-o'-lantern}}s for {{wp|Halloween}}. Gourgeist's behavior of knocking on people's front doors before whisking them away may reference {{wp|trick-or-treating}}. Its upper body may be based on the {{wp|candle}}s within jack-o'-lanterns themselves, with the curl resembling its wick and its hair-like extensions its dripping wax. Its ability to retract into and pop out of its pumpkin-like body may also be a reference to a {{wp|jack-in-the-box}}.

Gourgeist's appearance may be inspired by depictions of {{wp|Baba Yaga}} inside her flying mortar. Alternatively, it could have potential connections to the Japanese ghost story ''{{wp|Yotsuya Kaidan}}'', in which a woman transforms into a paper lantern {{wp|yōkai}} with long hair.

Gourgeist's different forms are likely inspired by the large variety of sizes in pumpkins, the largest ones possibly being a reference to the agricultural tradition of growing {{wp|giant pumpkin|incredibly large pumpkins}}.

====Name origin====
Gourgeist may be a combination of ''{{wp|gourd}}'' and ''{{wp|geist}}''.

Pumpjin may be a combination of ''pumpkin'' and 神 ''{{wp|Shen (Chinese religion)|shin}}'' (deity, spirit), 人 ''jin'' (man), or ''{{wp|jinn}}''.
