'''Mudsdale''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''バンバドロ''' ''Banbadoro'') is a {{type|Ground}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation VII]].

It [[Evolution|evolves]] from {{p|Mudbray}} starting at [[level]] 30.

==Biology==
[[File:Mudsdale Gallop VII OD.png|200px|thumb|left|Mudsdale Gallop]]
Mudsdale is a large, equine {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} with short, brown fur and a black-and-red mane and tail. It has heavy-lidded, black eyes with a red rim along the bottom, a red blaze on its muzzle, and long, pointed black ears with brown insides. This Pokémon's mane extends down around its eyes. Along the top of its neck, its mane is black with red at the tip, and stands up straight. On either side of its neck, the mane forms three thick structures similar to {{wp|dreadlocks}} with red mud around the tips. The mane extends all the way down Mudsdale's back in a thin line before becoming its tail. The tail appears similar to its dreadlocks but is much larger and has several black notches missing from the mud around the tip. Around its hooves is a protective coating of red mud, which has three notches around the bottom.

Mudsdale is known for both its powerful body and emotional fortitude, which prevents it from becoming agitated. Regardless of the trouble it is in, it will never cry out and will defeat opponents with a single blow.<ref>[http://www.pokemon-sunmoon.com/en-us/pokemon/mudsdale/ Pokémon Sun and Moon site | Mudsdale]</ref> The mud around its legs gives its kicks extra force; one kick can turn a car or large truck into a scrap pile. When galloping, its hooves can dig holes in asphalt. While it is not swift, it has excellent stamina and can continue on for three days and three nights while dragging over 10 tons. It will mix dirt and saliva to create a special mud with resistance to both wind and water. The mud is also harder than even stone. Because of its unique qualities, the walls of some old houses were coated with Mudsdale's mud. Mudsdale is highly treasured for both physical labor and pottery made from its high-quality mud. In [[Alola]], Mudsdale is used as a [[Poké Ride|Ride Pokémon]] called Mudsdale Gallop, to allow natives to travel across rocky terrains.

Prior to [[Generation IX]], Mudsdale and its pre-evolved form, {{p|Mudbray}}, were the [[Signature Ability|only known Pokémon]] that could have {{a|Stamina}} as an [[Ability]].

====Major appearances====
=====[[Hapu's Mudsdale]]=====
A Mudsdale debuted in ''[[SM104|That's Some Spicy Island Research!]]'', under the ownership of [[Hapu]]. It helps her in her radish field by carrying the grown radishes in its bags. Mudsdale was later used to battle {{tc|Team Skull Grunt}}s [[Tupp]], [[Zipp]], and [[Rapp]].

=====Other=====
Mudsdale debuted in ''[[SM075|Some Kind of Laziness!]]'' as a [[Poké Ride|Ride Pokémon]] used by [[Acerola]] and {{Ash}}. It helped them cross a sandstorm in the middle of the [[Haina Desert]] and reach the [[Ruins of Abundance]], where they hoped to find {{DL|Guardian deities (anime)|Tapu Bulu}}. It was used by Acerola to go to the Ruins of Abundance again in ''[[SM077|Guiding an Awakening!]]''.

A Mudsdale appeared in ''[[HZ035|The Wild Pair, Friede and Cap!]]'', under the ownership of one of the {{OBP|Pokémon hunters|HZ035}}.

====Minor appearances====
A Mudsdale appeared in ''[[SM083|Dummy, You Shrunk the Kids!]]''. It walked across a river, unknowingly causing a tidal wave that splashed a shrunken {{Ash}}, {{an|Lillie}}, and {{an|Sophocles}}.

A Mudsdale appeared in ''[[JN050|A Pinch of This, a Pinch of That!]]'', under the ownership of [[Bray Zenn]]. It accidentally sent a large rock flying towards Ash, [[Goh]], and [[Chloe]], but it was destroyed by {{AP|Pikachu}}'s {{m|Iron Tail}}.

Three Trainers' Mudsdale appeared in ''[[HZ001|The Pendant That Starts It All (Part One)]]''.

==Trivia==
* Mudsdale is the [[List of Pokémon by weight|heaviest]] of all Pokémon excluding [[Legendary Pokémon]], [[Ultra Beast]]s, and [[Mega Evolution]]s.
* While Mudsdale has a 5% chance of holding the [[Light Clay]], it cannot learn {{m|Light Screen}}, {{m|Reflect}}, or {{m|Aurora Veil}}.

===Origin===
Mudsdale resembles {{wp|draft horse}}s such as the {{wp|Clydesdale horse|Clydesdale}}, known for the {{wp|Feathering (horse)|feathering}} on their legs. Its hair appears to be based on {{wp|dreadlocks}} hairstyle.

====Name origin====
Mudsdale may be a combination of ''mud'' and ''{{wp|Clydesdale horse|Clydesdale}}''.

Banbadoro may be a combination of 輓馬 ''banba'' (draft horse) and 泥 ''doro'' (mud).
