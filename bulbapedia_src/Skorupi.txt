'''Skorupi''' ([[List of Japanese Pokémon names|Japanese]]: '''スコルピ''' ''Scorupi'') is a dual-type {{2t|Poison|Bug}} {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} introduced in [[Generation IV]].

It [[Evolution|evolve]]s into {{p|Drapion}} starting at [[level]] 40.

==Biology==
Skorupi is a {{OBP|Pokémon|species}} which resembles an oversized, pale purple scorpion. Its claws and tail have been swapped, giving it two stingers and a claw tail. The miniature jaws, claws, and tail claws are white. Its arms are attached directly to its head, close to its teal eyes. A pointed marking joins its eyes. The head, arms, two tail segments, and all four legs are light blue, while the body, tail end, and other two tail segments are a darker shade of blue.

It buries itself in sand and lies in wait for unsuspecting prey, gripping it with its tail claws and injecting poison; it will then hold on until the poison takes effect. However, it is capable of surviving for a year without nourishment. Skorupi is related to {{p|Sizzlipede}} through a common ancestor. Skorupi is found in deserts and dry areas like [[Reversal Mountain]], though it can be found in marshy areas such as the [[Great Marsh]]. Skorupi is considered a highly dangerous Pokémon due its lethal claws and poison. However, Skorupi cannot stand cold temperatures, becoming weaker when exposed.

===Evolution===
Skorupi evolves into {{p|Drapion}}.

===Major appearances===
Skorupi debuted in ''[[DP099|A Trainer and Child Reunion!]]'', under the ownership of [[Aaron]]. It was commonly seen [[Walking Pokémon|outside of its Poké Ball]].

Multiple Skorupi appeared in ''[[XY094|From A to Z!]]'', ''[[XY102|Meeting at Terminus Cave!]]'', ''[[XY107|An Explosive Operation!]]'', and ''[[XY132|A Towering Takeover!]]'', all under the ownership of {{tc|Team Flare Grunt}}s.

===Minor appearances===
In ''[[DP114|Another One Gabites the Dust!]]'', a {{pkmn|Coordinator}}'s Skorupi competed in the [[Appeal|Performance Stage]] of the {{to|Chocovine}} {{pkmn|Contest}}.

In ''[[DP125|Battling a Cute Drama!]]'', a Skorupi was at [[Shelter Town]]'s [[Pokémon Center]].

In ''[[DP140|Historical Mystery Tour!]]'', {{Ash}} encountered a Skorupi in [[Xatu's Forest]].

In ''[[JN139|Must Be Our Heroes and the Witch!]]'', a Skorupi appeared in a fantasy.

A {{pkmn|Trainer}}'s Skorupi appeared in ''[[JN144|Getting to the Heart of it All!]]''.

A Skorupi appeared in ''[[HZ035|The Wild Pair, Friede and Cap!]]''.

==Trivia==
* Skorupi is the only dual-type {{2t|Bug|Poison}} Pokémon to have Poison as its primary [[type]] instead of its secondary type.
** Skorupi is also the only Pokémon to have the Bug type changed upon [[Evolution]].
* Skorupi and Drapion are the only [[Generation IV]] {{DL|List of Pokémon by shape|Pokémon with an insectoid body}}.
** Skorupi and Drapion are also the only {{cat|Generation IV Pokémon}} in the {{egg|Water 3}} [[Egg Group]].

===Origin===
Skorupi appears to be based on a {{wp|scorpion}}. It has some characteristics that resemble a {{wp|Thelyphonida|vinegarroon}}, a similar arachnid. Its {{egg|Water 3}} Egg Group designation may be a reference to the ancient group of arthropods known as {{wp|Eurypterid|sea scorpions}} that used to inhabit the oceans and may have been the ancestors of modern scorpions.

====Name origin====
Skorupi and Scorupi appear to be shortenings of ''{{wp|scorpion}}''.
